Chapter 62 – National Human Rights Commission (NHRC)

PHRA 1993 • Statutory Body • Paris Principles

PHRA 1993 — Legislative Basis
Foundation
Protection of Human Rights Act (PHRA) 1993 — established NHRC (statutory, NOT constitutional)
Operative: October 12, 1993; HQ: New Delhi
Background: Paris Principles 1991 (UN guidelines for national HR institutions)
NHRC has 'A' status accreditation from GANHRI (Global Alliance of NHRIs)
Human Rights = life, liberty, equality, dignity — under Constitution + international covenants
PHRA amended in 2006 and 2019
Composition (After 2019 Amendment)
Members
1 Chairperson (retired CJI) + 5 Members = 6 full members
Members: retired SC judges OR persons with HR experience; at least 1 woman (2019)
Deemed members: Chairs of NCSC + NCST + NCW + NCBC (participate on domain matters)
Appointed by President on recommendation of 6-member committee: PM + Speaker(LS) + Home Minister + Dy.Chair(RS) + LoP(LS) + LoP(RS)
Only retired (not sitting) judges can be appointed
Tenure & Removal
Service Conditions
Tenure: 3 years OR age 70 (whichever earlier) — no re-appointment (changed from 5 yrs by 2019 Amendment)
Removal: President removes after SC inquiry (proved misbehaviour OR incapacity)
Salaries charged to Consolidated Fund of India
Post-retirement bar: no further employment under Central or State Govt
Service conditions cannot be varied to disadvantage after appointment
Jurisdiction & Functions
What NHRC Can Do
Investigate human rights violations by State actors (Central + State Govts)
Suo motu action; act on petitions; court referrals
Complaint within 1 year of violation (condone-able)
Visit any institution under State Govt (jails, detention centres, etc.)
Recommend compensation, interim relief, initiation of proceedings, law amendments
Annual Report → Central Govt (Home Ministry) → Parliament
Approach SC or HC if recommendations ignored
Limitations
What NHRC Cannot Do
Armed Forces: no direct jurisdiction — forwards to Central Govt
Matters pending before other commissions: not taken up
Complaints older than 1 year (unless condoned)
Recommendations are ADVISORY only — NOT binding
Private individual violations (needs State actor involvement)
State Human Rights Commissions (SHRC)
SHRC
Established by State Govts under PHRA 1993
Chair: retired Chief Justice of HC; Members: retired HC judges
Appointed by Governor on committee: CM + Speaker(LA) + Home Minister + LoP(LA)
Tenure: 3 years or age 70 (same as NHRC); salaries from State CFI
Jurisdiction: State Govt violations within State; parallel to NHRC (not subordinate)
NHRC can transfer cases from/to SHRC
