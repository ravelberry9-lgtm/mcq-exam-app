NCSK Basics

Under Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment
Originally temporary (sunset clause); Parliament extends periodically
1+1+3 = 5 members (Chair+VC+3); all from SC communities; Central Govt appoints
3-year tenure; Central Govt removes (no SC inquiry)
NCSK Chair: NOT a deemed NHRC member
Manual Scavenging
Definition & Context
Manual scavenging = manually handling human excreta from dry latrines, railway tracks, open drains, sewers without gear
Imposed on SC communities (Valmiki/Bhangi/Mehtar sub-castes) — caste-based degrading practice
Violates: Art 17 (untouchability), Art 21 (dignity), Art 23 (forced labour)
Safai Karamchari Andolan 2014 (SC): Rehabilitation + Rs.10 lakh sewer death compensation
Prohibition Laws
PEMSR Act 2013
First Central law: Employment of Manual Scavengers Act 1993 (weak; replaced)
PEMSR (Prohibition of Employment as Manual Scavengers and Rehabilitation) Act 2013
Broad definition: dry latrines + railways + open drains + sewers/septic tanks without gear
Penalty: 1 yr/Rs.50K (first); 2 yrs/Rs.1L (subsequent) — employers/contractors liable
Rehabilitation: Rs.40K one-time cash + training + loan Rs.15L at 4% + scholarship + housing
Dry latrine conversion: immediate; Sewer cleaning without gear = prohibited
NCSK Functions
Key Functions
Monitors PEMSR 2013 implementation; investigates sewer deaths; recommends compensation
Civil Court powers; suo motu action; Annual Report → Central Govt → Parliament
Monitors SRMS (Self-Employment Scheme for Rehabilitation of Manual Scavengers)
Recommendations advisory only (not binding)
