Chapter 61 – National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC)

102nd Amendment 2018 • Article 338B • Article 342A

Historical Background
Backward Classes Commissions
Kaka Kalelkar Commission (1953) — 1st OBC Commission under Article 340; submitted 1955; recommendations mostly rejected
Mandal Commission (1978-80): Chairman B.P. Mandal; appointed by Morarji Desai; report submitted 1980
Mandal: Identified 3,743 backward castes (52% of population); recommended 27% OBC reservation in Central services
V.P. Singh Govt (1990): Implemented Mandal — 27% OBC reservation in Central services (Article 16(4))
Anti-Mandal protests 1990; Indra Sawhney case filed → 9-judge bench ruling 1992
Indra Sawhney Case 1992
9-Judge Constitution Bench Rulings
Upheld 27% OBC reservation (Article 16(4))
Creamy layer among OBCs must be EXCLUDED (currently: family income > ₹8 lakh/year)
Struck down Narasimha Rao's 10% EWS reservation — economic backwardness alone insufficient
Total reservations ordinarily cannot exceed 50% ceiling
Carry forward rule valid but 50% ceiling applies per year
SC directed Govt to set up permanent body for OBC list → NCBC Act 1993
SC/ST have NO creamy layer; OBCs have creamy layer
NCBC Act 1993 (Statutory Phase)
Statutory NCBC
Set up by Parliament under NCBC Act 1993 (statutory, not constitutional)
Composition: 1 Chairperson + 1 Vice Chairperson + 3 Members = 5; appointed by President; 3-year tenure
Function: Examine requests for inclusion/exclusion in Central OBC List; advice was BINDING on Central Govt
Civil Court powers for inquiry; dealt only with Central OBC List
Repealed after 102nd Amendment 2018 gave NCBC constitutional status
102nd Amendment 2018 — Constitutional Status
Key Articles Added
102nd Constitutional Amendment Act 2018: added Articles 338B (NCBC) and 342A (Central OBC List)
NCBC now at par with NCSC (Art 338) and NCST (Art 338A)
NCSC = Art 338; NCST = Art 338A; NCBC = Art 338B
All in Part XVI of Constitution (Special Provisions for certain classes)
Article 338B — NCBC (Constitutional)
Composition & Powers
1 Chairperson + 1 Vice-Chairperson + 3 Members = 5; appointed by President by warrant
Functions: investigate complaints, advise on development, monitor safeguards, participate in planning for OBC
Consult NCBC on all major policy matters affecting social and educational interests of OBCs
Civil Court powers: summon witnesses, require documents, receive affidavits
Annual Report to President → President lays before Parliament + forwards relevant portions to State Govts
Article 342A — Central OBC List
Central OBC List Process
Article 342A(1): President specifies backward classes (Central List) by public notification (consults Governor for States)
Article 342A(2): Parliament by law includes/excludes from Central List (not executive)
Mirrors Article 341 (SC list) and Article 342 (ST list) — same pattern
Central List (2,600+ communities) for Central services and Central educational institutions
State lists maintained separately for State services/institutions
105th Amendment 2021
Restoring State Power
Maratha reservation case 2021 (5-judge bench): SC struck down Maharashtra SEBC Act; States had lost OBC identification power under 102nd Amendment
105th Constitutional Amendment 2021: Restored States/UTs' power to identify SEBCs for State-level OBC lists
Amended Articles 338B and 342A to clarify: Central List under 342A; State Lists under restored State power
Existing State OBC lists remain valid (saving clause)
Two separate lists confirmed: Central OBC List + State OBC Lists
