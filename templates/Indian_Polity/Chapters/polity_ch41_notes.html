Chapter 41 — నీతి ఆయోగ్ / NITI Aayog

January 1, 2015 | Non-Constitutional | Think Tank | No Fund Allocation Powers

Section 1 — Establishment & Background
NITI Aayog: National Institution for Transforming India. Est. January 1, 2015 by Cabinet Resolution (NOT by Act of Parliament).
Replaced: Planning Commission (est. March 15, 1950). PM Modi abolished Planning Commission.
Nature: Non-constitutional, non-statutory body. Can be abolished by executive resolution.
Headquarters: Sansad Marg, New Delhi (same Yojana Bhavan).
Section 2 — Composition
Chairperson: Prime Minister (ex-officio).
Vice-Chairperson: Appointed by PM. First VC = Arvind Panagariya (2015-17).
CEO: Appointed by PM for fixed term. First CEO = Sindhushree Khullar.
Full-time Members: Domain experts appointed by PM (typically 4-5).
Ex-officio Members: Up to 4 Cabinet Ministers nominated by PM.
Governing Council: All State CMs + LGs/Administrators of UTs with legislature. Key federal body.
Section 3 — Functions & Key Features
Think Tank: Policy advisory, strategic direction, technical expertise — NO FUND ALLOCATION.
Cooperative Federalism: States as active partners; Governing Council = States' voice.
Vision Documents: Viksit Bharat@2047, 3-Year Action Agenda.
Monitoring: SDG India Index, Aspirational Districts Programme (112 districts).
Innovation: Atal Innovation Mission (AIM), Atal Tinkering Labs.
Section 4 — Planning Commission vs NITI Aayog
Planning Commission: 5-Year Plans; TOP-DOWN; allocated funds to States; criticized for undermining federalism.
NITI Aayog: No 5-Year Plans; no fund allocation; BOTTOM-UP; think tank only.
Key difference: Planning Commission = financial power; NITI Aayog = advisory only.
Both: PM as Chairperson; Cabinet Resolution; non-constitutional bodies.
Finance Commission (Art.280): Constitutional; devolves tax revenue — unlike non-constitutional NITI Aayog.
12th Five-Year Plan (2012-17): Last Plan by Planning Commission.
Section 5 — Key Reports & Initiatives
SDG India Index: Annual ranking of States/UTs on 17 SDGs. Kerala = top performer consistently.
Aspirational Districts Programme: 112 most underdeveloped districts — monthly monitoring.
State Health Index: Annual ranking on health outcomes.
India Innovation Index: Ranks States on innovation ecosystem.
Viksit Bharat@2047: India as developed nation by centenary of independence.
