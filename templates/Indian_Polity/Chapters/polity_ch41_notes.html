Chapter 41 — నీతి ఆయోగ్ / NITI Aayog

January 1, 2015 | Non-Constitutional | Think Tank | No Fund Allocation Powers

Section 1 — Establishment & Background
NITI Aayog: National Institution for Transforming India. Established January 1, 2015 by Cabinet Resolution (executive action — NOT by Act of Parliament).
Replaced: Planning Commission (est. March 15, 1950 by Resolution). PM Modi abolished Planning Commission, replaced with NITI Aayog.
Nature: Non-constitutional, non-statutory body. Created by executive resolution. Can be abolished by executive resolution without Parliament/constitutional amendment.
Expansion: NITI = National Institution for Transforming India. Aayog = Commission/Institution (Hindi).
Headquarters: Sansad Marg, New Delhi (same Yojana Bhavan as Planning Commission).
Section 2 — Composition
Chairperson: Prime Minister of India (ex-officio). PM = de facto head (just like Planning Commission).
Vice-Chairperson: Appointed by PM. Works full-time. Effectively = head of day-to-day operations. First VC = Arvind Panagariya (2015-2017).
CEO: Chief Executive Officer — appointed by PM for fixed term by warrant. First CEO = Sindhushree Khullar. Manages NITI Aayog's day-to-day administrative affairs.
Full-time Members: Experts appointed by PM. Typically 4-5 members. Domain specialists (economists, social scientists, technologists).
Part-time Members: Leading universities/research institutions' representatives. Rotate.
Ex-officio Members: Up to 4 Cabinet Ministers nominated by PM.
Special Invitees: Ministers with relevant expertise nominated by PM.
Governing Council: Chief Ministers of all States + LGs/Administrators of UTs (with legislature). Key federal body.
Section 3 — Functions & Role
Think Tank: Policy think tank of the Government of India — provides strategic direction, policy advice, technical expertise.
NO FUND ALLOCATION: NITI Aayog CANNOT allocate funds. Finance Ministry/Planning Commission used to allocate. NITI Aayog has NO financial role in fund distribution.
Cooperative Federalism: Promotes cooperative federalism — brings States as active partners in national development. Governing Council = States' voice.
Vision/Strategy Documents: Prepares long-term vision documents (Vision 2030, 2047), medium-term strategies (3-Year Action Agenda), sector-specific reports.
Monitoring & Evaluation: Monitors implementation of government programs. Publishes progress reports (Aspirational Districts Programme, SDG India Index).
Innovation: Promotes innovation and entrepreneurship. Atal Innovation Mission (AIM), Atal Tinkering Labs.
SDGs: Coordinates and monitors India's progress on Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Publishes SDG India Index annually.
Section 4 — Planning Commission vs NITI Aayog
Planning Commission (1950-2014): Created 1950; 5-Year Plans; TOP-DOWN approach; allocated funds to States/Ministries; States had to go to Centre; criticized as undermining federalism.
NITI Aayog (2015-): No 5-Year Plans; no fund allocation; BOTTOM-UP approach; States as partners; cooperative federalism; think tank role.
Key Difference: Planning Commission = financial power (grant allocations); NITI Aayog = advisory only, no financial power.
Both: PM as Chairperson; Cabinet Resolution (not statute); Yojana Bhavan/Sansad Marg; non-constitutional bodies.
Finance Commission difference: Finance Commission (Art.280) = constitutional; NITI Aayog = non-constitutional. FC = devolves tax revenue; NITI = no fund powers.
Section 5 — Plans vs Strategy Documents
12th Five-Year Plan (2012-17): Last Five-Year Plan by Planning Commission. NITI Aayog did NOT continue 5-Year Plans.
Three-Year Action Agenda (2017-20): First strategic document by NITI Aayog replacing 12th Plan's remaining period.
National Development Agenda: NITI Aayog outlines sector-specific priorities without binding financial allocations.
Vision@2047: India@100 — NITI Aayog prepared Viksit Bharat Strategy to make India a developed nation by 2047 (centenary of independence).
SDG India Index: Annual ranking of States/UTs on 17 SDGs. Shows progress on health, education, gender, environment etc.
Section 6 — Governing Council & Committees
Governing Council: Apex body of NITI Aayog. PM chairs. Members = all State CMs + LGs of UTs with legislature (Delhi, Puducherry, J&K). Meets to discuss national priorities.
Regional Councils: Address issues specific to regions; PM/VC presides; CMs of States in region + relevant Central Ministers.
Task Forces/Expert Groups: Constituted for specific issues (e.g., Agriculture, Railways, Urban development).
Atal Innovation Mission (AIM): Flagship initiative — promotes innovation and entrepreneurship ecosystem; Atal Tinkering Labs in schools; Atal Incubation Centres.
No National Development Council: Planning Commission had NDC (PM + all CMs + Planning Commission members); NITI Aayog = Governing Council replaces NDC function.
Section 7 — Key Initiatives & Reports
Aspirational Districts Programme: 112 most underdeveloped districts → rapid development; NITI monitors progress monthly.
SDG India Index: Annual; States ranked on 17 SDGs across 115 indicators. Kerala = top performer consistently.
State Health Index: Annual; ranks States on health outcomes.
Composite Water Management Index: Ranks States on water management.
India Innovation Index: Ranks States on innovation ecosystem.
Viksit Bharat@2047: Road map for India to become developed nation by 2047 (centenary of independence).
Digital India, Startup India: NITI Aayog supports and provides policy frameworks.
Section 8 — Comparative Facts & Criticisms
Criticism 1: No financial powers = less leverage over States than Planning Commission.
Criticism 2: Non-statutory = no legal mandate; can be abolished easily.
Criticism 3: Governing Council meetings infrequent; States' voices not adequately heard.
Criticism 4: Overlaps with Finance Ministry and sector ministries in policy advice.
Support: Freed States from Centre's grip; more genuine partnership; better monitoring; tech-driven governance; innovation focus.
Finance Commission vs NITI Aayog: FC (Art.280) = constitutional, devolution of taxes; NITI = non-constitutional, no fund powers, policy advice only.
NITI vs Ministry of Finance: Finance Ministry = budget, spending, revenue; NITI Aayog = policy, strategy, think tank — no overlap in fund allocation.
