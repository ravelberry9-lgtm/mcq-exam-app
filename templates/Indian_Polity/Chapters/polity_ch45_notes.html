NCW | NCM | NCBC | NCPCR | NCMEI — All Statutory Bodies

National Commission for Women (NCW)
Act: National Commission for Women Act, 1990. Established: January 31, 1992
Nature: Statutory body; NOT constitutional
Composition: Chairperson + 5 members (at least 1 SC + 1 ST mandatory) + Member Secretary
Appointment: By Central Government. Tenure: 3 years
Functions: Review safeguards for women; recommend remedial measures; examine complaints; take suo motu notice of violations
Powers: Civil Court powers; can forward cases to Magistrate
First Chairperson: Jayanti Patnaik (1992)
National Commission for Minorities (NCM)
Act: National Commission for Minorities Act, 1992. Established: 1993
Nature: Statutory body; NOT constitutional
Composition: Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 5 members = 7 total; all from minority communities
Appointment: By Central Government. Tenure: 3 years (one re-appointment allowed)
Minorities notified (6): Muslims, Christians, Sikhs, Buddhists, Zoroastrians (Parsis), Jains (added 2014)
Functions: Evaluate minority development; monitor safeguards; investigate complaints
Powers: Civil Court powers during inquiry
National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC)
Constitutional status: 102nd Amendment 2018 → Art.338B (earlier statutory under NCBC Act 1993)
Composition: Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 members = 5. Appointed by President
Art.342A: President specifies Central OBC list; Parliament can include/exclude
Art.338B(9): Mandatory consultation — Centre and States MUST consult NCBC on major OBC policy matters
105th Amendment 2021: States restored power for their own State OBC lists
National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR)
Act: Commissions for Protection of Child Rights Act, 2005. Established: March 2007
Nature: Statutory body. Child = below 18 years
Composition: Chairperson + 6 members (at least 2 women; from education, child health, juvenile justice, disability, child labour, psychology)
Tenure: 3 years; age limit 60 (Chair), 65 (members); NOT eligible for re-appointment
Functions: Examine child rights laws/policies; monitor RTE Act; examine POCSO implementation; investigate violations
National Commission for Minority Educational Institutions (NCMEI)
Act: NCMEI Act, 2004. Chairperson = retired HC Chief Justice
Art.30: Right of religious/linguistic minorities to establish and administer educational institutions
Functions: Advise on minority education; examine complaints; grant minority status certificates
Powers: Civil Court powers; appellate authority in minority institution disputes
