Chapter 71 — National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA)

జాతీయ విపత్తు నిర్వహణ సంస్థ | Disaster Management Act 2005 | PM as Chairman

0. Background & Trigger

నేపథ్యం

Major trigger: Indian Ocean Tsunami December 2004 — killed ~10,800 in India.

Disaster Management Act 2005 enacted.

NDMA officially constituted: September 27, 2006.

Earlier: relief-focused; NDMA introduced prevention, mitigation, preparedness framework.

HQ: New Delhi.

1. Legal Framework

Disaster Management Act 2005

Disaster Management Act 2005: constitutes NDMA, SDMA, DDMA and NDRF.

Comprehensive national framework for disaster risk reduction and management.

Replaced earlier ad-hoc relief approach with institutionalized disaster management.

National Disaster Management Plan (NDMP) prepared under DM Act.

2. NDMA — Composition & Chairperson

సంఘటన

Chairperson: Prime Minister of India (ex-officio).

Vice-Chairperson: appointed by Central Government.

Members: up to 9 members (including VC) — appointed by PM.

NDMA HQ: New Delhi.

PM as chairman makes NDMA highest-level disaster authority.

3. SDMA & DDMA

State & District Levels

SDMA (State Disaster Management Authority): Chairperson = Chief Minister of state.

DDMA (District Disaster Management Authority): Chairperson = District Collector/District Magistrate.

Three-tier structure: NDMA (national) → SDMA (state) → DDMA (district).

NEC (National Executive Committee): headed by Union Home Secretary — implements NDMA guidelines.

4. NDRF — National Disaster Response Force

NDRF

NDRF: National Disaster Response Force — dedicated federal disaster response force.

Under NDMA's overall supervision.

Director General (DG): IPS officer heads NDRF.

NDRF battalions stationed across India for quick deployment.

Deployed for floods, earthquakes, cyclones, landslides.

5. Powers & Functions of NDMA

విధులు

Lay down policies, plans, guidelines for disaster management.

Approve National Disaster Management Plan (NDMP).

Coordinate response activities at national level.

Recommend relief norms (minimum standards).

Capacity building, awareness — disaster preparedness.

Issue guidelines to Ministries/Departments/SDMAs.

6. National Disaster Management Plan (NDMP)

NDMP

NDMP: first national plan under DM Act 2005 — released 2016, updated 2019.

Based on Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction 2015-2030.

Four priorities: understanding risk, governance, investing in DRR, enhancing preparedness.

NDMP approved by NDMA.
