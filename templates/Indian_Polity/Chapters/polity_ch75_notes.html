Chapter 75 — North Eastern Council (NEC)

ఈశాన్య మండలి | NEC Act 1971 | 8 North Eastern States

0. Background & Establishment

నేపథ్యం

NEC = North Eastern Council: statutory advisory body for NE region development.

Established by North Eastern Council Act 1971.

First meeting: November 7, 1972 at Shillong (headquarters).

NEC Act 1971 amended by NEC (Amendment) Act 2002 — significantly expanded mandate.

HQ: Shillong, Meghalaya.

1. Member States

Member States

Originally 6 states (1972): Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Nagaland, Tripura + Arunachal Pradesh (then NEFA).

Mizoram joined after becoming full state (1987).

Sikkim added as member by 2002 Amendment — now 8 members.

8 states: Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, Tripura.

2. Composition

సంఘటన

Chairman: Union Home Minister (ex-officio) — after 2002 Amendment.

Before 2002: Governor of each member state was Chairman on rotation / Governor of Meghalaya.

Members: Governor + Chief Minister of each member state.

Central Government representative: may be nominated.

NEC Secretary: senior IAS officer.

3. Functions

విధులు

Prepare regional development plans for NE region.

Review implementation of development programmes in NE states.

Recommend measures for economic and social development.

Coordinate inter-state activities in NE region.

Post-2002: also a planning body for NE region development.

Work in conjunction with DoNER (Ministry of Development of NE Region).

4. 2002 Amendment Changes

2002 Amendment

Sikkim added as member (8th state).

NEC chairman: Union Home Minister (ex-officio) — replacing Governor system.

NEC mandate expanded: from advisory to include planning functions.

NEC now functions as Regional Planning Body for NE.

DoNER Ministry created (2004) to work alongside NEC.

5. DoNER Ministry

DoNER

DoNER = Ministry of Development of North Eastern Region.

Created 2004 to give focused attention to NE development.

DoNER works in coordination with NEC for NE development.

NLCPR (Non-Lapsable Central Pool of Resources): funds for NE development through DoNER/NEC.

6. Significance

Significance

Forum for inter-state coordination among 8 NE states.

Addresses common regional problems: connectivity, security, economic development.

Regional development planning: power, transport, industry, tourism.

Acts as forum for peaceful resolution of inter-state issues.

'Act East Policy' implementation partner for NE region.
