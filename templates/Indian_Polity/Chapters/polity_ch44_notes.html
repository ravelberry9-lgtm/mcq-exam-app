Ch44 — జాతీయ మానవ హక్కుల కమిషన్ / NHRC

PHRA 1993 | Statutory Body | Paris Principles

Background & PHRA 1993
PHRA 1993: Protection of Human Rights Act — established NHRC (statutory, NOT constitutional)
Established: October 12, 1993. HQ: New Delhi
Paris Principles: UN 1993 standards — NHRIs must be independent, pluralistic, broad mandate
GANHRI accreditation: "A" status (Global Alliance of NHRIs)
Composition (post-2019 Amendment)
Chairperson: Retired Chief Justice of India OR retired SC judge (2019 broadened eligibility)
Members: Up to 5 — including 2 retired SC judges + 3 from human rights/women/minorities/children fields
Ex-officio Members: Chairpersons of NCSC, NCST, NCBC, NCPCR, NCW, NCM, NCMEI = 7 ex-officio
Appointment, Tenure & Removal
Appointment: By President on recommendation of 6-member Committee: PM (Chair) + Speaker LS + Home Minister + Leader of Opposition LS + Leader of Opposition RS + Deputy Chairman RS
Tenure: 3 years OR age 70 (whichever earlier); eligible for re-appointment once
Post-retirement: NOT eligible for further employment by Centre or State
Removal: By President after SC inquiry (on misbehaviour/incapacity)
Salary: Charged on Consolidated Fund of India
Jurisdiction & Powers
Inquiry powers: Civil Court powers — summon, receive evidence, compel documents, examine witnesses
Armed forces: Can ONLY seek report from Central Government; cannot directly investigate (Section 19 PHRA)
Time limit: Cannot inquire into matters more than 1 year old
Visit: Can inspect prisons and detention centres under Central/State Govt
Recommendations: Advisory only — NOT binding on government
Can approach SC/HC: Can approach courts for orders; can intervene in HC proceedings
State Human Rights Commissions (SHRCs)
Chairperson: Retired Chief Justice of a High Court
Appointment: By Governor (CM + Speaker + Home Minister + Leader of Opposition)
Jurisdiction: State List subjects only; Central subjects → NHRC
Transfer: NHRC can transfer to SHRC; SHRC cannot transfer to NHRC
Limitations
"Toothless tiger": No enforcement power; recommendations not binding
Armed forces gap: Cannot directly investigate
1-year bar: Complaints older than 1 year not accepted
Private parties: Not covered — only state actors
