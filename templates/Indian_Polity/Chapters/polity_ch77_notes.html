🏛️ Special Provisions for Certain Classes

App Chapter 77  |  Lakshmikanth Book Ch74

0. Overview and Constitutional Basis

Part XVI – Special Provisions (Art.330–342A)

Part XVI: Art.330 to Art.342A covers special provisions for certain classes

SC/ST reservation in Lok Sabha (Art.330) + State Assemblies (Art.332)

Anglo-Indian reserved seats (Art.331 + Art.333) — abolished by 104th Amendment 2020

OBC — Art.340: President can appoint Commission to investigate OBC conditions

Art.341: Presidential notification of Scheduled Castes (SCs)

Art.342: Presidential notification of Scheduled Tribes (STs)

Art.342A (added by 105th Amendment 2021): Central List of Socially and Educationally Backward Classes (SEBCs/OBCs)

Reservation Philosophy

Art.14 (equality) + Art.15(4) (special provision for SC/ST/OBC) + Art.16(4) (reservation in services)

Art.16(4A): reservation in promotion for SC/ST (added by 77th Amendment 1995)

Art.16(4B): carry-forward of unfilled vacancies for SC/ST not subject to 50% ceiling

Indra Sawhney 1992: 50% overall reservation ceiling (except extraordinary situations)

1. Scheduled Castes (Art.341)

Art.341 – Presidential Specification of SCs

Art.341(1): President may specify castes, races, tribes, parts/groups → constitute SCs for any State/UT by public notification (after consulting the Governor)

Art.341(2): Parliament can include/exclude from the Presidential list by law

SC cannot belong to religion other than Hinduism, Sikhism, or Buddhism (Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order 1950)

Christians and Muslims cannot be in SC list (subject to ongoing debate/judicial review)

SC Reservation

Art.330: SC/ST seats reserved in Lok Sabha

Art.332: SC/ST seats reserved in State Legislative Assemblies

SC seats: 84 in Lok Sabha (out of 543), STs: 47 seats

101st Amendment 2016 (GST): no change to SC/ST reservation

104th Amendment 2020: extended SC/ST reservation to 2030; abolished Anglo-Indian nominated seats

2. Scheduled Tribes (Art.342)

Art.342 – Presidential Specification of STs

Art.342(1): President specifies STs (tribal communities) by public notification for any State/UT after consulting Governor

Art.342(2): Parliament can include/exclude by law

ST criteria: primitive traits, geographical isolation, shyness of contact with community, backwardness

No religion bar for STs (unlike SCs) — can be of any religion

Fifth Schedule and Sixth Schedule

Fifth Schedule: Tribal Areas in 10 states (AP, Telangana, Odisha, MP, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Gujarat, Rajasthan, Maharashtra, HP)

Sixth Schedule: Tribal Areas in 4 NE states (Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, Mizoram)

Tribes Advisory Council: Fifth Schedule states with substantial STs must have TAC

PESA Act 1996: Panchayats Extension to Scheduled Areas

3. OBCs and Art.340

Art.340 – Backward Classes Commission

Art.340: President may appoint Commission to investigate conditions of socially and educationally backward classes and recommend steps for their advancement

First Backward Classes Commission: 1953 — Kaka Kalelkar Commission; report 1955

Second BC Commission: 1979 — Mandal Commission (B.P. Mandal); report 1980

Mandal Commission: recommended 27% reservation for OBCs in central services

Indra Sawhney v. Union of India 1992 (Mandal case): SC upheld 27% OBC reservation; BUT struck down reservation in promotion; 50% ceiling affirmed; creamy layer exclusion for OBCs

105th Amendment 2021 — Art.342A

105th Amendment 2021: Restored power of States to identify OBCs for their own reservation purposes

Added Art.342A: Central Government has Central List of SEBCs; President specifies with Parliament's concurrence

Background: SC judgment in Maratha Reservation case (2021) held States had no power to identify OBCs for central list — 105th Amendment reversed this for State lists

States can now maintain their own State OBC lists independently

4. Anglo-Indians (Art.331 and Art.333)

Anglo-Indian Provisions (Now Abolished)

Art.331 (original): President could nominate 2 Anglo-Indians to Lok Sabha if inadequately represented

Art.333 (original): Governor could nominate 1 Anglo-Indian to State Legislative Assembly

104th Constitution Amendment Act 2020: abolished Anglo-Indian nominated seats in Lok Sabha and State Assemblies effective January 25, 2020

Anglo-Indian = person whose father/male line ancestor was of European descent but who is domiciled in India and born of parents permanently resident in India

Anglo-Indian Reservation History

Originally Anglo-Indian seats were for 10 years from commencement of Constitution (1950-1960)

Extended by successive amendments every 10 years until 104th Amendment 2020 abolished them

104th Amendment 2020 also extended SC/ST reservation in Parliament and State assemblies until 2030

Art.366(2): defines Anglo-Indians for constitutional purposes

5. Reservation in Services (Art.16)

Art.16(4) – Reservation in Public Employment

Art.16(4): State can make provision for reservation in public services for backward classes (SC/ST/OBC) not adequately represented

Art.16(4A): Reservation in promotion for SC/ST (added 77th Amendment 1995)

Art.16(4B): Carry-forward unfilled SC/ST vacancies not subject to 50% cap (added 81st Amendment 2000)

Art.335: Reservation must be consistent with maintenance of efficiency of administration

Indra Sawhney Case (1992) — Key Holdings

Mandal Commission 27% OBC reservation upheld

Total reservation ceiling: 50% (except extraordinary circumstances)

Creamy layer exclusion: advanced sections of OBCs to be excluded (income/status criterion)

Reservation in promotion struck down (later partially reversed by 77th Amendment)

No creamy layer concept for SCs and STs (only OBCs have creamy layer exclusion)

6. National Commissions (Art.338-338B)

National Commission for SCs (Art.338)

Art.338: National Commission for Scheduled Castes — constitutional body

89th Amendment 2003: bifurcated into (1) NCSC — Art.338 and (2) NCST — Art.338A

Before 89th Amendment: single National Commission for SC and ST

NCSC: Chairman + Vice-Chairman + 3 other members; appointed by President

National Commission for STs (Art.338A) and OBCs (Art.338B)

Art.338A: National Commission for Scheduled Tribes (added by 89th Amendment 2003)

Art.338B: National Commission for Backward Classes (added by 102nd Amendment 2018)

NCBC: constitutional status elevated from statutory (National Commission for Backward Classes Act 1993) by 102nd Amendment 2018

All three Commissions (NCSC, NCST, NCBC) investigate safeguards, monitor implementation, advise Centre/States, submit annual reports to President
