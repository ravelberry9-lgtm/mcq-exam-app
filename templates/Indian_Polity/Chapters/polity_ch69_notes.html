Chapter 69 — Public Interest Litigation (PIL)

జనహిత వ్యాజ్యం | Art.32 + Art.226 | India's Democratic Tool

0. Meaning & Origin of PIL

PIL అర్థం

PIL = Public Interest Litigation: any person can approach SC/HC for enforcement of legal/constitutional rights of public importance.

India: PIL started in late 1970s-1980s — Justice P.N. Bhagwati and Justice V.R. Krishna Iyer.

USA: similar concept — Class Action suits; India's PIL is uniquely home-grown variant.

Traditional litigation: aggrieved person files. PIL: any public-spirited person can file for public interest.

1. Constitutional Basis of PIL

రాజ్యాంగ ప్రాతిపదిక

Art.32: SC writ jurisdiction — PIL filed in SC for FR violations.

Art.226: HC writ jurisdiction — PIL filed in HC for FR or other legal right violations.

Locus standi relaxed: any member of public can file PIL — court itself can treat letter as PIL.

Epistolary jurisdiction: SC accepts letters/postcards from prisoners/poor as writ petitions.

2. Features of PIL

లక్షణాలు

Relaxed locus standi: petitioner need not be directly aggrieved party.

Low/no cost: nominal court fees — accessible to poor and marginalized.

Flexible procedure: court may appoint amicus curiae, commissioners to investigate.

Remedy oriented: court may give continuing mandamus — monitor compliance over time.

No fixed adversarial form: court can summon govt directly.

3. Pioneers of PIL in India

PIL Pioneers

Justice P.N. Bhagwati: accepted letters as PIL; expanded Art.32 jurisdiction; pioneered epistolary jurisdiction.

Justice V.R. Krishna Iyer: social justice focus; liberalized PIL rules; recognized third-party standing.

Hussainara Khatoon (1979): Justice Bhagwati — accepted letter from journalist as PIL — first major PIL case.

S.P. Gupta v. Union of India (1981): formal expansion of locus standi for PIL — "sufficient interest" test.

4. Landmark PIL Cases

ముఖ్య PIL కేసులు

Hussainara Khatoon (1979): Undertrial prisoners — right to speedy trial.

Bandhua Mukti Morcha (1984): Bonded labour — SC issued detailed rehabilitation directions.

MC Mehta (1987+): Environmental protection — Ganga, Taj Mahal, Delhi pollution.

Vishaka (1997): Sexual harassment guidelines at workplace.

PUCL (2003): Right to food — PDS, midday meals.

Common Cause v. Union of India: Various — vehicular pollution, voter registration, CNG.

2G Spectrum (2012): SC cancelled 122 telecom licenses — public interest in natural resources.

5. PIL Jurisdiction & Amicus Curiae

Jurisdiction / Amicus Curiae

PIL in SC: Art.32 — Original writ jurisdiction; PIL in HC: Art.226 — broader jurisdiction.

Amicus Curiae: "Friend of Court" — expert appointed by court to assist in complex PIL matters.

Court commissioners: SC appoints commissioners to visit sites, report on conditions (jails, bonded labour areas).

Continuing Mandamus: court retains jurisdiction until complete compliance — used in environmental PILs.

6. Misuse & Criticism of PIL

PIL విమర్శలు

PIL industry: frivolous/motivated PILs for publicity, personal interest, or to harass opponents.

Judicial Overreach: PIL sometimes enables courts to enter executive/legislative domain.

Docket explosion: PIL cases clog SC/HC schedules.

Inconsistency: different PIL outcomes in similar cases.

Counter-majoritarian: unelected courts making policy through PIL orders.
