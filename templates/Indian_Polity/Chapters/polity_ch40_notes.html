Chapter 40 — SC, ST & OBC జాతీయ కమిషన్లు

Art.338 (NCSC) | Art.338A (NCST) | Art.338B (NCBC) | Constitutional Bodies

Section 1 — Art.338: National Commission for Scheduled Castes
Art.338: NCSC = Constitutional body. Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 members = 5 total. Appointed by President.
89th Amendment 2003: Bifurcated single SC/ST commission → separate NCSC (Art.338) and NCST (Art.338A).
65th Amendment 1990: Replaced original single Commissioner with multi-member Commission.
Section 2 — Art.338A: National Commission for Scheduled Tribes
Art.338A: NCST = added by 89th Amendment 2003. Same composition: 5 members appointed by President.
5th Schedule: NCST advisory role for Tribal areas in 10 States.
PESA 1996: NCST reviews implementation in Scheduled Areas.
Section 3 — Art.338B: National Commission for Backward Classes
Art.338B: NCBC = added by 102nd Amendment 2018. Constitutional status (earlier statutory under 1993 Act).
Art.342A: Also added by 102nd Amendment — President specifies Central OBC list; Parliament may include/exclude.
105th Amendment 2021: Restored States' power to identify OBC for their lists.
Section 4 — Common Features of All Three Commissions
Composition: Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 other members = 5 total (all three).
Appointment: By President of India.
Functions: Investigate complaints, inquire, advise Centre/States, recommend measures, evaluate progress, present annual reports to President.
Civil Court Powers: All three = power to summon persons, require documents, receive evidence on affidavit, request public records.
Annual Report: To President; laid before Parliament (+ State Legislature if State matter).
Not binding: Recommendations advisory — governments not legally bound.
Section 5 — Constitutional Amendments Timeline
Original Constitution: Art.338 = single Special Officer (Commissioner) for SC & ST.
65th Amendment 1990: Multi-member Commission for SC & ST.
89th Amendment 2003: Separate NCSC (Art.338) + NCST (Art.338A).
102nd Amendment 2018: NCBC constitutional (Art.338B) + Art.342A (OBC list).
104th Amendment 2020: Removed Anglo-Indians from NCSC coverage.
105th Amendment 2021: Restored State power for OBC identification.
Indra Sawhney 1992: 27% OBC upheld; non-creamy layer only; 50% cap; separate NCBC mandated.
