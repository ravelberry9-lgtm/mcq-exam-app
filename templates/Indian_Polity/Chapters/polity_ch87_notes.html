National Integration

App Ch87 · Book Ch84 · Indian Polity (Lakshmikanth)

1. Meaning and Importance

What is National Integration?

National integration means the unification of a diverse nation into a single political community with a shared sense of identity, loyalty, and purpose. It involves the psychological and emotional process by which people of diverse backgrounds — linguistic, religious, cultural, regional — feel themselves part of one nation.

India's Challenge

India is characterised by extraordinary diversity — 22+ scheduled languages, hundreds of dialects, multiple religions, thousands of castes, and diverse regional cultures. The colonial legacy of partition further complicated the integration challenge. India's founding fathers chose unity in diversity as the national motto.

National Integration Council (NIC)

Established in 1961 during Jawaharlal Nehru's tenure

Chaired by the Prime Minister of India

Purpose: To consider measures to combat communalism, casteism, regionalism, linguism, and narrow-mindedness

NIC is an advisory body — not a statutory body

Reconstituted periodically; last reconstituted under PM Manmohan Singh (2013)

Membership: PM, Union Cabinet ministers, CM of all states, leaders of major political parties

2. Communalism

Meaning

Communalism is the ideology that holds that the interests of members of one religious community are fundamentally different from and opposed to those of other religious communities. It seeks to mobilise people on the basis of religious identity for political purposes.

Historical Background

Partition (1947) — based on two-nation theory; traumatic communal violence

1984 anti-Sikh riots following Indira Gandhi's assassination

1992 Babri Masjid demolition and aftermath riots

2002 Gujarat riots

Moplah Rebellion 1921 (Mapilla uprising Kerala — communal dimensions)

Causes of Communalism

Historical: Colonial divide-and-rule policy; separate electorates under Morley-Minto 1909

Political: Vote bank politics; communal organisations; religion-based mobilisation

Socio-economic: Competition for jobs, land, and resources between communities

Failure of secularism in practice — mixing of religion and state despite constitutional secularism

Media and social media: Fake news, hate speech spreading communal tensions

Constitutional and Legal Measures

Article 25–28: Freedom of religion and secular state

Article 15: Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion

Sec.153A IPC: Promoting enmity between classes — imprisonment up to 3 years

Sec.295A IPC: Deliberate acts to outrage religious feelings

Prevention of Communal and Targeted Violence (Access to Justice and Reparations) Bill — proposed but not enacted

National Foundation for Communal Harmony — established 1992; promotes communal harmony

3. Regionalism and Linguistic Nationalism

Regionalism

Regionalism is the belief that the interests of a particular region should take precedence over national interests. It may be based on language, culture, geography, economic disparities, or a distinct ethnic identity. India has experienced regionalism leading to both centrifugal and centripetal tendencies.

Forms of Regionalism

Demand for separate statehood: Telangana (achieved 2014), Jharkhand (2000), Uttarakhand (2000), Chhattisgarh (2000), Gorkhaland (ongoing), Vidarbha (ongoing), Bodoland (ongoing)

Secessionism: Demand for independence — Punjab Khalistan movement (1980s), Assam ULFA, Kashmir separatism, Nagaland secessionism

Sons of the soil: Maharashtra (Shiv Sena — Marathi vs. South Indians), assam (against Bangladeshi migrants)

River water disputes: Cauvery (Karnataka-Tamil Nadu), Ravi-Beas (Punjab-Haryana-Rajasthan)

States Reorganisation

States Reorganisation Act 1956 — based on linguistic lines following Fazl Ali Commission (1953) recommendation

Potti Sriramulu fasted unto death for Andhra Pradesh; state created November 1, 1956

Telangana created June 2, 2014 — 29th state; Andhra Pradesh bifurcated

India now has 28 states + 8 UTs (after J&K bifurcation 2019 and Dadra/DNH merger)

Language Issues

Hindi imposition controversy — Tamil Nadu opposition to three-language formula; 1965 agitation

Official Languages Act 1963 — English continued as official language along with Hindi

Eighth Schedule languages: 22 scheduled languages

Classical language status: Tamil (2004), Sanskrit (2005), Kannada (2008), Telugu (2008), Malayalam (2013), Odia (2014), Marathi (2024)

4. Casteism and Untouchability

Casteism as a Challenge

Casteism — the practice of giving preference to members of one's own caste and discriminating against those of other castes — is a major challenge to national integration. The caste system creates hierarchy and exclusion, preventing the emergence of a unified national identity.

Untouchability

Article 17: Abolition of untouchability — a fundamental right; practice is a punishable offence

Protection of Civil Rights Act 1955 (earlier Untouchability Offences Act): punishes caste-based discrimination

SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act 1989: stringent punishment for atrocities against SC/ST; amended 2018

Manual Scavenging: Prohibition of Employment as Manual Scavengers and their Rehabilitation Act 2013

Despite legal provisions, caste discrimination and atrocities continue; Dalit movements (Ambedkarite) advocate for full equality

Reservation Policy

Art.15(4)/16(4): Reservation in education and services for SC/ST/OBC

SC 15%, ST 7.5%, OBC 27%, EWS 10% (103rd Amendment 2019)

Reservations aim to overcome historical exclusion but also generate tensions (anti-reservation protests, Mandal agitations)

B.R. Ambedkar: "Caste is a system of graded inequality"

5. Terrorism, Naxalism, and Insurgency

Internal Security Challenges

Left-wing extremism (Naxalism/Maoism): Active in "Red Corridor" — Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Bihar, AP/Telangana, Maharashtra. CPI(Maoist) is designated terrorist organisation

Northeast insurgency: Naga insurgency (oldest — NSCN-IM Nagaland), Assam (ULFA), Manipur (various groups), Tripura (NLFT, ATTF)

Kashmir terrorism: Cross-border sponsored terrorism; Article 370 abrogation August 2019

Khalistan movement (Punjab): 1980s — SGPC, Jarnail Singh Bhindranwale; Operation Blue Star 1984

Legal Measures Against Terrorism

UAPA (Unlawful Activities Prevention Act) 1967 — amended 2008, 2019; principal anti-terrorism law

NIA (National Investigation Agency) Act 2008 — federal investigation agency for terrorism

TADA (Terrorist and Disruptive Activities Prevention Act) 1987–95 — lapsed

POTA (Prevention of Terrorism Act) 2002 — repealed 2004 by UPA

Naxalism and Measures

Naxal movement origin: Naxalbari uprising 1967 (West Bengal, Charu Mazumdar)

Government response: Integrated Action Plan (IAP) for Naxal-affected districts — development + security

Operation Green Hunt — security forces operations in Red Corridor areas

Aspirational Districts Programme (later Vibrant Villages) — development for tribal/backward regions

6. Constitutional Provisions

Preamble

The Preamble declares India a "Sovereign Socialist Secular Democratic Republic" and aims to secure "Unity and Integrity of the Nation" — the ultimate goal of national integration. The word "secular" was added by the 42nd Amendment 1976.

Key Articles for National Integration

Art.1: India is a Union of States — "Union" not "Federation" implies indestructible unity

Art.3: Parliament can create, alter, or abolish states — preserves Central power over territorial integrity

Art.14: Equality before law — counters casteism, communalism

Art.15: No discrimination on basis of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth

Art.17: Abolition of untouchability

Art.25–28: Religious freedom with state secularism

Art.51A: Fundamental Duties — including to promote harmony and spirit of common brotherhood transcending religious, linguistic, regional diversity

Art.356: President's Rule — Central intervention in states facing breakdown of constitutional machinery (including communal breakdown)

Fundamental Duties (Art.51A) Relevant to Integration

Art.51A(e): To promote harmony and the spirit of common brotherhood among all the people of India transcending religious, linguistic, regional or sectional diversities

Art.51A(f): To value and preserve the rich heritage of our composite culture

Art.51A(i): To safeguard public property and to abjure violence

These duties were added by 42nd Amendment 1976 (Swaran Singh Committee recommendation)

7. Measures for National Integration

Institutional Measures

National Integration Council (NIC) — PM chaired; periodic meetings on communal harmony

National Foundation for Communal Harmony (NFCH) — 1992; scholarships for riot orphans

National Commission for Minorities — protects minority rights

Kendriya Vidyalayas and NCC — common educational/cultural experiences across regions

All India Services (IAS, IPS, IFS) — officers serve across states; promotes national outlook

Socio-Cultural Measures

Composite culture emphasis — Sarva Dharma Samabhav (equal respect for all religions)

National festivals: Republic Day, Independence Day, Gandhi Jayanti — shared national occasions

Inter-state cultural exchanges, shared curriculum elements

Ekta Diwas (National Unity Day) — October 31 (Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel's birth anniversary); observed since 2014

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel's "Iron Man" role in integrating 562 princely states

Economic Measures

Reducing inter-regional economic disparities — backward region funds, Article 275 grants

Finance Commission recommendations for horizontal devolution to poorer states

Development of backward tribal areas (Fifth and Sixth Schedules)

Northeast development — Look/Act East Policy, DoNER Ministry, NEC
