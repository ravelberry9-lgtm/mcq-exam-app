0. Meaning and Role of Political Parties

Definition and Significance

Political party = group of people organised to acquire and exercise political power

Edmund Burke: "A body of men united for promoting by their joint endeavours the national interest upon some particular principle"

Essential for democracy — link between people and government

Constitution does not explicitly mention political parties (except in 10th Schedule — Anti-Defection)

RPA 1951 and ECI regulations govern political party recognition and functioning

Functions of Political Parties

Contesting elections and forming governments

Formulating and implementing policies

Political education and socialisation of citizens

Articulating and aggregating interests

Providing loyal opposition and political accountability

Recruiting and training political leaders

1. National Party Recognition (ECI)

Criteria for National Party Status (2014 ECI Rules)

A party is recognized as a National Party if it satisfies ANY ONE of 3 conditions:

Condition 1: Secures at least 6% of valid votes polled in 4 or more States in LS general election OR State Assembly elections, AND wins at least 4 seats in LS

Condition 2: Wins at least 2% of total LS seats (currently 11 seats out of 543) from at least 3 different States

Condition 3: Recognized as State Party in at least 4 States

National Parties (2023 ECI Status)

BJP (Bharatiya Janata Party) — National Party

INC/Congress (Indian National Congress) — National Party

BSP (Bahujan Samaj Party) — National Party

CPI(M) (Communist Party of India-Marxist) — National Party

NPP (National People's Party) — National Party (from Meghalaya)

Note: AAP, NCP, TMC have had national party status; ECI reviews and can revoke status

2. State Party Recognition

Criteria for State Party Status

A party recognized as State Party in a State if it satisfies ANY ONE of these:

Condition 1: Secures at least 6% of valid votes in State + wins 2 seats in State Assembly election

Condition 2: Wins at least 3% of seats or minimum 3 seats in State Assembly (whichever more)

Condition 3: Wins at least 1 LS seat for every 25 LS seats or part thereof from State

Condition 4: Secures at least 8% of votes in the State in a LS election or State Assembly election

Benefits of Recognition

National parties: exclusive party symbol throughout India

Allotted reserved election symbol (no one else can use it)

Free broadcast time on AIR/Doordarshan during elections

Access to electoral rolls, supply of polling station details at reduced cost

Proposer requirement: 1 proposer (not 10) in nomination paper

Star Campaigner benefit: recognized party can have more star campaigners

3. Registration of Political Parties

Registration under RPA 1951

Section 29A, Representation of People Act 1951: Any association can apply to ECI for registration as a political party

Requirements: (1) 30-day public notice; (2) Memorandum/rules of association; (3) Commitment to socialism, secularism, democracy; (4) Allegiance to Constitution and sovereignty

ECI registers parties — but registration ≠ recognition (recognition requires meeting vote/seat criteria)

Registered unrecognised parties: can contest but don't get special benefits

ECI Powers over Parties

ECI allots election symbols to registered parties

ECI decides disputes over party names and symbols

ECI can split party symbols if there is a split in a party

ECI has power to de-register parties in extreme cases (very limited use)

ECI cannot dissolve a political party or interfere in internal affairs

4. Party System in India

Multi-Party System

India has a multi-party system (not two-party like USA/UK)

Multiple national parties + numerous state parties + many independent candidates

Coalition governments common at Centre and States since 1989

First-Past-The-Post (FPTP) electoral system encourages regionalism

Over 2,500 registered parties; only 6-8 recognised national parties

Historical Development

INC dominated 1947–1967: "Congress System" (Rajni Kothari)

1967: Congress dominance weakened; regional parties emerged

1977: First non-Congress government at Centre (Janata Party)

1989 onwards: Coalition era — unstable governments

1999-2014: NDA era (BJP-led coalition)

2014-present: BJP majority without coalition

5. Election Commission's Role

ECI and Parties

ECI conducts elections; recognizes parties; allots symbols; enforces Model Code of Conduct

Election Symbols (Reservation and Allotment) Order 1968: governs symbol allotment

ECI is the authority to decide disputes over party names, leadership, symbols during splits

Indira Gandhi vs Syndicate (1969): ECI allotted Congress-I the name and symbol after party split

Janata Party vs ECI: ECI powers to decide recognized party issues upheld

Model Code of Conduct

MCC: guidelines for parties and candidates during election period

Not statutory — based on consensus; but ECI enforces it

Covers: election speeches, polling day conduct, parties in power, manifesto guidelines (2013)

MCC violations can lead to notices, warnings, deferral of election dates

6. Political Party Funding

Election Funding and Transparency

Parties receive funds from membership fees, donations, public meetings

Electoral Bonds: introduced by Finance Act 2017; SBI sells bonds; parties encash anonymously

SC struck down Electoral Bonds scheme (February 15, 2024): violation of RTI, Art.19(1)(a); upheld in ADR v. Union of India case

FCRA: foreign contributions to political parties are prohibited

Parties with annual income >20 lakh must file audit report with ECI

IT exemption: Section 13A income tax exemption for political parties

Transparency Issues

RTI Act: political parties are NOT public authorities (SC Delhi HC ruling; SC referred to larger bench)

Donations below Rs.20,000: anonymous (no disclosure required)

ECI recommendation: state funding of elections (not implemented)

ADR (Association for Democratic Reforms): NGO that campaigns for party funding transparency

7. Key Facts Quick Revision

Key Numbers

National Party: 6% in 4+ states + 4 LS seats OR 2% LS seats from 3 states OR State party in 4 states 2% LS = 11 seats (543) State Party: 6% + 2 seats OR 3% seats OR 8% votes Sec.29A RPA 1951 — registration Rs.20,000 threshold — anonymous donations

Quick Facts

Constitution does not mention political parties (except 10th Schedule anti-defection)

RPA 1951 Section 29A: registration of parties with ECI

Election Symbols Order 1968: symbol allotment rules

Electoral Bonds struck down by SC: February 15, 2024

India: multi-party system, over 2,500 registered parties

ECI can review national/state party status after every election
