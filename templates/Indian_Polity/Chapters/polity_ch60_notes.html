0. Historical Background & 7th Amendment 1956

నేపథ్యం

States Reorganisation Act 1956 — linguistic basis state formation.

7th Constitutional Amendment 1956: Article 350B inserted — Special Officer for Linguistic Minorities.

Fazl Ali Commission (SRC 1953-55): Recommended linguistic states + minority language protections.

India's linguistic diversity: 22 Eighth Schedule languages; 100s of dialects.

1. Article 350B — Special Officer for Linguistic Minorities

Article 350B

Article 350B(1): President shall appoint Special Officer for Linguistic Minorities.

Duty: Investigate all matters relating to safeguards for linguistic minorities.

Report: To President at intervals → President lays before Parliament + sends to State Govts.

Article 350B(2): President prescribes duties.

2. Article 350 & 350A — Language Rights

Articles 350, 350A

Article 350: Any person may submit representation to Central/State Govt in own language (no Hindi/English compulsion).

Article 350A: DPSP — adequate primary education facilities in mother tongue for linguistic minority children.

Article 29: Linguistic minorities right to conserve language, script, culture.

Article 30: Linguistic/religious minorities right to establish/administer educational institutions.

3. Commissioner for Linguistic Minorities — Functions

Commissioner functions

Title: Commissioner for Linguistic Minorities (CLM).

Office: Allahabad (HQ) + Regional offices: Belgaum (Karnataka), Chennai, Kolkata.

Investigate: Constitutional safeguards — 3-Language Formula, mother tongue primary education, minority language media, courts use.

Annual/periodic Report: President కి → Parliament + State Govts.

4. Eighth Schedule & Official Languages

8th Schedule

Original: 14 languages. Currently 22 languages.

Article 344: Official Languages Commission; Article 351: Hindi development.

Languages added by amendments: Sindhi (21st, 1967), Konkani/Manipuri/Nepali (71st, 1992), Bodo/Dogri/Maithili/Santhali (92nd, 2003).

Classical Languages: 6 declared (Tamil 2004, Sanskrit 2005, Kannada+Telugu 2008, Malayalam 2013, Odia 2014).

5. States Reorganisation & Linguistic States

SRC 1953-55

States Reorganisation Commission (SRC): S.K. Fazl Ali, H.N. Kunzru, K.M. Panikkar.

SRC recommended: Linguistic states; but not purely; national unity consideration.

States Reorganisation Act 1956: 14 States + 6 UTs (major reorganisation).

First linguistic state demand: Andhra Pradesh (Potti Sriramulu fast, 1952) — AP October 1, 1953.

6. Language Safeguards for Minorities

Safeguards

3-Language Formula: Regional + Hindi + English (South: regional + any modern Indian language + English).

Article 350A: Mother tongue education (primary level) — State Govt duty.

Article 29: Cultural + linguistic identity protection.

CLM investigates: Whether states comply with language safeguard provisions.
