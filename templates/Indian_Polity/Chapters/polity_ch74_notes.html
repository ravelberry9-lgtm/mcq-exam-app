Chapter 74 — Delimitation Commission

పరిమితి కమిషన్ | Art.82 + Art.170 | Parliamentary & Assembly Constituencies

0. Meaning & Purpose

అర్థం

Delimitation: redrawing boundaries of electoral constituencies (Lok Sabha + State Assemblies).

Purpose: equal representation — each constituency should have roughly equal population.

Constitutional basis: Art.82 (Parliament seats after census) + Art.170 (State Assembly seats).

Delimitation Commission: independent body to carry out delimitation after each census.

1. Constitutional Provisions

రాజ్యాంగ నిబంధనలు

Art.82: Parliament shall readjust allocation of seats + divisional of constituencies after each census.

Art.170: State Legislative Assembly seats readjusted after census.

Art.327: Parliament can make provisions with respect to elections to Parliament and State Legislatures.

42nd Amendment 1976: froze seats/delimitation until 2001 census.

84th Amendment 2001: extended freeze on seat allocation until after 2026 census.

2. Delimitation Commissions in India

కమిషన్లు

1st Delimitation Commission: 1952 — after 1951 census.

2nd Delimitation Commission: 1963 — after 1961 census.

3rd Delimitation Commission: 1973 — after 1971 census.

4th Delimitation Commission: 2002 — after 2001 census.

No Delimitation Commission after 1981 and 1991 censuses (seats frozen by 42nd Amendment).

3. Composition

సంఘటన

Chairman: retired judge of Supreme Court.

Ex-officio members: Chief Election Commissioner + State Election Commissioners (for state-level delimitation).

Associate members: Lok Sabha/State Assembly members from the concerned state.

Constituted by Delimitation Act (parliamentary legislation).

4. Powers & Finality

అధికారాలు

Orders have force of law — cannot be questioned in any court.

Orders published in Gazette of India + State Gazette.

Orders come into force on date specified in the order.

No court (including SC) can question delimitation orders.

Associate members cannot dissent from commission's final orders.

5. SC/ST Reserved Constituencies

Reserved Constituencies

Delimitation Commission determines SC/ST reserved constituencies.

SC reservation: Art.330 (Lok Sabha) + Art.332 (State Assemblies).

Criteria: percentage of SC/ST population in constituency determines reservation.

Reserved constituencies rotate after each delimitation.

6. Seat Freeze (42nd + 84th Amendments)

Seat Freeze

42nd Amendment 1976: froze seats in Lok Sabha and State Assemblies until 2001 census.

84th Amendment 2001: extended freeze until first census after 2026 (i.e., effectively until 2031 census delimitation).

Reason for freeze: prevent states that control population (by having more children) from gaining more political representation — incentivize family planning.

Delimitation of constituencies within existing seats: done by 4th Delimitation Commission (2002) based on 2001 census.

7. Key Facts

Key Facts

Delimitation = redrawing electoral constituency boundaries

Art.82: Parliament; Art.170: State Assembly

1st Commission: 1952; 2nd: 1963; 3rd: 1973; 4th: 2002

Chairman: retired SC judge

Orders: not questionable in any court

42nd Amendment 1976: freeze until 2001

84th Amendment 2001: freeze until 2026 census
