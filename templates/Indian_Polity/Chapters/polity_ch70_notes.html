Chapter 70 — National Investigation Agency (NIA)

జాతీయ దర్యాప్తు సంస్థ | Counter-Terrorism Federal Agency | 2008

0. Origin & Background

నేపథ్యం

NIA established after 26/11 Mumbai terrorist attacks (November 26, 2008).

NIA Act 2008: passed by Parliament in December 2008.

Operational from January 1, 2009.

India's first dedicated federal counter-terrorism investigation agency.

Headquarters: New Delhi.

1. Legal Framework

చట్టపరమైన నిర్మాణం

NIA Act 2008: constitutes NIA; defines scheduled offences.

NIA (Amendment) Act 2019: expanded jurisdiction to offences outside India + added new scheduled offences.

Scheduled Offences: terrorism (UAPA), explosives, nuclear, cyber-terrorism, organized crime (MCOCA), trafficking, piracy, WMD, financing of terrorism.

UAPA (Unlawful Activities Prevention Act) — primary law NIA investigates under.

2. Composition & Structure

సంఘటన

Director General (DG): head of NIA — IPS officer of DGP rank.

NIA officers have powers of police officers throughout India — pan-India jurisdiction.

Branch offices in multiple states + union territories.

Officers drawn from Central/State police services.

3. Jurisdiction & Powers

అధికార పరిధి

Pan-India jurisdiction: NIA can investigate scheduled offences anywhere in India without state government's consent.

2019 Amendment: NIA can investigate offences committed outside India against Indian citizens or interests.

Special NIA Courts: set up in states for speedy trial of NIA cases.

NIA can take over cases from state police; state must cooperate.

NIA officers: powers of police officer (arrest, search, seize) under CrPC.

4. Scheduled Offences

Scheduled Offences

UAPA offences (unlawful activities, terrorist acts, financing terrorism).

Weapons of Mass Destruction (WMD) and Delivery Systems Act.

Explosives Substances Act.

Atomic Energy Act offences.

Anti-Hijacking Act offences.

2019 Amendment added: cyber-terrorism, organized crime (MCOCA), human trafficking, counterfeit currency, maritime piracy.

5. NIA vs State Police

NIA vs State Police

NIA: central agency, pan-India; State Police: within state boundaries.

NIA can take over investigation from state police for scheduled offences.

No state government consent needed for NIA to take over cases.

State police must assist NIA when requested.

NIA cases tried in Special NIA Courts designated in states.

6. 2019 Amendment

2019 Amendment

NIA (Amendment) Act 2019: expanded powers significantly.

Extraterritorial jurisdiction: NIA can investigate offences against Indians/India's interests outside India.

New scheduled offences: cyber-terrorism, organized crime (MCOCA), human trafficking, counterfeit currency, piracy.

Special Courts can also try cases under added offences.
