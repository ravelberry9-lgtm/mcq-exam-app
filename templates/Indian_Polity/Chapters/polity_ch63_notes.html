Chapter 63 – National Commission for Women (NCW)

NCW Act 1990 • Statutory Body • Constituted January 1992

Foundation
NCW Basics
National Commission for Women Act 1990 — statutory (NOT constitutional); HQ New Delhi
Constituted: January 31, 1992; First Chairperson: Jayanti Patnaik
Under Ministry of Women and Child Development (MoWCD)
India ratified CEDAW 1993; NCW monitors compliance
Composition & Appointment
Members
1 Chairperson + 5 Members = 6; ALL must be women
Central Government appoints (NOT President — unlike NHRC, NCSC, NCST)
Members: persons with experience in women's matters (law, admin, social work)
Also: Member Secretary (Central Govt appoints — Central Civil Services officer)
No deemed members from other commissions (unlike NHRC)
Tenure & Removal
Service Conditions
Tenure: 3 years (re-appointment allowed — unlike NHRC)
Removal: Central Govt removes (NO SC inquiry needed — unlike NHRC)
Removal grounds: incapacity, proved misbehaviour, conflict of interest
Salaries: from Central Govt grants (NOT Consolidated Fund directly)
Resignation: to Central Government (not President)
Functions & Powers
Key Functions
Review constitutional/legal safeguards for women; recommend remedial legislation
Investigate specific complaints; suo motu cognizance
Civil Court powers for inquiries
Inspect jails, remand homes, detention places for women
Conciliation and Counselling Cell for dispute resolution
Annual Report → Central Govt (MoWCD) → Parliament
Recommendations advisory only (NOT binding)
Key Constitutional Provisions for Women
Articles
Art 14: Equality before law; Art 15(1): No discrimination on sex
Art 15(3): State can make special provisions for women and children
Art 39(d) DPSP: Equal pay for equal work; Art 42 DPSP: Maternity relief
Art 51A(e) FD: Renounce practices derogatory to dignity of women
73rd/74th Amendments: 1/3 women reservation in PRIs and Municipalities
106th Amendment 2023: 33% women reservation in Parliament/State Assemblies
Key Women's Legislation
Laws
Dowry Prohibition Act 1961 (+ Section 498A IPC)
Equal Remuneration Act 1976 (now Code on Wages 2019)
PWDVA 2005 — Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act
POCSO 2012 — children from sexual offences
POSH Act 2013 — Sexual Harassment at Workplace (Vishaka case 1997)
Maternity Benefit Amendment 2017 — 26 weeks leave
PCMA 2006 — Prohibition of Child Marriage Act
