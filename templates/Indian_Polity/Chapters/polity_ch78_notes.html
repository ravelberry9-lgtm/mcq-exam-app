1. Art.370 — Jammu and Kashmir (Abrogated 2019)

Art.370 Original Provisions

J&K had special autonomous status — Defence, External Affairs, Finance, Communications under Parliament; rest subject to concurrence of J&K Constituent Assembly/Legislature

J&K had its own Constitution (1956) and special privileges — permanent residents, property rights

Art.35A (Presidential Order 1954): J&K legislature could define permanent residents and their special rights

Art.370 was in Part XXI — labeled "Temporary Provisions" but remained for 70 years

Abrogation of Art.370 — August 5, 2019

August 5, 2019: Presidential Order (CO 272) — Art.370 operative provisions abrogated

Home Minister Amit Shah moved resolution in Rajya Sabha; J&K Reorganization Act 2019 passed

J&K bifurcated: J&K (State status → UT with Legislature) and Ladakh (UT without Legislature)

J&K Reorganization Act 2019: effective October 31, 2019

SC (5-judge bench, December 2023): Abrogation of Art.370 upheld as constitutionally valid; President had power; J&K did not have sovereignty

2. Art.371 — Maharashtra and Gujarat

Art.371 — Development Boards

Art.371: Special responsibility of Governor of Maharashtra and Gujarat for establishment of development boards for: Vidarbha, Marathwada, rest of Maharashtra; Saurashtra, Kutch, rest of Gujarat

Purpose: equitable distribution of funds from state budget for these regions

Ensures development of backward regions within the state

Governor has special responsibility — acts in discretion for these boards

3. Art.371A — Nagaland

Art.371A — Naga Customary Law

Art.371A: No Act of Parliament applies to Nagaland with respect to: (1) Naga religious and social practices; (2) Naga customary law and procedure; (3) Administration of civil and criminal justice involving Nagas; (4) Ownership and transfer of land and its resources

Unless Nagaland Legislative Assembly by a resolution so decides

Protects Naga customary law from parliamentary override

Governor of Nagaland has special responsibility for law and order in the state (Naga insurgency context)

Art.371B and 371C — Assam and Manipur

Art.371B (Assam): President may provide for constitution and functions of a committee of Assembly members elected from tribal areas of Assam

Art.371C (Manipur): President may provide for committee of members of State Assembly elected from hill areas of Manipur; Governor reports annually to President on administration of hill areas

Both protect tribal/hill area interests in state legislatures

4. Art.371D/E — Andhra Pradesh and Telangana

Art.371D — AP and Telangana

Art.371D: President may provide for equitable opportunities/facilities for people from different regions of AP in public employment/education

President may establish an Administrative Tribunal (APAT) for AP to decide matters related to appointment, allotment, promotion, transfer of state government employees

Art.371E: Parliament may establish a Central University in Andhra Pradesh

After bifurcation 2014: provisions apply to both AP (residual) and Telangana

Andhra Pradesh Reorganization Act 2014 created Telangana; 100th Amendment also applied Art.371D to Telangana

5. Art.371F — Sikkim

Art.371F — Sikkim's Special Provisions

Art.371F: Added by 36th Amendment 1975 when Sikkim joined India as 22nd state

Laws existing in Sikkim before merger continue to operate

Sikkim's merger with India: April 26, 1975 (after referendum under Chogyal)

Protects pre-merger laws, customs, and arrangements in Sikkim

35th Amendment 1974: Sikkim → Associate State first; 36th Amendment 1975: Full state

Art.371G — Mizoram

Art.371G: Similar to Art.371A for Nagaland — No Act of Parliament applies to Mizoram regarding Mizo religious and social practices, customary law, administration of civil/criminal justice, ownership of land unless Mizoram Assembly so decides

Added when Mizoram became a full state in 1987 (from Mizo Hills UT)

Protects Mizo customary law from parliamentary override

6. Art.371H, 371I, 371J

Art.371H — Arunachal Pradesh

Art.371H: Governor of Arunachal Pradesh has special responsibility for law and order — acts in individual judgment after consulting Council of Ministers

Governor's decision is final; not justiciable in courts

Reflects security concerns on sensitive China border

Art.371I and 371J

Art.371I (Goa): Legislature of Goa shall consist of not less than 30 members (minimum size guarantee)

Art.371J (Karnataka): Added by 98th Amendment 2013 — Hyderabad-Karnataka region (6 districts: Bidar, Kalaburagi, Yadgir, Raichur, Ballari, Koppal) gets special development provisions

Art.371J: Separate development board for Hyderabad-Karnataka region; equitable funds allocation; local reservations in education and employment in that region
