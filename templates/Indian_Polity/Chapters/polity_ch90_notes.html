9th Schedule and Basic Structure

9th Schedule was added by 1st Amendment 1951 to protect land reform laws from judicial review

Waman Rao 1981 clarified: laws added BEFORE April 24, 1973 (Kesavananda date) are immune; laws added AFTER are subject to basic structure review

IR Coelho v. State of TN 2007 (9-judge bench) confirmed: any 9th Schedule law post-1973 violating basic structure can be struck down

Bachan Singh — "Rarest of Rare" Test (1980)

Upheld constitutional validity of death penalty under S.302 IPC

Death sentence can be imposed only in the "rarest of rare" cases

Macchi Singh v. State of Punjab 1983 further elaborated the rarest of rare test with factors: manner of murder, motive, anti-social/socially abhorrent nature, magnitude, victim's personality

Hussainara Khatoon — Speedy Trial (1979)

SC directed release of undertrial prisoners detained beyond maximum sentence period

Held speedy trial is a fundamental right under Art.21

Considered India's first Public Interest Litigation (PIL) case

Section 4 — Personal Liberty & Criminal Law
ADM Jabalpur v. Shivkant Shukla (1976) — Habeas Corpus Case

Decided during Emergency: SC (4:1) held Art.21 can be suspended during Emergency → no habeas corpus maintainable

Justice H.R. Khanna dissented: even without Art.21, no one can be deprived of life without legal authority (rule of law)

Widely criticised as the SC's darkest moment; overruled in part by Puttaswamy 2017

D.K. Basu v. State of West Bengal (1997)

Laid down 11 guidelines on arrest to prevent custodial violence

Key requirements: arrest memo, inform family/friend, medical examination, right to consult lawyer, police ID tag

Violation = contempt of court; incorporated into CrPC and now BNSS 2023

Privacy & Digital Rights

Puttaswamy 2017 (9-judge bench): Privacy is a fundamental right under Art.21; overruled ADM Jabalpur on this point

Shreya Singhal 2015: Struck down S.66A IT Act — online speech restrictions must satisfy Art.19(2) tests precisely

Anuradha Bhasin 2020: Internet access protected under Art.19(1)(a)/(g); indefinite shutdowns impermissible; orders must be published

Puttaswamy 2018 (Aadhaar): Struck down S.57 — private entities cannot use Aadhaar; mandatory Aadhaar for bank/SIM struck down

Decriminalisation Cases

Navtej Singh Johar 2018 — S.377 IPC read down; consensual adult same-sex relations decriminalised (5:0)

Joseph Shine 2018 — S.497 IPC (adultery) struck down; adultery is no longer a criminal offence (5:0)

S.G. Vombatkere 2022 — S.124A (sedition) put on hold; government asked to reconsider

Religion & Personal Law Cases

Shah Bano 1985: Divorced Muslim woman entitled to maintenance under S.125 CrPC → reversed by Parliament (Muslim Women Act 1986)

Shayara Bano 2017: Instant triple talaq (talaq-e-biddat) declared unconstitutional (3:2) → Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Marriage) Act 2019

S.R. Bommai 1994: Secularism is basic structure; Art.356 subject to judicial review; Assembly in suspended animation until Parliament approves

Sabarimala 2018: Women aged 10-50 cannot be excluded from temple entry (4:1) — referred to 9-judge bench 2019

Minority Rights Cases

TMA Pai Foundation 2002 (11-judge bench): Redefined "minority" on state-wise basis; right to administer but not maladminister

PA Inamdar 2005: Government cannot impose reservation quotas on unaided minority institutions

Society for Un-Aided Private Schools v. UOI 2012: RTE Act applies to unaided non-minority schools (5:2)

Section 6 — Environment, Federalism & Governance
Environmental Law Cases

RLEK v. State of UP 1985 (Doon Valley): First SC closure of industries on environmental grounds under Art.21

M.C. Mehta v. UOI 1987 (Oleum Gas Leak): Absolute Liability rule — no exceptions for hazardous industries

Vellore Citizens Welfare Forum 1996: Polluter Pays Principle and Precautionary Principle constitutionalised

M.C. Mehta (Vehicular Pollution) 1998-2002: CNG buses in Delhi; Bharat Stage emission norms ordered

Governance & PIL

S.P. Gupta v. UOI 1981 (First Judges case): Broadened locus standi — any public-spirited citizen can file PIL

Vishaka 1997: SC issued guidelines in absence of legislation — creative judicial law-making

Lily Thomas 2013: S.8(4) RPA struck down — convicted MPs/MLAs immediately disqualified

Electoral Bonds 2024: EC bond scheme struck down — voters have right to information about political funding

Important One-Liners

Golden Triangle = Arts. 14, 19, 21 — must all be satisfied (Maneka Gandhi 1978)

Absolute Liability = M.C. Mehta 1987 — no exceptions for hazardous industry harm

ADM Jabalpur (1976) = SC's "darkest hour"; overruled by Puttaswamy 2017

First PIL = Hussainara Khatoon 1979 (speedy trial); broadened locus standi = S.P. Gupta 1981

1st Amendment 1951 = response to Champakam Dorairajan — added Art.15(4)

50% reservation ceiling — Indra Sawhney 1992 (Mandal case, 9-judge bench)

Constructive euthanasia advance directive = Common Cause 2018; Aruna Shanbaug 2011 was the first recognition
