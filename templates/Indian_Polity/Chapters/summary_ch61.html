Chapter 61 — National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC)

Historical Background
  • Kaka Kalelkar Commission 1953 (1st; Article 340)
  • Mandal Commission 1978-80; B.P. Mandal; 27% recommendation
  • V.P. Singh 1990 — implementation; anti-Mandal protests
  • Indra Sawhney 1992 (9-judge): upheld 27%, creamy layer, 50% ceiling
102nd Amendment 2018
  • Added Articles 338B (NCBC) + 342A (Central OBC List)
  • Constitutional status — parallel to NCSC (338) + NCST (338A)
  • NCBC Act 1993 repealed
  • Part XVI — NCSC + NCST + NCBC
Article 338B — NCBC
  • 5 members: Chair + VC + 3; President appoints
  • Civil Court powers; Annual Report → President → Parliament
  • Monitor safeguards; advise on OBC development
  • Consult on major OBC policy matters (Govt duty)
Article 342A — Central OBC List
  • President specifies (consults Governor); Parliament modifies
  • Mirrors Articles 341 (SC) + 342 (ST)
  • 2,600+ communities; Central services only
105th Amendment 2021
  • Maratha case 2021 → States lost OBC power under 102nd
  • 105th Amendment restored States/UTs SEBC identification power
  • Two lists: Central (342A) + State (State law)
  • Existing lists valid (saving clause)
