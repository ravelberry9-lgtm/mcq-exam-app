1. Meaning and Determinants

What is Foreign Policy?

Foreign policy is a set of goals and actions that a country pursues in its interactions with other countries. It defines the country's position on international issues and guides its diplomatic, economic, and military relationships with the rest of the world.

Determinants of India's Foreign Policy

Geography: Location in South Asia, Indian Ocean, Himalayan barrier

History: Colonial experience, Partition, Cold War geopolitics

Economy: Need for trade, investment, technology; export promotion

Security: Threats from Pakistan-sponsored terrorism, China border dispute

National interest: Resource access, diaspora welfare, nuclear security

Ideology: Nehru's idealism + realism, Non-Alignment, Panchsheel

Constitutional directive: Art.51 — promote international peace; respect international law; settlement by arbitration

Constitutional Provision — Article 51

Article 51 (DPSP): The State shall endeavour to promote international peace and security

To maintain just and honourable relations between nations

To foster respect for international law and treaty obligations

To encourage settlement of international disputes by arbitration

2. Evolution of India's Foreign Policy

Nehru Era (1947–1964)

Non-Alignment — neither the Western nor the Soviet bloc

Panchsheel (Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence) — signed with China 1954

Co-founded NAM (Non-Aligned Movement) — Bandung Conference 1955; NAM founded Belgrade 1961

India-China war 1962 — shattered Nehru's "Hindi-Chini bhai-bhai" idealism

Supported decolonisation, anti-apartheid movement

Post-Nehru Realism (1964–1991)

Indira Gandhi's pragmatic non-alignment — closer to USSR

1971 Indo-Soviet Treaty of Peace, Friendship and Cooperation

1971 War — liberation of Bangladesh; India-Pakistan war

Pokhran I — Smiling Buddha nuclear test (1974) under Indira Gandhi

Rajiv Gandhi era — technology-focused, improved US ties, mediating Sri Lanka (IPKF)

Gujral Doctrine (1996–97) — I.K. Gujral: give without expectation of reciprocity to smaller neighbours

Post-1991 Liberalisation Era

Look East Policy (1991, Narasimha Rao) — engage Southeast and East Asia; renamed "Act East" by Modi 2014

Nuclear tests Pokhran II (1998, Vajpayee) — Operation Shakti

India declared nuclear weapons state; US imposed sanctions briefly

Lahore Declaration (1999) — Vajpayee-Nawaz Sharif; India-Pakistan peace initiative; undone by Kargil war

Indo-US nuclear deal (2008, Manmohan Singh — George W. Bush) — opened India to civilian nuclear commerce

3. Non-Alignment and Panchsheel

Panchsheel — Five Principles

Panchsheel (Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence) was first incorporated in the Agreement between India and China on Tibet (April 29, 1954). The five principles are:

1. Mutual respect for each other's territorial integrity and sovereignty

2. Mutual non-aggression

3. Mutual non-interference in each other's internal affairs

4. Equality and mutual benefit

5. Peaceful coexistence

Non-Aligned Movement (NAM)

Bandung Conference (1955) — 29 Asian and African nations; groundwork for NAM

NAM formally founded at Belgrade Conference (1961) — 25 founding members

Founders: Nehru (India), Nasser (Egypt), Tito (Yugoslavia), Sukarno (Indonesia), Nkrumah (Ghana)

Current membership: 120 countries

India hosted NAM summit in New Delhi 1983 under Indira Gandhi

Relevance of Non-Alignment Today

Cold War ended 1991 — original rationale for NAM weakened

India now pursues "strategic autonomy" — maintaining independent decision-making capacity

India abstained on UNHRC resolutions against Russia (Ukraine war, 2022) — reflecting strategic autonomy

QUAD (India-US-Japan-Australia) — critics say India moved away from NAM; India calls it "strategic partnership"

4. India's Relations with Neighbours

India-Pakistan

Three wars: 1947–48 (Kashmir), 1965 (Rann of Kutch/Kashmir), 1971 (Bangladesh); Kargil 1999

Simla Agreement (1972) — bilateral settlement of disputes; Indira Gandhi-Zulfiqar Ali Bhutto

Lahore Declaration (1999) — Vajpayee-Sharif; peace initiative

Agra Summit (2001) — Vajpayee-Musharraf; collapsed over Kashmir

Cross-border terrorism — main stumbling block; Mumbai 26/11 (2008), Pulwama (2019)

SAARC stalled — India boycotted 2016 summit (after Pathankot/Uri attacks)

India-China

1962 India-China War — Himalayan border (McMahon Line, Aksai Chin); China's forward policy

LOAC (Line of Actual Control) — undefined border; disputed Aksai Chin + Arunachal Pradesh

Doklam standoff (2017) — Bhutan-China-India tri-junction; 73-day standoff

Galwan Valley clash (June 15, 2020) — first deaths on LAC since 1975; 20 Indian soldiers killed

Trade: China is India's largest trading partner (despite tensions); $100+ billion bilateral trade

BRI (Belt and Road Initiative) — India refuses to join; CPEC passes through Pakistan-Occupied Kashmir

India's Neighbourhood Policy — SAARC and Beyond

SAARC (South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation) — founded 1985, HQ Kathmandu, 8 members

BIMSTEC (Bay of Bengal Initiative for Multi-Sectoral Technical and Economic Cooperation) — India's alternative to SAARC

Neighbourhood First Policy — Modi government's priority for South Asian neighbours

India-Nepal: open border, special ties; tensions over Kalapani border

India-Sri Lanka: Tamil issue, fishermen disputes; IPKF episode (1987–90)

India-Bangladesh: Land Boundary Agreement 2015, Teesta water dispute

India-Bhutan: Special relationship, security umbrella

India-Maldives: "India Out" campaign; strategic Indian Ocean importance

5. India's Major Power Relations

India-USA

Cold War: Strained — US supported Pakistan; India closer to USSR

Post-Cold War improvement: NSSP (Next Steps in Strategic Partnership) 2004

Indo-US Civil Nuclear Deal (2008) — 123 Agreement; ended India's nuclear isolation

GSOMIA (General Security of Military Information Agreement), LEMOA, COMCASA, BECA — defence pacts signed 2016–20

QUAD (Quadrilateral Security Dialogue) — India, US, Japan, Australia; revived 2017, elevated to leader-level 2021

I2U2 (India-Israel-UAE-USA) — new minilateral; West Asia focus

India-Russia

Special relationship — Indo-Soviet Treaty 1971 (supported India in 1971 war)

Major arms supplier: T-90 tanks, Su-30 MKI fighters, BrahMos missiles (joint venture)

S-400 missile defence system — India purchased despite US CAATSA sanctions threats

Oil imports — India significantly increased Russian oil imports after Ukraine war sanctions (2022)

India-Russia annual summit mechanism; special and privileged strategic partnership

India-China

Panchsheel (1954) — foundational framework; "Hindi-Chini Bhai Bhai"

1962 war — reset relationship negatively for decades

Normalisation under Rajiv Gandhi 1988; full ambassadorial relations restored

Competitor in Asia, largest trading partner, border adversary — complex relationship

India refused to sign BRI; supports QUAD as balance to China

6. India's Multilateral Engagements

UN and Global Bodies

India is a founding member of the United Nations (1945)

UNSC Permanent Seat campaign — India seeks P5 membership; G4 (India, Brazil, Germany, Japan)

India has contributed the largest number of troops to UN Peacekeeping Missions among all nations

International Solar Alliance (ISA) — India-France initiative, 2015 COP21; HQ Gurugram; 120+ member nations

Coalition for Disaster Resilient Infrastructure (CDRI) — India launched 2019 at UNGA

BRICS, G20, SCO

BRICS (Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa) — economic grouping; India co-founder

G20: India is a founding member of G20; India held G20 Presidency 2022–23 and hosted G20 Summit in New Delhi September 2023

SCO (Shanghai Cooperation Organisation) — India became full member in 2017 (Astana summit)

Commonwealth of Nations — India a member; periodic summits (CHOGM)

India's Nuclear Policy

No First Use (NFU) — India declared NFU policy after Pokhran II (1998)

Credible Minimum Deterrence — India maintains minimum necessary nuclear arsenal

India has NOT signed NPT (Non-Proliferation Treaty) or CTBT (Comprehensive Test Ban Treaty)

India signed the Indo-US 123 Agreement (2008) which allows civilian nuclear commerce

MTCR (Missile Technology Control Regime) — India joined 2016

NSG (Nuclear Suppliers Group) — India seeking membership; China blocking

7. Post-Cold War Shifts

Major Shifts After 1991

LPG reforms — economic interests drove foreign policy (trade, investment, technology)

US-India relationship transformed: from adversarial to strategic partnership

Look East / Act East Policy — engagement with ASEAN, Japan, South Korea

Nuclear deal 2008 — ended India's nuclear isolation

QUAD revival (2017) — alignment with US, Japan, Australia on Indo-Pacific

Modi Era Foreign Policy (2014–present)

Neighbourhood First — inaugurated all SAARC leaders at swearing-in 2014

Act East Policy — upgraded Look East

Indo-Pacific vision — SAGAR (Security and Growth for All in the Region)

Yoga Diplomacy — June 21 as International Day of Yoga (UNGA adopted 2014)

Diaspora outreach — Pravasi Bharatiya Divas, Jan 9 (day Mahatma Gandhi returned from South Africa 1915)

G20 Presidency 2022–23 — "Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam" (One Earth One Family One Future)

Voice of the Global South Summit 2023 — India positioned as leader of developing world
