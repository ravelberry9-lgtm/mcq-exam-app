Chapter 39 — సహకార సంఘాలు / Co-operative Societies

97th Amendment 2011 | Art.43B (DPSP) | Part IXB Art.243ZH–243ZT | Multi-State Co-ops

Section 1 — 97th Amendment: History
97th Constitutional Amendment Act 2011: Effective February 15, 2012. Gave constitutional status to co-operative societies.
Three changes: (1) Art.19(1)(c) amended — right to form co-operatives; (2) Art.43B added (DPSP); (3) Part IXB (Art.243ZH–243ZT) inserted.
SC 2021 (Rajendra N. Shah v. UOI): Part IXB applies only to Multi-State Co-operative Societies; State co-ops = State subject (Entry 32 State List).
Section 2 — Art.19(1)(c) & Art.43B
Art.19(1)(c) amended: Added "co-operative societies" to FR to form associations.
Art.43B (DPSP): State shall endeavour to promote voluntary formation, autonomous functioning, democratic control and professional management of co-operative societies.
Section 3 — Part IXB Provisions (Art.243ZH–ZT)
Art.243ZJ — Board Size: Not more than 21 directors + one seat SC/ST + one seat women.
Art.243ZL — Supersession: Max 6 months (up to 1 year total). Elections within 6 months of supersession.
Art.243ZM — Audit: At least once a year.
Art.243ZN — AGM: Annual General Body Meeting mandatory each year.
Art.243ZO — Member Rights: Right to inspect books, accounts, records.
Art.243ZP — Returns: Annual report, audited accounts, list of members filed with authority.
Art.243ZR: Part IXB applies to Multi-State Co-operatives (Parliament law).
Art.243ZS: Existing State co-op laws must be brought in conformity within 1 year.
Section 4 — Key Cases & Facts
Entry 32, State List: Co-operative societies = State subject. Single-State co-ops fully under State authority.
Gujarat HC 2013: Part IXB unconstitutional as it encroaches on Entry 32 State List.
SC 2021: Upheld — Part IXB valid ONLY for multi-state co-ops.
NAFED, IFFCO, KRIBHCO: Examples of Multi-State Co-operative Societies.
India's First Co-op: Anyonya Co-operative Bank, Baroda (1889).
Ministry of Co-operation: Separate Ministry created July 2021 (Amit Shah = first minister).
Three-tier credit structure: Primary societies → District Central Co-op Banks → State Co-op Banks.
