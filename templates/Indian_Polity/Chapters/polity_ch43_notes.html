Ch43 — పార్టీ ఫిరాయింపు నిరోధక చట్టం / Anti-Defection Law

52nd Amendment 1985 | 10th Schedule | Art.102(2) & Art.191(2) | Kihoto Hollohan 1992

Background & 52nd Amendment 1985
"Aaya Ram Gaya Ram": Gaya Lal (Haryana MLA, 1967) changed party 3 times in ONE day — coined the phrase
52nd Amendment 1985: Added 10th Schedule; amended Art.101, 102, 190, 191; effective March 1, 1985
91st Amendment 2003: Deleted the split (1/3) exception (Para 3); capped CoM size at 15%
Constitutional basis: Art.102(2) — Parliament; Art.191(2) — State Legislature
10th Schedule — Structure
Para 2: Grounds of disqualification (main provision)
Para 3: Originally — Split (1/3 members); DELETED by 91st Amendment 2003
Para 4: Exception for merger (2/3 members)
Para 5: Exemption for Speaker/Chairman (presiding officers)
Para 6: Decision by Speaker/Chairman — subject to judicial review (Kihoto Hollohan)
Para 7: Rules by Speaker — struck down in Kihoto Hollohan (needed State ratification)
Grounds of Disqualification — Para 2
Ground 1: Voluntarily gives up membership of the party on whose ticket elected
Ground 2: Votes or abstains contrary to party whip without prior permission
Ground 3: Independent member joins a party after election
Nominated members: Joining a party after 6 months of taking seat → disqualified
"Voluntarily giving up": Can be inferred from conduct even without formal resignation (Ravi Naik 1994)
Exceptions to Disqualification
Merger (Para 4): Not disqualified if at least 2/3 of legislature party members agree to merge
91st Amendment 2003: Split (1/3) exception deleted — ONLY merger (2/3) protects now
Speaker/Deputy Speaker (Para 5): May resign from party; can rejoin after ceasing to be presiding officer
Cross-voting in President/VP elections: NOT covered by 10th Schedule — free voting allowed

91st Amendment 2003 — Key Changes
Deleted Para 3: Split exception (1/3) completely removed
CoM size capped: Art.75(1A) — Central CoM ≤ 15% of Lok Sabha strength; Art.164(1A) — State CoM ≤ 15% of Assembly (min 12)
Disqualified member: Art.75(1B) and Art.164(1B) — disqualified member barred from ministerial office
