Chapter 72 — Bar Council of India

భారత బార్ కౌన్సిల్ | Advocates Act 1961 | Statutory Professional Body

0. Origin & Legal Framework

నేపథ్యం

Bar Council of India (BCI): established under Advocates Act 1961.

Before 1961: Legal Practitioners Act 1879 — various classes of legal practitioners.

Advocates Act 1961 unified the legal profession — single class: Advocate.

BCI: statutory body (not constitutional); HQ: New Delhi.

1. Composition

సంఘటన

Chairman: elected by BCI members.

Members: one member elected by each State Bar Council + Attorney General of India (ex-officio) + Solicitor General of India (ex-officio).

Total: State Bar Council representatives + 2 ex-officio members (AG + SG).

Term: 5 years for elected members.

2. State Bar Councils

State Bar Councils

State Bar Councils: one for each state (and some High Courts).

SC/HC (All India) Bar Association: affiliated with BCI.

Every advocate must be enrolled with a State Bar Council — cannot practice without enrollment.

State Bar Councils elect members to BCI.

3. Powers & Functions of BCI

విధులు

Lay down standards of professional conduct and etiquette for advocates.

Prescribe standards for legal education.

Grant recognition to law universities/colleges.

Conduct All India Bar Examination (AIBE) — qualifying exam for advocates to practice.

Entertain and determine disciplinary cases against advocates.

Safeguard rights and interests of advocates.

Manage and invest funds of Bar Council.

4. Disciplinary Jurisdiction

Disciplinary Jurisdiction

Disciplinary Committee of State Bar Council: hears complaints against advocates first.

BCI Disciplinary Committee: appellate authority — hears appeals from State Bar Council decisions.

SC: final appeal from BCI disciplinary orders.

Punishments: censure, suspension, removal from roll.

5. All India Bar Examination (AIBE)

AIBE

AIBE: qualifying examination for newly enrolled advocates before they can practice.

Introduced by BCI to ensure minimum competency standards.

SC upheld AIBE's constitutionality (V. Sudeer case 2009 and subsequent SC cases).

AIBE conducted by BCI — tests legal knowledge and practical application.

6. Legal Education

Legal Education

BCI: prescribes standards for legal education — duration, curriculum, examination.

BCI gives approval/recognition to law colleges/universities.

5-year integrated LLB programme and 3-year LLB programme — BCI regulates.

BCI can withdraw recognition from law colleges not meeting standards.

NLUs (National Law Universities) — modern law schools also regulated by BCI.

7. Key Facts

Key Facts

BCI established by: Advocates Act 1961

BCI HQ: New Delhi

Ex-officio members: Attorney General + Solicitor General

Elected member term: 5 years

AIBE: qualifying exam for new advocates

Disciplinary appeal: State Bar Council → BCI → SC

BCI regulates legal education in India

Nature: Statutory body (not constitutional)
