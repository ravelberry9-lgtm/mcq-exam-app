Voting Behaviour (Ch83)
Key theories: Michigan Model (party identification), Columbia Model (social determinism — Lazarsfeld), Rational Choice (Anthony Downs). In India, caste is the most significant determinant (Rajni Kothari: "caste is politicised, politics is casteised"). Post-Mandal (1990), OBC identity became politically decisive. Religion: Hindu vote is fragmented by caste; Muslim vote is tactical. Regional identity drives state-level outcomes. Welfare schemes, anti-incumbency, youth vote, social media (WhatsApp), and leadership charisma are modern factors. Lokniti-CSDS conducts National Election Studies. Psephology coined by R.B. McCallum (1952).
