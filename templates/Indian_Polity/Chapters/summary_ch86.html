Pressure Groups (Ch86)
Pressure groups are organised groups that seek to influence government policy without contesting elections. Pioneer scholar: Arthur Bentley (1908). Key types: sectional (trade unions, industry bodies) and promotional (cause-based — environmental, human rights). Major Indian groups: FICCI (1927), CII (industry); INTUC (Congress), BMS (RSS/BJP) trade unions; BKU (farmers); PUCL, MKSS, Anna Hazare (civil society). Methods: lobbying, PILs, protests, strikes. Successes: RTI 2005, Lokpal 2013, NOTA 2013, Farm Laws Repeal 2021. Merits: articulate interests, check government. Demerits: sectional bias, unaccountable, rich groups dominate.
