Ch44 Quick Summary — జాతీయ మానవ హక్కుల కమిషన్ / NHRC

PHRA 1993 | Statutory Body | Est. October 12, 1993 | Paris Principles

Background & Nature
PHRA 1993: Protection of Human Rights Act — established NHRC (statutory, NOT constitutional)
Established: October 12, 1993; HQ — Manav Adhikar Bhavan, New Delhi
Paris Principles (1991/1993): NHRIs must be independent, pluralistic, have broad mandate, adequate resources
Accreditation: "A" status from GANHRI (Global Alliance of NHRIs)
Human Rights (PHRA): Rights relating to life, liberty, equality, dignity — guaranteed by Constitution or international covenants (ICCPR + ICESCR)
Composition
Chairperson: Retired CJI or retired SC judge (post-2019 amendment)
Members: Up to 5 (2 retired SC judges + up to 3 human rights/women/minority/children experts)
Ex-officio (7): Chairpersons of NCSC, NCST, NCBC, NCPCR, NCW, NCM, NCMEI
Appointment: President on recommendation of 6-member committee (PM chairs)
Committee: PM + Speaker LS + Home Minister + Leader of Opposition (LS) + Leader of Opposition (RS) + Deputy Chairman RS
Tenure, Removal & Conditions
Tenure: 3 years OR age 70 (whichever earlier); re-appointment once
Removal: By President after SC inquiry — on grounds of misbehaviour/incapacity
Salary: Charged on CFI (Consolidated Fund of India)
Post-retirement bar: Cannot hold further Central or State Government employment
First Chairperson: Justice Ranganath Misra (1993–96)
Powers & Jurisdiction
Inquiry powers: Civil Court powers — summon, receive evidence, examine witnesses on oath, compel documents
Visit: Any institution under Central/State Government (prisons, detention centres)
Armed Forces (Section 19): Can ONLY seek report from Central Government; cannot directly investigate
Time bar: Cannot inquire into complaints older than 1 year (except exceptional circumstances)
Recommendations: Advisory ONLY — NOT binding on government
Can approach SC/HC: For enforcement of its recommendations
Intervene: In any HC proceedings involving human rights
State Human Rights Commissions (SHRCs)
Establishment: Optional (not mandatory) under PHRA Section 21
Chairperson: Retired HC Chief Justice
Member: One retired HC judge
Appointment: By Governor (committee: CM + Speaker + Home Minister + Leader of Opposition)
Jurisdiction: State List subjects ONLY; Central List → NHRC
Transfer: NHRC → SHRC (allowed); SHRC → NHRC (NOT allowed)
Report: Annual report to Governor → State Legislature
Limitations
"Toothless tiger": Recommendations not binding
Armed forces: Cannot directly investigate AFSPA-related violations
1-year bar: Cannot inquire into complaints older than 1 year
Private actors: No jurisdiction over private parties/non-state actors
Pending cases: Cannot investigate if case pending before SHRC or other commissions
