Chapter 36 — అటార్నీ జనరల్ & అడ్వొకేట్ జనరల్ / Attorney General & Advocate General

Art.76 | Art.165 | Government's Legal Advisor | Part V & Part VI

Section 1 — Art.76: Attorney General of India
Art.76(1): President = Attorney General నియమిస్తారు. Qualification = SC judge అర్హత ఉండాలి (HC judge గా 5 years + Advocate 10 years).
Art.76(2): AG = duty: Union Government కి legal advice; President assigned legal duties perform; President/Parliament కి appear.
Art.76(3): AG = all courts in India లో audience అధికారం ఉంది (SC + all HCs + all courts).
Art.76(4): AG = remuneration President determines. Fixed not in Constitution.
AG = India's first law officer. Government lawyer, not judge. Part V (The Union), Chapter I.
Section 2 — AG Functions, Rights & Limitations
Functions: (1) Legal advice to Union government; (2) SC + other courts లో Union appear చేయడం; (3) President-referred matters; (4) Constitutional cases; (5) Defend government policies.
Rights: AG = all courts audience right (Art.76(3)). Parliament proceedings లో participate (both Houses, committees) — without voting right. SC/HC bars లో priority seating.
Limitations: (1) Government against cases accept చేయలేరు; (2) Accused defend చేయలేరు unless President permission; (3) Company director కాలేరు; (4) Full-time government employee కాదు — private practice allowed.
Private practice: AG = private practice possible (unlike government servants). But government-related conflicts avoid. AG = part-time law officer.
Section 3 — AG Status, Salary & Removal
Status: AG = NOT a member of Cabinet. NOT a civil servant. High constitutional office — first law officer of India.
Salary: President determines (Art.76(4)). NOT charged to CFI — paid from Union Budget (voted). Unlike CAG/CEC/UPSC.
Removal: President pleasure (Art.76 — no security of tenure). AG holds office during President's pleasure — can be removed anytime without Parliament procedure. No fixed tenure.
Convention: AG typically resigns when new government takes office (like Cabinet). Convention, not constitutional requirement.
Committees: AG can participate in Parliament joint committees, Select committees — advice purpose.
Section 4 — Solicitor General & Additional SGs
Solicitor General: Second law officer of India. NOT a constitutional position — statutory/convention based. Assists AG in SC.
Additional Solicitor General (ASG): Multiple ASGs — appear for Union in SC + HCs. Constitution లో mention లేదు.
Hierarchy: AG → Solicitor General → Additional Solicitor Generals. All = Union government legal officers.
State level: State has Advocate General (Art.165) + Government Pleader + Additional Government Pleader. AG of India + State AG are different.
Section 5 — Art.165: Advocate General of State
Art.165(1): Governor = Advocate General నియమిస్తారు. Qualification = HC judge అర్హత ఉండాలి.
Art.165(2): AG = State government legal advice; Governor assigned duties; all courts in State లో audience.
Art.165(3): Remuneration = Governor determines.
Removal: Governor pleasure — no security of tenure. Convention = resigns when new State government forms.
State Legislature: Advocate General = State Legislature proceedings లో participate చేయవచ్చు (without vote). Art.177 కింద.
Section 6 — AG vs Advocate General Comparison
Appointment: AG = President; Advocate General = Governor.
Qualification: AG = SC judge qualified (Art.76(1)); Advocate General = HC judge qualified (Art.165(1)). AG higher qualification.
Jurisdiction: AG = all courts India wide (Art.76(3)); Advocate General = all courts in State (Art.165(2)).
Salary from: AG = Union Budget (voted); Advocate General = State Budget. Neither from CFI/CSF.
Removal: Both = respective executive's pleasure. No security of tenure. No Parliament/Legislature address needed.
Parliament/Legislature: AG = Parliament (both Houses) participate; Advocate General = State Legislature participate. Art.88 (AG in Parliament), Art.177 (Advocate General in Legislature).
Section 7 — AG vs CAG vs CEC: Status Comparison
AG: No fixed tenure; President's pleasure; salary from voted grants; no post-retirement bar; private practice allowed; Parliament attend (no vote).
CAG: Fixed tenure 6 yrs/65 yrs; SC judge removal procedure; salary from CFI; post-retirement bar; no private practice.
CEC: Fixed tenure 6 yrs/65 yrs; SC judge removal; salary from CFI; no re-appointment.
Key difference: AG = NOT independent constitutional authority — dependent on government goodwill. CAG + CEC = strongly independent.
Section 8 — Important Facts & Notable AGs
First AG of India: M.C. Setalvad (1950–1963) — longest serving AG.
C.K. Daphtary: 2nd AG (1963–1968).
Niren De: AG during Emergency (1972–1977) — Emergency justification arguments.
Soli Sorabjee: AG twice (1989-90, 1998-2004) — human rights advocate.
Mukul Rohatgi: AG (2014–2017); R. Venkataramani (2022–present).
AG Parliament: AG has right to speak in both Houses + committees — but no right to vote (Art.88). Not an MP.
