Chapter 39 — సహకార సంఘాలు / Co-operative Societies

97th Amendment 2011 | Art.43B (DPSP) | Part IXB Art.243ZH–243ZT | Multi-State Co-ops

Section 1 — 97th Amendment: History
97th Constitutional Amendment Act 2011: Effective February 15, 2012. Gave constitutional status to co-operative societies.
Three changes: (1) Art.19(1)(c) amended — right to form co-operatives; (2) Art.43B added (DPSP); (3) Part IXB (Art.243ZH–243ZT) inserted.
Gujarat HC 2013: Struck down Part IXB as unconstitutional (violated State subjects — Entry 32, List II). SC upheld Part IXB for multi-state co-ops only.
SC 2021 (Rajendra N. Shah v. UOI): Part IXB applies only to Multi-State Co-operative Societies; State co-ops = State subject (Entry 32 State List).
Section 2 — Art.19(1)(c) & Art.43B
Art.19(1)(c) amended: Added "co-operative societies" to Fundamental Right to form associations. Now reads: "to form associations or unions or co-operative societies".
Art.43B (DPSP): "The State shall endeavour to promote voluntary formation, autonomous functioning, democratic control and professional management of co-operative societies." New DPSP added by 97th Amendment.
Significance: Art.19(1)(c) = Fundamental Right (enforceable) vs Art.43B = DPSP (non-enforceable directive). Both added simultaneously by 97th Amendment.
Section 3 — Part IXB Definitions (Art.243ZH)
Part IXB: Art.243ZH to Art.243ZT — Co-operative Societies chapter.
Co-operative Society: Registered under State co-operative society law or Multi-State Co-operative Societies Act 2002.
Multi-State Co-operative Society: Has operations in more than one State — governed by Parliament law.
Board: Board of directors or governing body of a co-operative society.
Member: Includes nominal/associate members as per State law.
Section 4 — Art.243ZI-ZK: Incorporation, Board, Elections
Art.243ZI: Incorporation, regulation, winding up of co-operative societies = as per State law.
Art.243ZJ — Board Size: State law to ensure board has not more than 21 directors (including functional directors + one seat each SC/ST + one seat women).
Art.243ZK — Elections: Elections to board = before expiry of term. Superintendence/direction/control of elections = authority provided by State law (similar to SEC concept).
Functional directors: May be co-opted — State law decides (not counted in 21 limit in some interpretations).
Section 5 — Art.243ZL-ZM: Supersession, Audit & Meetings
Art.243ZL — Supersession: Board may be superseded/suspended for not more than 6 months (extended to max 1 year total in exceptional circumstances). After supersession, elections within 6 months.
Art.243ZM — Audit: State Legislature shall by law make provisions for maintenance of accounts and audit — at least once a year. Auditor = qualified as per State law.
Art.243ZN — Annual General Body Meeting: Co-operative society must convene one general body meeting each year; agenda includes annual accounts, audit report, election plan.
Section 6 — Art.243ZO-ZT: Rights & Application
Art.243ZO — Member Rights: Every member has right to get information (inspection of books, accounts, records). Cannot be denied. Promotes transparency.
Art.243ZP — Returns: Co-operative societies must file returns (annual report, audited accounts, list of members, minutes of general body) to authority specified by State law.
Art.243ZQ — Offences: State Legislature provides for penalties for contravention of provisions (board members, officers).
Art.243ZR — Multi-State Application: Part IXB applies to Multi-State Co-operatives (Parliament law — Multi-State Co-operative Societies Act 2002).
Art.243ZS — Existing Law: All existing State co-operative laws continue; must be brought in conformity within 1 year of 97th Amendment.
Art.243ZT — Saving: Provisions regarding elections in existing co-op societies remain valid until expiry of their term.
Section 7 — Multi-State Co-ops & Key Cases
Multi-State Co-operative Societies Act 2002: Parliament law for co-ops operating in more than one State. Central Registrar = authority.
Entry 32, State List: "Co-operative societies" = State subject. Single-State co-ops fully under State authority.
Entry 44, Union List: "Corporations other than those specified in List III" — Parliament jurisdiction over multi-state corps.
Gujarat HC 2013 (Rajendra N. Shah): Part IXB unconstitutional as it encroaches on State List Entry 32 — required ratification by at least half the State legislatures (like Art.368(2) proviso).
SC 2021 (Rajendra N. Shah): UPHELD Gujarat HC — Part IXB NOT applicable to State co-operatives (State subject). Part IXB valid ONLY for multi-state co-ops.
NAFED, IFFCO, KRIBHCO: Examples of Multi-State Co-operative Societies.
Section 8 — Key Comparative & Quick Facts
Co-op vs Company: Co-op = member-owned, one member one vote; Company = shareholder, voting proportional to shares.
Rochdale Principles (1844 UK): Origin of modern co-operative movement — voluntary, democratic, member benefit.
India's First Co-op: Anyonya Co-operative Bank, Baroda (1889).
Co-op Credit Societies Act 1904: First legislation for co-operatives in India.
NABARD: National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development — apex body for rural co-op credit.
Three-tier structure: Primary societies (village) → District Central Co-op Banks → State Co-op Banks.
ICA: International Co-operative Alliance — world body. India member.
Ministry of Co-operation: Separate Ministry created July 2021 (Amit Shah — first MoC).
