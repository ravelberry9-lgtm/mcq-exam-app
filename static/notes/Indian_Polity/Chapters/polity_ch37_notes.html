Chapter 37 — పంచాయతీ రాజ్ / Panchayati Raj

73rd Amendment 1992 | Art.243–243O | Part IX | 11th Schedule | Three-tier

Section 1 — History & 73rd Amendment
Balwant Rai Mehta Committee (1957): Three-tier Panchayati Raj recommend చేసింది — Gram Panchayat, Panchayat Samiti, Zila Parishad. Rajasthan = first state to implement (Oct 2, 1959, Nagaur).
Ashok Mehta Committee (1977-78): Two-tier system recommend — District Parishad + Mandal Panchayat. Politicization of Panchayats recommend చేసింది.
L.M. Singhvi Committee (1986): Constitutional status to Panchayats recommend చేసింది. 'Grama Raj' concept. Gram Sabha = essential.
73rd Constitutional Amendment Act 1992: April 24, 1993 నుండి effective (National Panchayati Raj Day). Part IX (Art.243-243O) + 11th Schedule add చేసింది.
April 24 = National Panchayati Raj Day (73rd Amendment effective date).
Section 2 — Art.243: Definitions & Structure
Part IX: Art.243 to Art.243O = Panchayats. Constitution లో add చేసింది 73rd Amendment.
Art.243(d): 'Panchayat' = rural areas లో self-government institutions (Art.243B).
Art.243(e): 'Panchayat Area' = State law specify.
Three tiers: Village level (Gram Panchayat), Intermediate level (Block/Taluka), District level (Zila Parishad). States with population < 20 lakh = intermediate level optional.
Art.243B: Constitution of Panchayats — every State shall constitute Panchayats (compulsory).
Section 3 — Art.243A-C: Gram Sabha & Composition
Art.243A (Gram Sabha): Village level body = all registered voters. Powers + functions = State law determines. Constitutional recognition first time.
Art.243B: Constitution of Panchayats — mandatory three-tier (or two-tier for smaller states).
Art.243C: Composition = State law. Direct elections to all levels. Seats total = State Legislature.
Chairpersons: Village Panchayat = Art.243C; Intermediate + District = State law election method. Chairperson election method = State law.
Section 4 — Art.243D: Reservation of Seats
SC/ST reservation: Not less than their proportion to total population in each Panchayat (at all three levels).
Women reservation: Not less than 1/3 (one-third) of all seats (including SC/ST reserved seats). Many States = 50% (State laws).
Chairpersons reservation: SC/ST + Women chairpersons = proportional reservation. State law determines.
Rotation: Reserved seats = different constituencies rotation — State law. OBC = State discretion (not mandatory in Art.243D).
Section 5 — Art.243E-F: Duration & Disqualifications
Art.243E: Panchayat duration = 5 years from first sitting. Dissolution before time → within 6 months election compulsory. New Panchayat = only remaining period (not fresh 5 years).
Art.243F: Disqualifications = State law. Minimum age = 21 years (for Panchayat membership) — not 18. State law additional disqualifications.
Art.243E(4): State Legislature dissolved or superseded → elections within 6 months (constitutional requirement).
Section 6 — Art.243G-I: Powers, Finance, State FC
Art.243G: State law = Panchayats powers + functions. 11th Schedule = 29 subjects (not automatically devolved — State law needed).
Art.243H: Panchayats financial powers — State law = taxes, duties, fees, tolls levy; grants from State government.
Art.243I: State Finance Commission = Governor constitutes every 5 years. Parallel to Union Finance Commission. Reviews financial position of Panchayats + Municipalities.
11th Schedule (Art.243G): 29 subjects including Agriculture, Land improvement, Minor irrigation, Animal husbandry, Fisheries, Education (primary+secondary), Health, Roads, Drinking water.
Section 7 — Art.243J-O: Audit, Elections, Bars
Art.243J: Accounts audit = State law (State Auditor General / State Audit Department, NOT CAG directly).
Art.243K: State Election Commission (SEC) = Governor appoints SEC. SEC = Panchayat elections superintendence, direction, control. (SEC = not CAG, not ECI).
Art.243L: Application to UTs = President may specify (with modifications).
Art.243M: NOT applicable to: (1) Scheduled areas (5th Schedule); (2) Tribal areas (6th Schedule — Northeast); (3) Nagaland, Meghalaya, Mizoram; (4) Manipur Hill areas; (5) Darjeeling (West Bengal) hill areas.
Art.243N: Continuance of existing laws — State laws inconsistent → continue for 1 year or until amended.
Art.243O: Bar on courts interference in Panchayat elections (like Art.329 for Parliament).
Section 8 — Committees, 11th Schedule & Key Facts
11th Schedule: 29 subjects (added by 73rd Amendment). NOT automatically devolved — State law required. Art.243G reference.
Key committees: Balwant Rai Mehta (1957) → 3-tier; Ashok Mehta (1977) → 2-tier; L.M. Singhvi (1986) → Constitutional status; P.K. Thungan (1989) → Constitutional amendment.
Rajasthan first: First state to implement Panchayati Raj (Oct 2, 1959, Nagaur). Jawaharlal Nehru inaugurated.
Exceptions (Art.243M): Scheduled Areas (5th Schedule) + Tribal Areas (6th Schedule) + Nagaland + Meghalaya + Mizoram + Manipur Hill areas + Darjeeling.
PESA Act 1996: Extension of Panchayats to Scheduled Areas. Parliament passed under Art.243M(4). Special provisions for tribal self-governance.
