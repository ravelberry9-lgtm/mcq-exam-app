Art.338 (NCSC) | Art.338A (NCST) | Art.338B (NCBC) | Part XVI

Three National Commissions — Overview
NCSC: Art.338 | Created by 89th Amendment 2003 | Replaces combined SC&ST commission (65th Amend 1990)
NCST: Art.338A | Created by 89th Amendment 2003 | Split from NCSC
NCBC: Art.338B | Constitutional status by 102nd Amendment 2018 | Earlier statutory (NCBC Act 1993)
All in: Part XVI — Special Provisions Relating to Certain Classes
Composition — All Three (Identical)
Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 other Members = 5 total
All appointed by President (warrant under hand and seal)
Conditions of service = determined by President by rule
Salaries = Charged on CFI (not voted)
Removal = by President (no SC judge equivalent protection)
No post-retirement bar; no fixed constitutional term
Functions — All Three (Similar)
Investigate complaints of safeguard violations
Inquire into specific complaints
Advise Central/State governments on planning
Recommend socio-economic development measures
Evaluate progress under Constitution/laws
Present annual + special reports to President
Civil Court Powers: Summon + documents + affidavits + public records (while investigating)
Recommendations = Advisory, NOT binding
Reports & Accountability
Annual Report: Commission → President → Parliament + memorandum of action taken
State matters: Copy → Governor → State Legislature + action memorandum
Special Reports: At any time (for urgent matters)
Presidential Reference: President may refer any matter to respective commission
Mandatory consultation (NCBC — Art.338B(9)): Centre+States SHALL consult NCBC on all major OBC policy matters
Constitutional Amendments Timeline
Original Art.338: Single Special Officer (Commissioner) for SC+ST (1950)
65th Amendment 1990: Multi-member National Commission for SC+ST
89th Amendment 2003: Split → NCSC (Art.338) + NCST (Art.338A)
NCBC Act 1993: Statutory NCBC (after Indra Sawhney 1992)
102nd Amendment 2018: Art.338B (NCBC constitutional) + Art.342A (OBC list)
104th Amendment 2020: Anglo-Indians removed from NCSC mandate
105th Amendment 2021: States' OBC list power restored (Art.342A(3))
OBC List Framework
Art.342A (102nd Amendment): President specifies Central OBC list; Parliament includes/excludes
Maratha case 2021: SC ruled States lost power to add OBC groups
105th Amendment 2021: States can maintain own State OBC lists (Art.342A(3))
Mirrors: Art.341 (SC list) + Art.342 (ST list) — same Presidential specification mechanism
Key Cases
Indra Sawhney v. UOI 1992: 27% OBC + non-creamy layer + 50% cap + permanent OBC commission → NCBC Act 1993
Maratha Reservation 2021: States cannot add to Central OBC list under Art.342A → 105th Amendment corrected
Art.340: President can appoint ad hoc backward classes commission (Kaka Kalelkar 1953, Mandal 1979)
Quick-Revision Pills
