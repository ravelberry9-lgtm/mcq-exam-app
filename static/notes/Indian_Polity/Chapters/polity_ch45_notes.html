NCW | NCM | NCBC | NCPCR | NCMEI — All Statutory Bodies

National Commission for Women (NCW)
Act: National Commission for Women Act, 1990
Established: January 31, 1992
Nature: Statutory body; NOT constitutional
Composition: Chairperson + 5 members (at least one from SC and at least one from ST — separately mandatory per proviso) + Member Secretary
Appointment: By Central Government
Tenure: 3 years; no age limit specified in Act
Functions: Review constitutional/legal safeguards for women; recommend remedial measures; examine complaints; take suo motu notice of violations
Powers: Civil Court powers (summon, evidence, documents); can forward cases to Magistrate for trial
Annual Report: To Central Government (not directly to President/Parliament)
First Chairperson: Jayanti Patnaik (1992)
National Commission for Minorities (NCM)
Act: National Commission for Minorities Act, 1992
Established: 1993
Nature: Statutory body; NOT constitutional
Composition: Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 5 members = 7 total; all from minority communities
Appointment: By Central Government
Tenure: 3 years; eligible for one more term
Minorities notified: Muslims, Christians, Sikhs, Buddhists, Zoroastrians (Parsis), Jains (added 2014)
Functions: Evaluate progress of minority development; monitor constitutional/legal safeguards; recommend measures; investigate complaints; review minority list
Powers: Civil Court powers during inquiry
Report: Annual report to Central Government; forwarded to Parliament
National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC)
Old NCBC: Statutory (NCBC Act 1993); created after Indra Sawhney 1992
New NCBC: Constitutional status by 102nd Amendment 2018 → Art.338B
Composition: Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 members = 5 total
Appointment: By President
Art.342A: President specifies Central OBC list; Parliament can include/exclude (added by 102nd Amendment)
Art.338B(9): Centre and States SHALL consult NCBC on all major OBC policy matters — mandatory consultation
105th Amendment 2021: States restored power to maintain their own State OBC lists (Art.342A(3))
Functions: Investigate complaints; recommend measures; evaluate OBC progress; advise on OBC inclusion/exclusion
Civil Court powers: During inquiry (summon, documents, affidavits, public records)
Reports: Annual + special reports to President → Parliament with action memorandum
National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR)
Act: Commissions for Protection of Child Rights Act, 2005
Established: March 2007
Nature: Statutory body; NOT constitutional
Child: Person below 18 years of age
Composition: Chairperson + 6 members (at least 2 women; from fields of education, child health, juvenile justice, children in distress, disability, child labour, child psychology)
Appointment: By Central Government
Tenure: 3 years; age limit 60 years for Chairperson; 65 years for members; not eligible for re-appointment
Functions: Examine laws/policies for child rights; inquire into violations; review Child Rights Charter; examine POCSO implementation; monitor RTE Act
Powers: Civil Court powers; can approach HC/SC for enforcement
Report: Annual report to Central Government
State equivalent: State Commissions for Protection of Child Rights (SCPCRs)
National Commission for Minority Educational Institutions (NCMEI)
Act: National Commission for Minority Educational Institutions Act, 2004
Nature: Statutory body
Chairperson: Retired HC Chief Justice (appointed by Central Government)
Functions: Advise on questions related to minority education; examine complaints about minority educational institutions; grant minority status certificates
Art.30: Right of religious/linguistic minorities to establish and administer educational institutions
Powers: Civil Court powers; appellate authority in minority institution disputes
Report: Annual report to Central Government
