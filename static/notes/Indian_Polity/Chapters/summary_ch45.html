Ch45 Quick Summary — ఇతర జాతీయ కమిషన్లు / Other National Commissions

NCW | NCM | NCBC | NCPCR | NCMEI — All Statutory (except NCBC = Constitutional since 2018)

National Commission for Women (NCW)
Act: NCW Act 1990 | Est.: January 31, 1992
Nature: Statutory (not constitutional)
Composition: Chairperson + 5 members (at least 1 SC and at least 1 ST mandatory separately) + Member Secretary
Appointment: Central Government | Tenure: 3 years (no age limit)
Report to: Central Government (NOT President)
Powers: Civil Court powers; can forward cases to Magistrate
First Chairperson: Jayanti Patnaik (1992)
National Commission for Minorities (NCM)
Act: NCM Act 1992 | Est.: 1993
Composition: Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 5 members = 7 (all from minority communities)
Appointment: Central Government | Tenure: 3 years; one re-appointment
6 notified minorities: Muslims, Christians, Sikhs, Buddhists, Parsis (Zoroastrians), Jains (added 2014)
Powers: Civil Court powers during inquiry
Report to: Central Government
National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC)
Old NCBC: Statutory (NCBC Act 1993) — after Indra Sawhney 1992
New NCBC: Constitutional — Art.338B (102nd Amendment 2018)
Composition: Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 members = 5 | Appointment: President
Art.342A: President specifies Central OBC list; Parliament includes/excludes
Art.338B(9): Mandatory consultation — Centre AND States MUST consult NCBC on major OBC policy matters
105th Amendment 2021: Restored States' power to maintain own OBC lists (Art.342A(3))
Report to: President → Parliament (with action memorandum)
NCPCR & NCMEI
NCPCR Act: CPCR Act 2005 | Est.: March 2007 | "Child" = below 18 years
NCPCR Composition: Chairperson + 6 members (≥2 women); age limit: Chair 60, Members 65; no re-appointment
NCPCR Appointment: Central Government | Monitors RTE, POCSO, JJ Acts
NCMEI Act: NCMEI Act 2004 | Chairperson = retired HC Chief Justice
NCMEI: Grants minority institution certificates; advises on Art.30 rights; appellate authority
Art.30: Religious/linguistic minorities right to establish and administer educational institutions
