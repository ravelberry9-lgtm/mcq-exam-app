Ch43 Quick Summary — పార్టీ ఫిరాయింపు నిరోధక చట్టం / Anti-Defection Law

52nd Amendment 1985 | 10th Schedule | Art.102(2) & 191(2) | Kihoto Hollohan 1992

Background & 52nd Amendment
"Aaya Ram Gaya Ram": Gaya Lal (Haryana MLA, 1967) — changed party 3 times in ONE day
52nd Amendment 1985: Added 10th Schedule; amended Art.101, 102, 190, 191; effective March 1, 1985
91st Amendment 2003: Deleted Para 3 (split/1/3 exception); added CoM size cap (15%)
Constitutional basis: Art.102(2) — Parliament; Art.191(2) — State Legislature
Disqualification Grounds — Para 2
Ground 1: Voluntarily gives up membership of the political party on whose ticket elected
Ground 2: Votes or abstains contrary to party whip without prior permission
Independent member: Joins any party after election → disqualified immediately
Nominated member: Joins any party after 6 months of taking seat → disqualified
"Voluntarily giving up" ≠ formal resignation: Can be inferred from conduct (Ravi Naik 1994)
Exceptions — Para 4 (Merger)
Merger (Para 4): At least 2/3 of legislature party members agree to merge → NOT disqualified
Split (Para 3) DELETED: 91st Amendment 2003 removed 1/3 split exception
Speaker/Dy. Speaker (Para 5): May resign from party; can rejoin after ceasing to be presiding officer
President/VP elections: Cross-voting allowed — not covered by 10th Schedule
Decision-Making — Speaker/Chairman
Para 6: Speaker (LS/State Assembly) or Chairman (RS/State Council) decides petitions
Nabam Rebia 2016: Speaker facing removal motion CANNOT hear disqualification petitions
Keisham Meghachandra 2020: Speaker must decide within 4 weeks
Criticism: Speaker belongs to ruling party — perceived bias
Law Commission 170th Report 1999: Recommended independent tribunal
Kihoto Hollohan 1992 (5-Judge Constitution Bench)
Verdict (3:2): 10th Schedule is constitutionally VALID
Art.105/194: 10th Schedule does NOT violate parliamentary privileges
Para 7 STRUCK DOWN: Required State ratification under Art.368(2) proviso — not done
Judicial review: Allowed under Art.136/226/227 ONLY after Speaker's order, not before
Basic structure: 10th Schedule strengthens parliamentary democracy (not violates)
91st Amendment 2003 — Key Additions
Para 3 deleted: 1/3 split exception completely removed
Art.75(1A): Central CoM ≤ 15% of Lok Sabha strength
Art.164(1A): State CoM ≤ 15% of Assembly strength (minimum 12)
Art.75(1B)/Art.164(1B): Disqualified member CANNOT be appointed as Minister
Key Cases
Ravi Naik 1994: "Voluntarily giving up" inferred from conduct — no formal resignation needed
G. Viswanathan 1996: Expulsion from party ≠ disqualification (if member doesn't acknowledge leaving)
Nabam Rebia 2016: Speaker facing removal motion cannot hear disqualification petitions
Keisham Meghachandra 2020: 4-week time limit for Speaker to decide
Subhash Desai 2023: Maharashtra Shiv Sena split case — referred larger questions to Constitution Bench
Quick-Revision Pills
