Ch42 Quick Summary — కేంద్రపాలిత ప్రాంతాలు & ఢిల్లీ / Union Territories & Delhi

Part VIII | Art.239–242 | Art.239AA (69th Amendment 1991) | 8 UTs

UTs — List & Classification
8 UTs (as of 2020): Delhi NCT, Puducherry, J&K, Ladakh, Chandigarh, D&NH + D&D (merged 2020), A&N Islands, Lakshadweep
3 UTs with Legislature: Delhi NCT (Art.239AA), Puducherry (Art.239A), J&K (J&K Reorganisation Act 2019)
5 UTs without Legislature: Ladakh, Chandigarh, D&NH & D&D, A&N Islands, Lakshadweep
Part VIII: Art.239–242 — The Union Territories
Merger 2020: Dadra & Nagar Haveli + Daman & Diu merged (January 26, 2020) — now single UT
Administration — Art.239 & 240
Art.239: President administers UTs through an Administrator (designated as LG or Governor depending on UT)
Puducherry Administrator: Designated as Lt. Governor
Art.240: President can make Regulations for UTs without Legislature (Andaman, Lakshadweep, D&NH & D&D, Ladakh, Chandigarh)
Regulation power: President's regulation has force of Act of Parliament; can modify or repeal Central Acts for those UTs
Art.239B: Administrator of UT with legislature may promulgate Ordinances when UT legislature not in session (similar to Art.123/Art.213)
Art.239AB: President's Rule in UT — if constitutional machinery fails in a UT with legislature
Chandigarh: Shared capital of Punjab and Haryana; administered by Centre
Delhi NCT — Art.239AA (69th Amendment)
69th Amendment 1991: Inserted Art.239AA; effective February 1, 1992
Delhi = NCT: National Capital Territory — special status, not a full State
Legislature: Legislative Assembly (70 seats); can legislate on State List + Concurrent List EXCEPT three subjects
Three Exceptions (Centre controls): Public Order + Police + Land
LG's role: Acts on aid and advice of CoM EXCEPT on reserved matters; can refer disputes to President
CoM accountability: To Delhi Legislative Assembly (not Parliament)
Delhi — LG vs CM Disputes & SC Judgments
SC 2018 (Constitution Bench — 5 judges): LG bound by aid and advice of Delhi CoM; LG ≠ independent authority; "aid and advice" = binding except reserved subjects
SC 2023 (5-judge bench): Delhi Legislative Assembly controls services (Entry 41 — State Public Services) — not Centre
GNCTD Amendment Act 2021: "Government" = LG in all laws; CoM's decisions to be communicated to LG first
GNCTD Amendment Act 2023 (106th Amendment): Services restored to Centre/LG — overruled SC 2023 judgment legislatively
Art.239AB: President's Rule in Delhi; if constitutional machinery fails
Puducherry & UTs with Legislature
Art.239A: Parliament may create a legislature and CoM for a UT; Puducherry covered under this
Puducherry: 4 districts (Puducherry, Karaikal, Mahe, Yanam) — geographically scattered
Legislature: 30-seat Legislative Assembly; can legislate on State + Concurrent Lists
LG (Puducherry): Acts on aid and advice of CoM; can reserve Bills for President; can refer matters to President
J&K (UT with Legislature): Created by J&K Reorganisation Act 2019; has Legislative Assembly
Art.241 & Art.242
Art.241: Parliament may establish a High Court for any UT OR declare any court to be HC for a UT
Delhi HC: Established under Art.241 — has jurisdiction over Delhi NCT
J&K HC: Continues as HC for J&K UT after 2019 reorganisation
Art.242: Originally dealt with Coorg — DELETED (Coorg merged with Karnataka in 1956)
Art.239B: Administrator can promulgate Ordinances when UT Legislature not in session
J&K Reorganisation 2019 & Ladakh
Art.370 abrogated: August 5, 2019 (Presidential Order + Parliamentary Resolution)
J&K Reorganisation Act 2019: Passed August 9, 2019; effective October 31, 2019
Two new UTs: J&K (with Legislature) + Ladakh (without Legislature)
Ladakh: Largest UT by area; no Legislature; administered by LG directly; two districts (Leh + Kargil)
Art.35A removed: Special privileges for J&K permanent residents — removed along with Art.370
J&K HC: Continues; jurisdiction extended to Ladakh also
States vs UTs — Key Differences
States: Art.152 (Part VI); Governor; CoM responsible to State Legislature; residual powers = Centre (Art.248)
UTs: Part VIII; Administrator/LG; no separate Governor; Centre has overriding power
Representation: UTs not in Rajya Sabha (except Delhi, Puducherry, J&K have representation)
Finance: No Finance Commission allocation for most UTs; funded directly by Centre
Legislative: Parliament can legislate for UTs (State List items); States cannot override Parliament on Concurrent List
