Art.239–242 | Art.239AA (Delhi) | Part VIII | 8 UTs | J&K Reorganisation 2019

Section 1 — Union Territories: Introduction
Part VIII (Art.239-242): Union Territories. Directly administered by Central Government.
Current 8 UTs (post-2019): Delhi (NCT), Puducherry, Chandigarh, Dadra & Nagar Haveli and Daman & Diu (merged 2020), Lakshadweep, Andaman & Nicobar Islands, Jammu & Kashmir, Ladakh.
UTs with Legislature: Delhi (NCT) + Puducherry + J&K (Legislature created by J&K Reorganisation Act 2019).
UTs without Legislature: Chandigarh, Dadra & Nagar Haveli and Daman & Diu, Lakshadweep, Andaman & Nicobar, Ladakh.
Why UTs: Strategic location, small size, special social/cultural conditions, economically non-viable as States, border security requirements.
Section 2 — Art.239-240: Administration
Art.239(1): Every UT shall be administered by the President acting through an Administrator appointed by him.
Administrator titles: Lieutenant Governor (LG) — for Delhi, Puducherry, Andaman & Nicobar, J&K, Ladakh; Administrator — for Chandigarh (also Punjab Governor), Dadra & Nagar Haveli (also Goa Governor), Lakshadweep.
Art.239(2): Notwithstanding Art.239(1) — if a UT has a legislature, the President may appoint the Governor of an adjacent State as LG of the UT.
Art.239A: Parliament may create a body (legislature or Council of Ministers or both) for certain UTs — created Puducherry legislature.
Art.240: President may make regulations for peace, progress and good government of certain UTs (without legislature). These regulations = have force of Act of Parliament.
Art.239B: Administrator of a UT with legislature may promulgate Ordinances when UT legislature is not in session — similar to President's Art.123 / Governor's Art.213 ordinance power.
Art.239AB: If constitutional machinery fails in a UT (with legislature), President may assume administration — similar to President's Rule (Art.356) in States.
Section 3 — Art.239AA: Delhi NCT Special Status
Art.239AA: Inserted by 69th Constitutional Amendment Act 1991 (effective February 1, 1992). Gave Delhi special status as National Capital Territory (NCT).
Legislative Assembly: Art.239AA(2) — Delhi to have Legislative Assembly; elected by direct election from all seats.
Legislative Competence: Delhi Assembly can legislate on State List and Concurrent List subjects EXCEPT three: (1) Public Order; (2) Police; (3) Land.
LG Powers: Art.239AA(4) — If CM/Cabinet advice differs from LG view: LG may refer to President if it involves Union matters or is against security/public order. Otherwise must act on CoM advice.
LG Role: LG = representative of President (not independent executive); must act on CoM advice except reserved matters. NOT equivalent to Governor of State.
Services (Entry 41): Constitutional Amendment Act 2023 (106th Amend) = gave Union Parliament power over 'services' in Delhi; overruled SC 2023 judgment.
Section 4 — Delhi LG vs CM Disputes
GNCTD Act 1991: Governs Delhi administration; amended 2021 and 2023.
SC 2018 (5-judge bench): LG has no independent decision-making power — must act on CoM advice except on matters where President's assent is sought; Delhi CM's CoM = real executive.
SC 2023 (Constitution Bench): Delhi government has legislative and executive power over 'services' (Entry 41, State List); LG must act on CoM advice even on services — reiterated CM's primacy over services.
Government of NCT of Delhi (Amendment) Act 2023: Parliament overruled SC 2023 — restored Union control over services in Delhi; Act of Parliament prevailed over CM's position.
106th Amendment 2023: Constitutional amendment gave Parliament power over services in Delhi — cannot be overruled by mere SC judgment; now settled constitutionally.
Section 5 — Puducherry & UTs with Legislature
Puducherry: Legislature established under Art.239A; Puducherry Legislative Assembly Act 1962. French colonial territory (handed over 1954; de jure 1962).
Puducherry CM: Chief Minister and Council of Ministers exist for State List subjects. LG (not Governor) = administrator; must act on CoM advice on State subjects.
Puducherry Legislature — limited: Cannot legislate on certain subjects; Parliament can override. Weaker than State legislature.
J&K Legislature: Created by J&K Reorganisation Act 2019 (Parliament law, not constitutional amendment). J&K has Legislature but no full State powers (services under LG).
LG of J&K: Greater powers than Delhi LG or Puducherry LG — J&K Assembly has limited legislative competence compared to States; LG retains control over police, All India Services, Anti-Corruption Bureau.
Section 6 — Art.241 & Art.242
Art.241: Parliament may constitute a High Court for a UT or declare any court in UT as High Court. Delhi HC = Parliament law; handles Delhi NCT + Haryana + HP matters too (originally).
Andaman & Nicobar: HC of Calcutta jurisdiction. Lakshadweep: Kerala HC. Dadra & Nagar Haveli + Daman & Diu: Bombay HC. Chandigarh: Punjab & Haryana HC.
Art.242 (deleted): Coorg provision — Coorg merged with Karnataka; Art.242 deleted. Historical provision now defunct.
UT HC vs State HC: Parliament constitutes UT HC under Art.241 (special provision); State HCs under Art.214 (mandatory — each State must have HC).
Section 7 — J&K 2019 & Ladakh
J&K Reorganisation Act 2019: Passed August 5-9, 2019; bifurcated J&K State into two UTs: (1) J&K UT (with Legislature); (2) Ladakh UT (without Legislature).
Art.370 abrogation: Presidential Order (CO 272) on August 5, 2019 abrogated Art.370 (special status); Art.35A simultaneously removed. J&K ceased to be State with Constitution.
SC 2023 on Art.370: SC upheld abrogation — Art.370 was temporary; President had power to abrogate via Constituent Assembly's (later Parliament's) role.
Ladakh: UT without Legislature; administered by LG directly. Largest UT by area (59% of former J&K area).
Statehood promise: Central government stated J&K statehood will be restored when conditions normalise — SC noted this in 2023 judgment.
Section 8 — Comparative: States vs UTs
States: Elected government; Governor = constitutional head; 3 lists (Union, State, Concurrent); High Court mandatory (Art.214); separate budget.
UTs (no legislature): President administers directly through LG; Parliament = legislature; President's regulations (Art.240).
UTs (with legislature): Legislature exists; CM/CoM; limited to State/Concurrent List subjects; LG not equivalent to Governor; Parliament can override UT legislature.
Union List: Only Parliament can legislate for UTs even on State List subjects (no state autonomy).
Chandigarh: Capital of Punjab + Haryana; UT administered by Punjab Governor as Administrator.
