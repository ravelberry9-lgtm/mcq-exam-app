Ch41 Quick Summary — నీతి ఆయోగ్ / NITI Aayog

January 1, 2015 | Cabinet Resolution | Non-Constitutional | Non-Statutory | Think Tank

Establishment & Nature
Established: January 1, 2015 by Cabinet Resolution
Replaced: Planning Commission (est. March 15, 1950 by Cabinet Resolution)
Nature: Non-constitutional, Non-statutory — created and can be dissolved by Cabinet Resolution
Full form: National Institution for Transforming India
HQ: Sansad Marg (formerly Yojana Bhavan), New Delhi
Composition
Chairperson: PM of India (ex-officio)
Vice-Chairperson: Appointed by PM; Cabinet rank; first VC = Arvind Panagariya (2015-17)
CEO: Appointed by PM; administers NITI Aayog; first CEO = Sindhushree Khullar; Amitabh Kant (notable)
Full-time Members: Domain experts (4-5); appointed by PM
Ex-officio Members: Up to 4 Cabinet Ministers nominated by PM
Part-time Members: Rotating; from universities/research institutions
Governing Council: All CMs + LGs of UTs with legislature; PM chairs
Key Differences: Planning Commission vs NITI Aayog
Planning Commission: Top-down; 5-Year Plans; fund allocation; States subordinate; 'super-cabinet'
NITI Aayog: Bottom-up; no 5-Year Plans; NO fund allocation; States as partners; think tank
Both: Cabinet Resolution basis; PM as Chairperson; same Yojana Bhavan; non-constitutional
NDC (PC) → Governing Council (NITI)
Last FYP: 12th Five-Year Plan (2012-17); no 13th FYP under NITI
Functions
Policy think tank — strategic direction and advice
NO fund allocation (key distinction from Planning Commission)
Cooperative federalism — States as active partners
Prepare Vision/Strategy documents (not binding 5-Year Plans)
Monitor implementation of government programs
Promote innovation — Atal Innovation Mission
Monitor India's SDG progress — SDG India Index
Key Initiatives & Reports
SDG India Index: Annual; 17 SDGs; States ranked; Kerala = top performer
State Health Index: Health outcomes + governance + inputs; with World Bank
Composite Water Management Index: 9 themes, 28 indicators
India Innovation Index: Innovation ecosystem ranking of States
Aspirational Districts: 112 districts; 5 sectors; monthly monitoring
Viksit Bharat@2047: India as developed nation by centenary; USD 30 trillion GDP target
Atal Innovation Mission: Atal Tinkering Labs + Incubation Centres + India Challenges
Governing Council
PM chairs; all CMs + LGs of UTs with legislature
Replaced Planning Commission's National Development Council (NDC)
No fixed meeting frequency (typically 1-2/year)
Regional Councils for regional issues
Criticisms & Limitations
No fund powers = less leverage over States
Non-statutory = can be dissolved easily
Governing Council meetings too infrequent
Overlaps with Finance Ministry and sector ministries
Not directly accountable to Parliament as institution
