Chapter 40 — SC, ST & OBC జాతీయ కమిషన్లు

Art.338 (NCSC) | Art.338A (NCST) | Art.338B (NCBC) | Constitutional Bodies

Section 1 — Art.338: National Commission for Scheduled Castes
Art.338: National Commission for Scheduled Castes (NCSC). Constitutional body.
89th Amendment 2003: Bifurcated original Art.338 commission → separate NCSC (Art.338) and NCST (Art.338A).
Original Art.338: Single commission for BOTH SC and ST (before 89th Amendment 2003).
Composition: Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 other members = total 5. Appointed by President.
Service conditions: Determined by President by rule — not voted from Consolidated Fund.
Section 2 — Art.338A: National Commission for Scheduled Tribes
Art.338A: National Commission for Scheduled Tribes (NCST). Added by 89th Amendment 2003.
Composition: Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 other members = 5 total. Appointed by President.
Scope: Covers all matters related to Scheduled Tribes — safeguards, rights, socio-economic development.
5th Schedule: Tribal areas in 10 States — NCST has advisory role for administration of these areas.
PESA 1996: NCST reviews PESA implementation in Scheduled Areas.
Section 3 — Art.338B: National Commission for Backward Classes
Art.338B: National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC). Added by 102nd Amendment 2018 (effective August 15, 2018).
Background: Earlier NCBC was statutory (National Commission for Backward Classes Act 1993); 102nd Amendment = constitutional status.
Composition: Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 other members = 5. Appointed by President.
OBC Lists: Parliament (Central OBC list) and State Legislatures (State OBC lists) have power; NCBC advises.
102nd Amendment also: Art.342A added — President specifies Central OBC list (for each State/UT); Parliament may include/exclude.
Section 4 — Composition & Appointment
All three commissions: Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 other members = 5 members total.
Appointment: All members appointed by President of India (by warrant under his hand and seal).
Conditions of service: Determined by President — not voted from CFI; charged upon CFI (for salary/allowances).
Removal: President removes — no removal protection like judges (not SC judge equivalent). By order of President.
Term: As determined by President; typically 3 years. No fixed constitutional term.
Eligibility: Constitution does not specify qualifications; President decides.
Section 5 — Functions & Powers (Civil Court)
NCSC Functions (Art.338(5)): (a) Investigate complaints of SC safeguard violations; (b) Inquire into specific complaints; (c) Advise Central/State governments on planning; (d) Recommend measures for socio-economic development; (e) Evaluate progress under Constitution/Parliament laws; (f) Present to President annual/special reports.
NCST Functions (Art.338A(5)): Same pattern — investigate, inquire, advise, recommend, evaluate, report.
NCBC Functions (Art.338B(5)): Investigate and monitor OBC safeguards; recommend measures; present reports to President; inspect working of OBC reservations.
Civil Court Powers: All three commissions = power of a Civil Court for summoning persons + requiring discovery of documents + receiving evidence on affidavits + requesting public records.
Not a judicial body: Recommendations are advisory — not binding on governments.
Section 6 — Annual Reports & Presidential Reference
Annual Report: Each commission presents annual report to President. President lays it before Parliament with memorandum of action taken.
State Governments: If report relates to State matters, copy sent to State Governor → laid before State Legislature.
Presidential Reference: President may refer any matter relating to SC/ST/OBC to respective commission; commission investigates and reports.
Special Reports: Commission may present special reports on urgent matters at any time (not just annually).
Union & State: Central government AND State governments both must consult respective commission on major policy decisions affecting SC/ST/OBC.
Section 7 — History & Constitutional Amendments
Original Constitution: Art.338 — Special Officer for SC & ST (Commissioner). Single Commissioner appointed by President.
65th Amendment 1990: Replaced Commissioner with multi-member Commission for SC & ST (Art.338 revamped).
89th Amendment 2003: Split into NCSC (Art.338) and NCST (Art.338A) — separate commissions.
National Commission for Backward Classes Act 1993: Statutory NCBC created after Mandal Commission implementation (under VP Singh; SC ruling in Indra Sawhney 1992).
102nd Amendment 2018: Constitutional status to NCBC (Art.338B) + Art.342A (OBC list power).
105th Amendment 2021: Restored States' power to identify OBC (after SC ruling in Maratha reservation case that only Parliament can add to Central OBC list).
Section 8 — Comparative & Key Facts
Art.338 vs Art.338A vs Art.338B: Same structure — 5 members, President appoints, Civil Court powers, annual reports to President.
NCSC covers: SCs + Anglo-Indians (deleted by 104th Amendment 2020); NCST covers: STs; NCBC covers: OBCs (SEBCs).
Key difference from statutory bodies: Constitutional commissions = cannot be abolished by ordinary Parliament law; require constitutional amendment.
Art.342 (SC List): President specifies SC list for each State/UT; Parliament may include/exclude.
Art.342A (OBC List): Same pattern as Art.342 — added by 102nd Amendment; restored State power by 105th Amendment.
Indra Sawhney 1992: SC upheld 27% OBC reservation; OBC = non-creamy layer only; cap 50% total reservation; separate NCBC mandated.
