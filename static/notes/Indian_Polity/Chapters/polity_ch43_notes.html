Ch43 — పార్టీ ఫిరాయింపు నిరోధక చట్టం / Anti-Defection Law

52nd Amendment 1985 | 10th Schedule | Art.102(2) & Art.191(2) | Kihoto Hollohan 1992

Background & 52nd Amendment 1985
Origin: Rampant floor-crossing ("Aaya Ram Gaya Ram" politics) led to demand for anti-defection law
"Aaya Ram Gaya Ram": Gaya Lal (Haryana MLA, 1967) changed party 3 times in ONE day — coined the phrase
52nd Amendment 1985: Amended Art.101, 102, 190, 191; added 10th Schedule; came into force March 1, 1985
91st Amendment 2003: Amended 10th Schedule — deleted the split (1/3) exception; merged Para 3
Constitutional basis: Art.102(2) — Parliament member; Art.191(2) — State Legislature member
Objective: Ensure stability of elected governments; prevent political horse-trading
10th Schedule — Structure
Para 1: Interpretation — defines "legislature party", "original political party", "election commission"
Para 2: Grounds of disqualification (main provision)
Para 3: Originally — Split (1/3 members); DELETED by 91st Amendment 2003
Para 4: Exception for merger (2/3 members)
Para 5: Exemption for Speaker/Chairman (presiding officers)
Para 6: Decision by Speaker/Chairman; finality clause (later struck down partly)
Para 7: Rules by Speaker/Chairman for disqualification proceedings
Para 8: Bar on courts (later subject to judicial review per Kihoto Hollohan)
Grounds of Disqualification — Para 2
Ground 1 — Voluntary Giving Up: Voluntarily gives up membership of the political party on whose ticket elected
Ground 2 — Voting Against: Votes or abstains from voting contrary to the direction of the party whip without prior permission
Ground 3 — Independent/Nominated: An independent member joins a political party after election
Nominated members: Nominated member joins a party after 6 months of taking seat → disqualified
"Voluntarily giving up" ≠ Resignation: Can be inferred from conduct even without formal resignation
Whip applies to: Voting in House; abstaining from voting; NOT to actions outside Parliament
Exceptions to Disqualification
Merger (Para 4): Not disqualified if member's original party merges with another party AND at least 2/3 of members of legislature party agree to merger
Old Split rule (Para 3): Was — 1/3 members split = faction exempt; DELETED by 91st Amendment 2003
91st Amendment 2003: Now ONLY 2/3 merger protection; split of 1/3 is NO LONGER an exception
Speaker/Deputy Speaker election: Para 5 — presiding officer may resign from party; can rejoin after ceasing to be presiding officer
Pre-poll alliance: NOT explicitly mentioned; no special exemption for switching within pre-poll alliance
Decision-Making — Speaker/Chairman's Role
Para 6: Speaker (Lok Sabha/State Assembly) or Chairman (Rajya Sabha/State Council) decides disqualification petitions
Finality clause: Para 6 originally said decision is "final and not subject to review" — upheld as valid in Kihoto Hollohan BUT subject to judicial review under Art.136/226/227
Criticism: Speaker belongs to ruling party — perceived bias; conflict of interest
Law Commission recommendation: Transfer power to Election Commission or independent tribunal
SC in Nabam Rebia 2016: Speaker facing removal motion cannot decide disqualification petitions
Procedure: Speaker must act within "reasonable time" — SC has set time limits in some cases

Kihoto Hollohan 1992 — Detailed
Full name: Kihoto Hollohan v. Zachillhu and Others (1992)
Bench: 5-judge Constitution Bench
Challenge: 10th Schedule challenged as unconstitutional on several grounds
Held valid: 10th Schedule does NOT violate Art.105/194 (parliamentary privileges); does NOT abridge right of speech
Para 7 struck down: Para 7 (rules by Speaker) requires ratification under proviso to Art.368(2) — ratification not done → Para 7 invalid
Judicial review: Para 6's finality clause does NOT exclude judicial review; High Court and Supreme Court can review under Art.226/227/136 — but ONLY after Speaker's order, not before
Ratio — 3:2: Majority (Verma, Sharma, Reddy JJ) upheld; minority (Jeevan Reddy, Agrawal JJ) took different view on Para 7
91st Amendment 2003 — Key Changes
Deleted Para 3: The "split" exception (1/3 of legislature party) completely removed
Council of Ministers size capped: Art.75(1A) — CoM ≤ 15% of Lok Sabha strength; Art.164(1A) — State CoM ≤ 15% of Assembly strength (minimum 12)
Disqualified member barred from ministerial office: Art.75(1B) and Art.164(1B) — disqualified member cannot be appointed as minister
Motive: Prevent "defections for ministerial berths" — close the loophole
Comparative Facts & Issues
Applies to: Both Houses of Parliament + all State Legislatures (including Legislative Councils)
Not applicable to: Local body elections, Panchayat/Municipal elections
President/Governor: NOT covered by 10th Schedule
Cross-voting in President/VP elections: NOT covered — free voting allowed
Dinesh Goswami Committee 1990: Recommended limiting whip to confidence/no-confidence motions and budget votes only
Law Commission 170th Report 1999: Recommended independent tribunal instead of Speaker
Election Commission: Does NOT decide disqualification under 10th Schedule (it decides under Art.103 for other qualifications)
