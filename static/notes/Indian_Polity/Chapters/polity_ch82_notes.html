0. Overview — ఎన్నికల సంస్కరణల అవసరం

Background (నేపథ్యం)

Money power, muscle power, booth capturing, criminalisation of politics — సమస్యలు reform కి trigger అయ్యాయి.

ముఖ్య Committees: Tarkunde (1974), Goswami (1990), Seshan era (1990-96), ADR/PUCL (2002-03).

1. Pre-1990 Reforms — Tarkunde & Goswami

Tarkunde Committee (1974)

Non-official, non-governmental committee — సిఫారసులు binding కావు.

State funding of elections, independent election commission, lowering voting age సిఫారసు చేసింది.

Voting age 21 నుండి 18 కి తగ్గించడం — 61st Amendment 1988 ద్వారా అమలు.

Goswami Committee (1990)

Dinesh Goswami (Law Minister) నేతృత్వంలో — అధికారిక committee.

Security deposit raise, anti-defection strengthening సిఫారసు.

అభ్యర్థి అనర్హత criteria కఠినంగా చేయాలని సిఫారసు.

2. Seshan Era (1990-96) — ఎన్నికల సంస్కారాలు

T.N. Seshan — 10వ CEC (1990-96)

Model Code of Conduct (MCC) కఠినంగా అమలు చేయడం మొదలుపెట్టాడు.

EPIC (Voter ID Card) 1993 లో ప్రవేశపెట్టాడు.

Election dates multiple times postpone చేశాడు — fair elections కోసం.

Seshan కారణంగా ECI కి "watchdog" గా పేరు వచ్చింది.

3. Constitutional & Statutory Amendments

ముఖ్యమైన చట్ట మార్పులు

61st Amendment 1988: Voting age 21 → 18 (Art.326).

ADR v. UOI 2002: Candidates must declare criminal record, assets, educational qualifications.

Lily Thomas 2013: Sec.8(4) RPA struck down — convicted sitting members immediately disqualified.

PUCL v. UOI 2013: NOTA (None of the Above) option on EVM.

CEC & CEC Act 2023: Appointment process changed — removed CJI from selection panel.

4. EVM & VVPAT

Electronic Voting Machine

EVM మొదటి use: 1982 — Kerala, Parur by-election (pilot).

All India nationwide: 2004 Lok Sabha elections నుండి.

VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail): 2019 Lok Sabha నుండి nationwide.

VVPAT — Nagaland 2013 assembly elections లో first full-fledged use.

5. Campaign Finance — ఎన్నికల నిధులు

Electoral Bonds & Funding

Electoral Bonds Scheme 2018 — anonymous corporate donations via SBI.

SC 2024: Electoral Bonds Scheme struck down — Art.19(1)(a) (voters' right to know) violated.

Election expenses limit: LS ₹95 lakh; Assembly ₹40 lakh (2022).

6. Recent Reforms (2000s-2020s)

Recent Key Reforms

NRI Voting (2011): NRIs can register as voters in constituency of origin.

Proxy Voting: Service personnel can vote through proxy.

Criminals in politics: SC direction — trial of MP/MLA criminal cases within 1 year (2017).

One Nation One Election: Kovind Committee 2024 report — simultaneous LS + Assembly polls.
