Chapter 33 — ఎన్నికల కమిషన్ / Election Commission

Art.324–329 | CEC | EVMs | Model Code | Delimitation | Part XV

Section 1 — Art.324: ECI Constitution & Powers
Art.324(1): ECI కి superintendence, direction and control of elections — Parliament, State Legislatures, President, Vice-President. రాజ్యాంగ సంస్థ.
Art.324(2): ECI = CEC (Chief Election Commissioner) + అవసరమైనప్పుడు Election Commissioners. President ECI strength నిర్ణయిస్తారు.
Art.324(3): District Election Officers, Returning Officers, Electoral Registration Officers — ECI నిర్దేశాలపై పని చేస్తారు.
Art.324(4): President/Governor — ECI consultation తో necessary staff provide చేస్తారు.
Art.324(5): CEC removal = SC judge మాదిరిగా — Parliament address + 2/3 majority + special majority. ECs = President removes CEC recommendation పై.
Plenary powers: ECI Art.324 కింద plenary powers hold చేస్తుంది — gaps fill చేయడానికి implied powers.
Section 2 — Art.325–329
Art.325: Uniform electoral roll — religion, race, caste, sex ఆధారంగా ఎవరినీ exclude చేయలేరు లేదా special roll create చేయలేరు.
Art.326: Elections = Adult Suffrage (Universal Adult Franchise) — 18 సంవత్సరాలు వయస్సు (61st Amendment 1989 = 21 → 18 changed).
Art.327: Parliament — elections conduct కి provisions make చేయవచ్చు (electoral rolls, corrupt practices, election petition etc.).
Art.328: State Legislature — State elections కి provisions make చేయవచ్చు (Parliament provisions conflict అయితే Parliament prevails).
Art.329: Courts elections లో intervene చేయకూడదు (election conduct matters కి bar). Election petitions = HC కి (Representation of People Act 1951).
Section 3 — ECI Composition & Appointment
Original ECI (1950–1989): Single-member — CEC only. T.N. Seshan reforms వరకు.
1989: Multi-member — President 2 ECs appoint చేశారు (before 1989 general elections) — but reverted after elections.
Since Oct 1993: Permanent multi-member — CEC + 2 Election Commissioners.
Appointment: President appoints CEC + ECs — 2023 కి ముందు: President sole discretion. Chief Election Commissioner and Other Election Commissioners (Appointment, Conditions of Service and Term of Office) Act 2023: Selection Committee = PM (Chair) + Leader of Opposition + CJI. SC stayed CJI's inclusion (2023).
Tenure: 6 years లేదా 65 సంవత్సరాలు — whichever earlier. Re-appointment లేదు.
Section 4 — CEC vs EC Status & Removal
CEC removal: Only by President — Parliament address (both Houses, special majority: 2/3 members present + voting AND absolute majority). Exactly like SC judge removal (Art.324(5)).
EC removal: President removes on CEC recommendation — no Parliament address needed. This asymmetry = ECs less secure than CEC.
Service conditions: CEC/EC conditions cannot be varied to their disadvantage after appointment (Art.324(5)).
Salary: SC judge equivalent (charged to CFI — Consolidated Fund of India).
Post-retirement: CEC = cannot hold further government appointment. ECs = not explicitly barred (but convention of restraint).
Section 5 — ECI Functions & Powers
Schedule: Announce election schedule, enforce Model Code. Polling dates, counting dates నిర్ణయిస్తుంది.
Symbols: Election symbols allot చేయడం + freeze చేయడం (party splits — ECI decides which faction gets symbol).
Political party recognition: National party (6 states లో 6% votes + 4 LS seats లేదా 11% seats) vs. State party criteria నిర్ణయిస్తుంది.
Model Code enforcement: Officials transfer, election expenses audit, exit polls ban.
Election petitions: ECI handle చేయదు — HC (via Representation of People Act 1951).
Bye-elections: 6 నెలల్లో conduct చేయాలి (vacancies filling).
Section 6 — Model Code of Conduct & Delimitation
Model Code of Conduct (MCC): రాజ్యాంగం/law లో లేదు — ECI Art.324 కింద enforce చేస్తుంది. Schedule announcement నుండి results వరకు.
MCC key rules: Government policy announcements ban; freebies ban; hate speech ban; use of religious/caste sentiments ban.
Delimitation Commission: Parliament law కింద — President appoints; SC judge chairs; ECI members ex-officio. Delimitation Act 2002.
Delimitation: 1952, 1963, 1973, 2002 (effective 2008). Frozen until 2026 under 42nd Amendment (now Art.82 after 84th Amendment 2001 froze till 2026).
84th Amendment 2001: Froze delimitation based on 1971 Census till first Census after 2026. SC/ST reserved seats readjustment done.
Section 7 — EVMs, VVPATs & Electoral Reforms
EVMs: First used 1982 (Kerala — Parur assembly constituency). Nationwide use = 2004 general elections. Manufactured by BEL and ECIL.
VVPATs: Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail — 2013 first use (Noksen, Nagaland bye-election). All booths nationwide = 2019 general elections.
NOTA: None Of The Above — introduced 2013 (SC order in PUCL case). Represented by ballot paper icon.
Voter ID (EPIC): Electors' Photo Identity Card — introduced 1993 by T.N. Seshan. Mandatory verification.
61st Amendment 1988: Voting age 21 → 18 years (Art.326). Effective from March 1989.
Paid News: ECI can treat paid news as election expenditure — 2013 onwards enforcement.
Section 8 — Important Cases & Comparisons
Mohinder Singh Gill vs. CEC (1978): SC — Art.324 ECI powers are plenary, not limited to statute. ECI can act in situations where law is silent.
T.N. Seshan reforms (1990s): Strict enforcement of MCC, voter ID cards, election expenditure monitoring. "One man who changed Indian elections."
PUCL vs. Union of India (2013): SC — NOTA introduced. Right to negative vote as part of freedom of expression.
Anoop Baranwal vs. Union of India (2023): SC — CEC/EC appointment Selection Committee = PM + LoP + CJI (until Parliament makes law). Later Parliament passed 2023 Act replacing CJI with Cabinet Minister.
ECI vs. UPSC: Both multi-member, President appoints, expenses charged to CFI. But CEC removal = SC judge level; ECs removal = CEC recommendation (unlike UPSC where all = SC inquiry).
