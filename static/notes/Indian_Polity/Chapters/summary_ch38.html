Ch38 Quick Summary — మునిసిపాలిటీలు / Municipalities

74th Amendment | Art.243P–243ZG | Part IXA | 12th Schedule (18 subjects)

74th Amendment — Key Facts
Effective Date: June 1, 1993 (74th CAA 1992)
Added: Part IXA (Art.243P–243ZG) + 12th Schedule (18 subjects)
Companion: 73rd Amendment (Rural) — same year 1992
Exemptions (Art.243ZC): Nagaland, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Manipur Hills, Darjeeling
3 Types of Municipalities — Art.243Q
Nagar Panchayat: Transitional area (rural → urban)
Municipal Council: Smaller urban area
Municipal Corporation: Larger urban area
Exempt: Cantonment Boards (Defence Ministry), Port Trusts, Industrial Townships — Art.243Q(2)
Composition & Ward Committees — Art.243R-S
Ward Committees: Mandatory in municipalities with ≥3 lakh population — Art.243S(1)
Composition: Directly elected members from wards; State law may add nominated members (MPs/MLAs/experts)
Nominated members: No voting right in municipality proceedings
Reservation — Art.243T
SC/ST: Proportional to their population in that municipal area
Women: Not less than 1/3 of total seats (including SC/ST women)
OBC: State Legislature's discretion — NOT mandatory
106th Amendment 2023: Does NOT cover municipalities — Art.243T already covers them since 1993
Rotation: Reserved seats may rotate — State law decides
Vikas Kishanrao Gawali case 2021: SC capped OBC reservation (no single category >50% if total >50%)
Duration & Disqualifications — Art.243U-V
Term: 5 years — constitutional mandate (cannot extend by State law)
Election after dissolution: Before expiry of remaining term OR 6 months — whichever earlier — Art.243U(3)
Reconstituted body: Serves only unexpired term
Minimum age: 21 years — Art.243V
Disqualifications: As per State Legislature law
Powers, Finance & SFC — Art.243W-X-Y
Functions: Art.243W — 12th Schedule subjects (State law needed — NOT automatic)
Taxes: Art.243X — State Legislature authorises levy; no automatic taxing power
SFC: Art.243Y — State Finance Commission covers BOTH Panchayats + Municipalities
14th FC: Rs.87,144 crore urban grants | 15th FC: Rs.4.36 lakh crore total local bodies 2021-26
Audit, Elections, Court Bar — Art.243Z-ZC
Audit: Art.243Z — State Legislature makes law (CAG may be authorised)
Elections: Art.243ZA — SEC (State Election Commission) conducts all municipal elections
Court Bar: Art.243ZG — No court jurisdiction over delimitation / election disputes (Election Tribunal only)
DPC & MPC — Art.243ZD-ZE
DPC: Art.243ZD — District Planning Committee (MANDATORY — "shall constitute" in every district)
DPC Composition: 4/5 elected members (Panchayat + Municipality proportional), 1/5 can be nominated
MPC: Art.243ZE — Metropolitan Planning Committee (MANDATORY for metro areas ≥10 lakh)
MPC Composition: ≥2/3 elected from municipalities + Panchayats in metro area
Reality: Both DPC & MPC poorly implemented; State urban development authorities (HUDA, BMRDA) bypass them
12th Schedule — 18 Subjects
Urban planning & land use | Regulation of constructions | Roads & bridges | Water supply
Public health, sanitation, conservancy | Fire services | Urban forestry
Slums | Urban poverty alleviation | Provision of urban amenities
Promotion of cultural/educational/aesthetic aspects | Burials & burial grounds
Cattle pounds | Prevention of cruelty to animals | Vital statistics
Public amenities (streets, lighting, parking, bus stops) | Regulation of slaughter houses
Key Comparison — 73rd vs 74th Amendment
73rd: Rural | Part IX | Art.243-243O | 11th Schedule (29 subjects) | April 24, 1993
74th: Urban | Part IXA | Art.243P-243ZG | 12th Schedule (18 subjects) | June 1, 1993
Elections: Both — SEC (State Election Commission — Art.243K / Art.243ZA)
SFC: Art.243Y — Single SFC covers BOTH rural & urban local bodies
Implementation: Rural (73rd) = somewhat better; Urban (74th) = poorer devolution (parastatals, DPC/MPC dysfunctional)
