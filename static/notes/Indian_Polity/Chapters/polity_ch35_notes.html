Chapter 35 — ఆర్థిక సంఘం / Finance Commission of India

Art.280 | Vertical Devolution | Grants-in-Aid | 16th FC | Part XII

Section 1 — Art.280: Constitution of Finance Commission
Art.280(1): President = Finance Commission (FC) constitute చేస్తారు — Constitution commences + 2 years లో; thereafter every 5th year లేదా President deems fit.
Art.280(3): FC duties: (a) Union tax proceeds distribution between Union + States; (b) Grants-in-Aid (Art.275) amounts to States; (c) Any other matter President refers.
Quasi-judicial body: FC = quasi-judicial character. Recommendations = advisory (not binding on Union government).
Ad hoc body: FC = permanent body కాదు — every 5 years constitute, then dissolves. Work complete అయిన తర్వాత.
Art.280(2): FC chairman + members = Parliament law prescribe. Finance Commission (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1951.
Section 2 — Composition & Appointment
Composition: Chairman + 4 members = President appoints.
Chairman qualifications: Experience in public affairs.
Members qualifications (one each): (1) HC judge or qualified as HC judge; (2) Special knowledge of government finance/accounts; (3) Wide experience in financial matters + administration; (4) Special knowledge of economics.
Tenure: Parliament law determines — usually about 3 years (until report submitted). President may re-appoint members.
FC Act 1951: Chairman + 4 members qualifications, service conditions, staff/expenses from CFI.
Section 3 — FC Functions: Devolution
Vertical Devolution: Union Divisible Pool — what % goes to States collectively. 14th FC = 42% (historic high). 15th FC = 41% (Jammu & Kashmir bifurcation adjustment).
Horizontal Devolution: Among States — how to distribute. Criteria: Population (2011 Census from 14th FC), Area, Income distance, Forest cover, Demographic performance, Tax effort.
Divisible Pool: All Union taxes (except cess + surcharges) = divisible pool. CGST, Corporation Tax, Income Tax, Customs, Excise — all in pool.
Cess + Surcharges: NOT in divisible pool — 100% Union retains. States complain about increasing cess (GST Compensation Cess etc.).
Grants-in-Aid (Art.275): FC recommends grants to specific needy States (beyond devolution). Local body grants + disaster management grants also.
Section 4 — FC Recommendations
Non-binding recommendations: FC = advisory. Union government can accept/modify/reject. Historically most recommendations accepted.
Grants-in-Aid categories: (1) Post-devolution revenue deficit grants; (2) Sector-specific grants; (3) State-specific grants; (4) Local body grants (urban + rural); (5) Disaster management grants.
15th FC (2021-26) innovations: Differentiated recommendations for J&K UT; performance-based incentives; health sector grants; defence expenditure fund.
FC Report Parliament: FC report = President to Parliament lay; explanatory memorandum (action taken). Binding once Parliament accepts.
Section 5 — Finance Commissions I–XVI
1st FC (1951-52): Chairman = K.C. Neogy. 1952-57 recommendations.
10th FC (1992-95): First to recommend 29% share to States. Chairman = K.C. Pant.
11th FC (1998-2000): 28.5%. First mention of local bodies. Chairman = A.M. Khusro.
12th FC (2003-05): 30.5%. Chairman = C. Rangarajan. Emphasized fiscal consolidation.
13th FC (2007-10): 32%. Chairman = Vijay Kelkar. FRBM compliance incentives.
14th FC (2013-15): 42% (highest ever). Chairman = Y.V. Reddy. Used 2011 Census population for first time. Largest ever vertical devolution.
15th FC (2017-20): 41%. Chairman = N.K. Singh. J&K bifurcation; performance grants; COVID-19 impact. Period: 2020-21 to 2025-26.
16th FC (2023-): Chairman = Arvind Panagariya. Period: 2026-27 to 2030-31 recommendations.
Section 6 — FC vs Planning Commission vs NITI Aayog
Finance Commission: Constitutional (Art.280); President appoints; vertical+horizontal devolution + grants; quasi-judicial; ad hoc; binding spirit though advisory.
Planning Commission (abolished 2014): Non-constitutional, executive; PM chairs; Plan transfers (plan grants, centrally sponsored schemes); Discretionary; permanent body.
NITI Aayog (2015–): Think tank; PM chairs; no fund transfer powers; policy advice only; permanent; replaces Planning Commission.
Key conflict: FC = constitutionally mandated share; Planning Commission = PM's discretion → States felt PC undermined FC. 14th FC increased share + reduced plan transfers.
Section 7 — Devolution Formulas
14th FC horizontal criteria: Population (2011) 17.5%; Area 15%; Forest cover 7.5%; Income distance 50%; Demographic performance 10%.
15th FC horizontal criteria: Population (2011) 15%; Area 15%; Forest+Ecology 10%; Income distance 45%; Demographic performance 12.5%; Tax+fiscal effort 2.5%.
Income distance: State per capita income vs. richest State — poorer States get more. Equalization principle.
Demographic performance: States that controlled population growth (lower TFR) = rewarded. Compensates South States for lower 2011 Census population share.
Forest cover: States maintaining more forests = more share (ecological services). North-East States benefit.
Section 8 — Important Facts & Comparisons
FC in Constitution: Part XII (Finance, Property, Contracts & Suits), Art.280. Not Part XIV (Services).
FC Chairman past: Usually senior economists/public servants. K.C. Neogy (1st), Y.V. Reddy (14th), N.K. Singh (15th), Arvind Panagariya (16th).
FC vs CAG: Both Art. in Constitution; both advisory/independent; but FC = periodic (5 years), CAG = permanent. FC = financial distribution; CAG = financial audit.
Cess controversy: Union increased cess (GST Compensation Cess, Swachh Bharat Cess, Krishi Kalyan Cess) — not in divisible pool. States' effective share decreased despite 42% recommendation.
FC Mandate expansion: 13th FC asked to consider FRBM; 14th FC = local body governance; 15th FC = defence + internal security fund.
