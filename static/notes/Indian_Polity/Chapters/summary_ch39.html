Ch39 Quick Summary — సహకార సంఘాలు / Co-operative Societies

97th Amendment 2011 (eff. Feb 15, 2012) | Art.43B (DPSP) | Part IXB Art.243ZH–ZT

97th Amendment — Three Changes
Change 1: Art.19(1)(c) — Added "co-operative societies" to FR to form associations/unions
Change 2: Art.43B — New DPSP: State shall promote voluntary formation, autonomous functioning, democratic control, professional management
Change 3: Part IXB — Art.243ZH to Art.243ZT inserted (Co-operative Societies chapter)
SC 2021 (Rajendra N. Shah): Part IXB = only for Multi-State Co-ops; Art.19(1)(c) + Art.43B = valid nationally
Part IXB Key Provisions
Art.243ZH: Definitions — Board, Co-op Society, Multi-State Co-op, Member
Art.243ZI: Incorporation, regulation, winding up = State law
Art.243ZJ: Board — max 21 directors + 1 SC/ST seat + 1 women seat
Art.243ZK: Elections — authority as per State law; must complete before term expiry
Art.243ZL: Supersession — max 6 months (exceptional: 1 year); elections within supersession period
Art.243ZM: Audit — at least once a year; State law sets qualifications
Art.243ZN: Annual General Body Meeting — mandatory once a year
Art.243ZO: Member rights — inspection of books, accounts, records
Art.243ZP: Annual returns — audited accounts, member list, GBM minutes, election plan
Art.243ZQ: Offences and penalties — State law defines
Art.243ZR: Multi-State application — Parliament applies Part IXB
Art.243ZS: Existing laws continue; conformity within 1 year
Art.243ZT: Saving — elections held before 97th Amendment = valid
State vs Multi-State Co-ops
Single-State Co-op: Entry 32, State List — exclusive State subject; State law governs
Multi-State Co-op: Entry 44, Union List — Parliament law (MSCS Act 2002); Central Registrar
SC 2021: Part IXB = multi-state only; States not bound by Art.243ZJ/ZL etc. for their co-ops
97th Amendment State ratification: Not obtained (required under Art.368(2) for State List Entry 32) → Part IXB invalid for State co-ops
Key Cases
Gujarat HC 2013 (Rajendra N. Shah): Part IXB unconstitutional — no State ratification obtained
SC 2021 (Constitution Bench — Rajendra N. Shah v. UOI): Upheld Gujarat HC for State co-ops; Part IXB valid for multi-state only; Art.19(1)(c) + Art.43B = valid nationally
Historical Facts
First co-op society in India: Anyonya Co-operative Bank, Baroda — 1889
First legislation: Co-operative Credit Societies Act 1904
Rochdale Pioneers 1844: Origin of modern co-operative movement (28 weavers, Lancashire, England)
ICA: International Co-operative Alliance — 1895, Geneva; India member through NCUI
Ministry of Co-operation: Created July 2021; Amit Shah = first minister
Rural Co-op Credit Structure
Three tiers: PACS (village) → DCCB (district) → SCB (State)
NABARD: Apex for rural credit; refinances + supervises SCBs and DCCBs
Urban Co-op Banks: Dual regulation — RBI (banking) + State Registrar (co-op); Banking Regulation Amendment 2020 strengthened RBI powers
Key Examples of Multi-State Co-ops
IFFCO: Indian Farmers Fertiliser Co-op — world's largest fertiliser co-op
KRIBHCO: Krishak Bharati Co-operative — fertilisers
NAFED: National Agricultural Co-op Marketing Federation — MSP/Price Support Scheme
NCDC: National Co-operative Development Corporation
