1. ఖనిజాల వర్గీకరణ (Classification of Minerals)

వర్గంనిర్వచనంముఖ్య ఉదాహరణలువిశేషం
లోహ ఖనిజాలు
(Metallic Minerals)		 లోహాలు సంగ్రహించే ఖనిజాలు; Smelting ద్వారా లోహం తీస్తారు Iron Ore, Copper, Bauxite, Manganese, Gold, Silver, Lead, Zinc India లో Jharkhand-Odisha-Chhattisgarh belt లో అత్యధికం
అలోహ ఖనిజాలు
(Non-Metallic Minerals)		 లోహాలు లేవు; Industries కి raw material గా వాడతారు Limestone, Mica, Gypsum, Salt, Asbestos, Silica, Phosphate AP = Mica #1 (current production, 95%+) + Baryte (World #1) + Limestone (India 2nd); Rajasthan = Limestone + Gypsum leader; Jharkhand = Mica historical quality leader
⚠️ TRAP: Older textbooks say Jharkhand = Mica 1st — current IBM data: AP = #1 producer
శక్తి ఖనిజాలు
(Energy Minerals)		 శక్తి ఉత్పత్తి కోసం వాడే ఖనిజాలు; Fossil fuels + Nuclear Coal, Petroleum (Crude Oil), Natural Gas, Uranium, Thorium Coal = India's largest energy mineral; Petroleum = Assam + Gujarat + Offshore
🔍 WHY India's Minerals concentrated in Peninsular India (Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Odisha belt)? (Concept Clarity)
Peninsular India లో ఖనిజాలు అత్యధికంగా ఎందుకు ఉన్నాయి?

① Ancient Precambrian Rocks: Peninsular India = 2.5–4 billion సంవత్సరాల పురాతన Archean + Proterozoic rocks → Minerals form over millions of years → Old rocks = mineral-rich
② Igneous + Metamorphic rocks: Heat + Pressure వల్ల mineral crystallization జరిగింది → Iron, Copper, Bauxite formation
③ Gondwana rock formations: Damodar Valley లో Coal beds = Ancient swamp forests that got buried → became coal over 300 million years
④ Indo-Gangetic Plains = mineral-poor: Alluvial soil is younger, deposited recently → no time for mineral formation; thick sediment cover buries ancient rocks
⑤ Deccan Trap basalt: Volcanic activity → Basalt rocks → Copper, Manganese formation in parts

★ Remember: "Old rocks = Mineral rich; New alluvial = Mineral poor" — ఇది exam లో reasoning question కి key answer.

⚠️ TRAP: "India's first oil refinery?" → Digboi (1867). "India's largest oil field?" → Mumbai High. "India's largest gas reserve area?" → KG Basin. వీటిని మార్చి అడుగుతారు!

6. ముఖ్య పరిశ్రమలు — Iron & Steel (ఇనుము-ఉక్కు)

🔍 WHY India's Steel Plants concentrated in Chota Nagpur Plateau (Jharkhand-Odisha-WB)? (Concept Clarity)
Steel = Iron Ore + Coking Coal + Limestone + Water + Labour. ఈ అన్నీ Chota Nagpur లో ఒకే చోట దొరుకుతాయి:
① Iron Ore: Jharkhand (Singhbhum), Odisha (Keonjhar), Chhattisgarh (Dalli-Rajhara) — అన్నీ nearby
② Coking Coal: Jharia (Jharkhand) = India's best coking coal; rail by road distance చాలా తక్కువ
③ Limestone: Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand లో locally available (flux కోసం)
④ Water: Damodar, Subarnarekha, Brahmani నదులు — industrial cooling water
⑤ Labour: Abundant tribal + skilled labour; low cost
⑥ Railways: British-era railway network → easy transport of raw materials + finished steel

★ South India లో Vizag Steel (RINL) = exception — Iron ore Odisha నుండి, Coking coal Australia నుండి import చేస్తారు. WHY Vizag? → Coastal location → easy import; Strategic AP development.

🔍 Mumbai = "Cottonopolis" WHY? — Cotton Textile Industry Location Factors (Concept Clarity)
Mumbai లో Cotton Textile industry ఎందుకు అభివృద్ధి చెందింది?
① Raw Material Proximity: Deccan Plateau (Black soil region) = Cotton hinterland → trains via Bhorghat pass
② Port Advantage: Mumbai port → machinery import (Lancashire, UK నుండి); Cloth export to East Africa, SE Asia
③ Humid Climate: Arabian Sea proximity → humid air → cotton yarn తక్కువగా తెగుతుంది (fewer yarn breaks) → better weaving
④ Capital availability: Parsi (Mumbai-based community — Tata, Wadia families) + Gujarati trading community → investment capital readily available
⑤ Labour: Cheap migrant workers from UP, Rajasthan, Konkan

9. పారిశ్రామిక విధానాలు (Industrial Policy)

విధానం / పథకంసంవత్సరంముఖ్య అంశాలు
Industrial Policy Resolution 1948; Revised 1956 1956 = "Nehruvian Model" — Industries 3 schedules లో: Schedule A (PSU only), B (PSU + Private), C (Private). Heavy industries = PSU; Consumer goods = Private
New Economic Policy (LPG) 1991 Liberalisation + Privatisation + Globalisation; Manmohan Singh (Finance Min) + Narasimha Rao (PM); License Raj తొలగింపు; FDI opening; Import duties reduction; PSUs disinvestment
Make in India 2014 Manufacturing hub: 25% GDP target; 25 focus sectors; FDI ease; Defence + Electronics + Automobiles + Textiles; "Zero Defect, Zero Effect" motto
PLI Scheme
(Production Linked Incentive)		 2020 14 sectors లో manufacturing boost; Mobile phones, Pharma, Textiles, Automobiles, Solar, Battery; Incentive = % of incremental sales; China+1 strategy
National Manufacturing Policy 2011 Manufacturing = 25% GDP (from 15%); 100 million jobs; National Investment + Manufacturing Zones (NIMZs); Green manufacturing
SEZ (Special Economic Zones) 2000 (China model); SEZ Act 2005 Export-focused enclaves; Tax exemptions; Deemed foreign territory; AP = Visakhapatnam SEZ (Pharma + IT); Kakinada SEZ (Petrochemical)
🔍 1991 LPG Reforms — WHY India needed it? (Concept Clarity)
1991 లో India economic crisis కి India ఏమి చేసింది? ఎందుకు ఈ reforms అవసరమయ్యాయి?

Crisis Background:
① Foreign exchange crisis: India's forex reserves = only 2 weeks of import cover (July 1991); Nearly bankrupt
② Gulf War (1990–91): Oil prices spike → India import bill sharply up; Indian workers from Gulf returning → remittance drop
③ License Raj failure: 1950s–80s లో heavy regulation → inefficiency, corruption, slow growth

LPG Reforms (Manmohan Singh as FM, PM Narasimha Rao):
① Liberalisation: License Raj తొలగించడం; Industries freely start చేయగలిగారు
② Privatisation: PSUs disinvestment; Private sector allowed in telecom, aviation, banking
③ Globalisation: Trade barriers down; FDI welcome; WTO membership (1995)

Result: India GDP growth 5–6% → 8–9% (2000s boom); IT industry exploded; Pharma exports grew; Middle class expanded
★ TRAP: "Who introduced LPG reforms?" → Manmohan Singh (Finance Minister) proposed; Narasimha Rao (PM) implemented. PV Narasimha Rao = PM, not FM. Manmohan = FM. Both are correct depending on question framing.
