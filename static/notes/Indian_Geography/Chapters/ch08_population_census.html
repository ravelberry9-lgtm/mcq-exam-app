1. జనగణన — చరిత్ర మరియు ముఖ్య వివరాలు (Census — History & Basics)

అంశంవివరాలు
జనగణన నిర్వహణ Office of the Registrar General & Census Commissioner of India (ORGI); 10 సంవత్సరాలకొకసారి (Decennial Census)
భారతదేశంలో మొదటి జనగణన 1872 — Lord Mayo హయాంలో (non-synchronous, partial); 1881 = First synchronous (uniform) census — Lord Ripon హయాం
స్వాతంత్ర్యానంతర మొదటి జనగణన 1951 — Independent India మొదటి census
అత్యంత తాజా census Census 2011 (15th census); Census 2021 = COVID-19 కారణంగా postponed — 2025–26 లో నిర్వహించే అవకాశం
Census 2011 Motto "Our Census, Our Future" (మన జనగణన, మన భవిష్యత్తు)
💥 Census సంవత్సరాలు — TRAP (పరీక్షలో తప్పుగా ఇస్తారు):
• 1872 = First census (partial/non-synchronous) — Lord Mayo
• 1881 = First proper/synchronous census — Lord Ripon ★ PYQ
• 1921 = "జనాభా మహా విభజన సంవత్సరం" / "Year of Great Divide" — జనాభా decline → growth కి turn అయింది
  (⚠️ "మహాసంగమ" అంటే confluence అవుతుంది — ఇది తప్పు; correct term = "మహా విభజన" = Great Divide)
• 1951 = Independent India's first census
• 2011 = Most recent completed census (15th)
⚠️ TRAP: "India's first census?" → 1872 (partial) లేదా 1881 (synchronous) — రెండూ different context లో సరైనవే. Question framing జాగ్రత్తగా చదవాలి!

🔍 WHY India's Population is so large — and WHY it's now slowing? (Concept Clarity)
India జనాభా ఎందుకు అంత పెద్దది? ఇప్పుడు ఎందుకు తగ్గుతున్నది?

WHY Large Population?
① Young age structure: India's population has huge proportion of reproductive age (15–49) → high natural increase
② Improved Death Rate: DDT (malaria eradication), antibiotics, vaccination → CDR sharply fell after 1921 but BR remained high → gap = population explosion
③ Early marriage tradition: Historically early marriages → longer reproductive period → more children
④ Son preference: Son preference → families continue having children until sons born → higher total fertility

WHY Slowing Now?
① Education (esp. female literacy): Educated women = fewer children; Kerala = 94% literacy → TFR 1.8 (below replacement)
② Urbanisation: Urban families = smaller; cost of living + education expenses → 1–2 child norm
③ Family Planning: Government contraception programmes, sterilization camps, ASHA workers
④ TFR declining: National TFR = 2.0 (2020) — at or below replacement level (2.1); South India already below 2.0

★ TFR (Total Fertility Rate) = average children per woman in lifetime. Replacement level = 2.1. South India TFR < 2.0 → population will eventually decline. ⚠️ TRAP: Bihar TFR ~3.0 (NFHS-5 data = 3.01, above replacement); UP TFR ~2.35 (significantly declined — NOT 3.0+; old textbooks may give wrong impression). Bihar = the outlier; UP = improving.

💥 Population vs Density — India's BIGGEST TRAP (పరీక్షలో 100% అడుగుతారు):

🔍 WHY Kerala = India's Best on ALL Human Development Indicators? (Concept Clarity)
Kerala అన్ని Human Development Indicators లో India's best ఎందుకు?

① Historical strong public education: Travancore royal family (19th century) established free schools; Christian missions + temple schools → universal education tradition
② Female empowerment: Kerala = matrilineal society (Nair community traditionally matrilineal) → women valued → girls educated → female literacy high → lower TFR
③ Gulf remittances: Kerala male workers in Gulf → money sent home → family investment in education, healthcare → better living standards
④ Strong public health system: Primary health centres (PHCs) in every panchayat; high doctor/nurse ratio; ICDS (Integrated Child Dev Services) effective
⑤ Land reforms (1969): Kerala Land Reform Act = land distributed to poor → reduced extreme poverty → money available for education
⑥ Low population density relative to income: High remittance income + small family size → higher per capita resources

★ "Kerala Model of Development" = high HDI despite low per capita income compared to other states. Nobel laureate Amartya Sen cited Kerala as proof that HDI ≠ just GDP. APPSC Group 1 Mains essay potential.

⚠️ 1921 = "Year of Great Divide" (జనాభా విభజన సంవత్సరం) — APPSC లో guarantee గా వస్తుంది:
1911–1921 దశాబ్దంలో India's population actually declined in absolute numbers: 1911 census = 252 million → 1921 census = 251 million (Spanish Flu 1918 killed ~12–17 million Indians; plague + famines అన్నీ కలిసి population decrease చేశాయి). 1921 తర్వాత మళ్ళీ grow చేయడం మొదలుపెట్టింది. అందుకే 1921 = "Great Divide" — the turning point from decline to growth.
⚠️ TRAP: "Year of Great Divide" = 1921 (NOT 1951, NOT 1911). తరచుగా wrong options గా 1911 లేదా 1951 ఇస్తారు!

🔍 Urban classification — WHY it matters for exam? (Concept Clarity)
India లో "Urban" area define ఎలా చేస్తారు? APPSC లో definition trap వస్తుంది:

Census of India Urban Criteria (statutory + census towns):
① Statutory Towns (Municipal body): Municipality, Cantonment Board, Town Committee etc. చేత governed; any size
② Census Towns (3 conditions ALL must be met):
  a. Population ≥ 5,000
  b. Density ≥ 400 persons/sq km
  c. 75%+ male working population in non-agricultural activities
③ Urban Agglomeration (UA): Core city + adjoining urban outgrowths/municipalities = UA; Example: Hyderabad UA includes Secunderabad, Cyberabad areas

★ TRAP: "Census Town = 5,000+ population only" → WRONG. All 3 conditions must be met simultaneously (population + density + non-agricultural occupation). Exam లో partial condition ఇచ్చి trap చేస్తారు.
