2. NASA & International Space Missions

SPHEREx Mission

NASA Launched: March 12, 2025 (Falcon 9, Vandenberg)
Purpose: Study 450 million galaxies + 100 million stars; create 3D sky map to understand universe history
Orbit: Sun-synchronous orbit at 650 km above Earth

PUNCH Mission

NASA Launched: March 12, 2025 (same Falcon 9 as SPHEREx)
Purpose: Solar mission; 4 satellites study solar corona, solar winds, and coronal mass ejections (CMEs) for space weather prediction

Europa Clipper

NASA Journey to Jupiter's moon Europa
Mars flyby: March 2025 (trajectory boost)
Earth flyby: December 2026
Arrives at Europa: April 2030

ESA Biomass Satellite

ESA Launched: End of April 2025 (Vega C rocket, French Guiana)
Purpose: Map global forests; measure carbon levels + forest health; 7th Earth Explorer satellite under ESA's climate programme
Uses radar to quantify forest biomass and create 3D forest structure models

5. Key Technology Concepts in News

NISAR — World's First Dual-Frequency SAR

First joint Earth observation mission by NASA + ISRO
Uses both L-band and S-band radar
Launched: GSLV-F16; sun-synchronous polar orbit
Studies: earthquakes, landslides, glaciers, forests, agriculture
Launched: July 30, 2025

SpaDeX — India's Docking Experiment

Full form: Space Docking Experiment
Goal: validate ability to dock & undock small satellites in Low-Earth Orbit (LEO)
Two satellites: ~220 kg each
Critical for Gaganyaan and future lunar/space station missions

Sonic Weapons (Acoustic Weapons)

Deliver loud, painful sounds over long distances
Use audible/inaudible sound waves to disorient/incapacitate people
First developed for military; US used in Iraq (2004)
Serbia accused of using against protesters in Belgrade

LOX-Methane Engine (NGLV)

Liquid Oxygen + Methane propellant — cleaner, enables reusability
Being developed by ISRO's Liquid Propulsion Systems Centre (LPSC)
For: Next Generation Launch Vehicle (NGLV) — reusable booster stage
Spark torch igniter tested on GSLV Cryogenic Upper Stage (March 3, 2025)

Sporosarcina pasteurii — Lunar Brick Bacteria (IISc)

Process: Bacterium converts urea + calcium → calcium carbonate crystals (bound with guar gum) to cement lunar soil (regolith) particles into bricks
Application: Repair bricks for lunar habitats under NASA's Artemis programme (permanent Moon settlements)
Advantage: Eco-friendly; avoids transporting materials from Earth

6. Space Science Concepts

ConceptExplanation
Lagrange Point L1Position ~1.5 million km from Earth (towards Sun) where gravitational forces balance; Aditya-L1 stationed here to study Sun
SAR (Synthetic Aperture Radar)Radar system that creates high-resolution images of Earth's surface; NISAR uses L-band + S-band SAR
LEO (Low Earth Orbit)Orbits at 200–2,000 km altitude; SpaDeX operates here; ISS is in LEO
Sun-Synchronous Orbit (SSO)Polar orbit where satellite passes over any given point at same local solar time each day; SPHEREx at 650 km SSO; NISAR also uses SSO
Lunar RegolithLoose soil/rock covering Moon's surface; used by IISc bacteria technique to make lunar bricks
CME (Coronal Mass Ejection)Massive burst of solar plasma from Sun's corona; PUNCH mission studies CMEs for space weather prediction
DNA BarcodingIdentifying species using short DNA sequences; ZSI used it to identify blackfly species spreading river blindness
