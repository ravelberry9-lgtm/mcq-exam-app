Twelve-Day War (June 2025) & 2026 Iran War — AP High Court Study Notes

1. BACKGROUND — HOW IT ALL BEGAN

Iran-Israel Proxy War to Direct War

Iran and Israel have fought a decades-long proxy war. Iran arms Hamas in Gaza and Hezbollah in Lebanon. Iran's nuclear program has caused international concern for over a decade, with Israel considering it an existential threat.

2015: JCPOA (Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action) signed — Iran froze nuclear program; USA, UK, France, Germany, Russia, China (P5+1) lifted sanctions

2018: US President Trump unilaterally withdrew from JCPOA → Iran resumed uranium enrichment

Oct 7, 2023: Hamas attacked Israel → Gaza War began (1,200 Israelis killed; 251 hostages taken)

April 1, 2024: Israel bombed Iranian consulate in Damascus, Syria — killed senior IRGC officials

April 13–14, 2024: Iran's first-ever direct missile/drone attack on Israel (~300 drones & missiles; mostly intercepted)

Oct 2024: Iran's second direct attack on Israel; Israel retaliated by striking Iran's air defence & military radar

June 13–24, 2025: Twelve-Day War — Israel attacked Iran's nuclear & military sites

Jan 2026: Massive Iranian pro-democracy protests; security forces killed thousands of civilians

Feb 28, 2026: 2026 Iran War begins — US + Israel launch full-scale strikes on Iran

April 8, 2026: Conditional ceasefire declared (Pakistan mediating)

May 7, 2026: Israel strikes Beirut — kills alleged Hezbollah leader

May 15, 2026: India's EAM Jaishankar meets Iranian FM Araghchi in New Delhi

2. THE TWELVE-DAY WAR (JUNE 13–24, 2025)

Overview

Dates: June 13 – 24, 2025 (12 days)
Belligerents: Israel + USA  vs  Iran + Houthi-controlled Yemen
Trigger: Israel launched a surprise attack on Iran's nuclear and military facilities
Ended: Ceasefire on June 24, 2025 — brokered under US pressure

Day Before the War — IAEA Resolution

On June 12, 2025 (the day before Israel attacked), the IAEA declared Iran non-compliant with its nuclear obligations — in a resolution put forward by the USA, UK, France, and Germany. This gave diplomatic cover for the military action.

Israel's Attack — Phase 1 (June 13, 2025)

Israel launched a surprise attack involving commandos, Mossad operatives, and airstrikes targeting:
  • Iran's nuclear facilities: Natanz, Isfahan, Fordow
  • Air defence systems, military bases
  • Assassination of 10+ leading nuclear scientists
  • Key IRGC generals killed: Hossein Salami (IRGC Commander), Amir Ali Hajizadeh (IRGC Aerospace), Mohammad Bagheri (Chief of Staff), Gholam Ali Rashid

Iran's Retaliation — "Operation True Promise III"

Iran retaliated with:
550+ ballistic missiles and 1,000+ suicide drones
Targets: Israeli civilian centres, hospitals, military sites, energy and government infrastructure
Iran struck at least 12 military, energy, and government sites in Israel

US Intervention (June 22, 2025)

The USA bombed three Iranian nuclear sites on June 22, 2025:
Natanz, Isfahan, Fordow — extensively damaged
Iran retaliated by firing missiles at Al Udeid Air Base (US military base in Qatar)

Ceasefire — June 24, 2025

Israel and Iran agreed to a ceasefire on June 24, 2025, under US pressure.
Iran subsequently suspended cooperation with the IAEA, accusing it of sharing nuclear site data with Israel.

3. THE 2026 IRAN WAR (FEBRUARY 28, 2026 – ONGOING)

Start of War

Date: February 28, 2026
Who started it: United States + Israel launched coordinated airstrikes on Iran
Surprise element: Attacks launched during ongoing US-Iran nuclear negotiations
Stated goals: Destroy Iran's nuclear & ballistic missile program; prevent Iran from getting nuclear weapon; achieve regime change

Targets of US-Israel Strikes

  • Military bases and government buildings
  • Schools, hospitals, heritage sites (civilian infrastructure)
  • Nuclear facilities (already damaged in Twelve-Day War)
  • 190+ ballistic missile launchers destroyed
  • 155 naval vessels destroyed or damaged

Iran's Response — Counterstrikes

Iran retaliated with hundreds of drones and ballistic missiles hitting:
  • Israel (direct strikes)
  • US military bases in Bahrain, Jordan, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, UAE
  • Iraq's Kurdistan Region
  • Britain's Akrotiri military base on Cyprus (struck by drone)
  • Missiles intercepted over Turkey
  • Oman (civilian infrastructure struck)
  • Azerbaijan (alleged Iranian strikes)

Strait of Hormuz Crisis

Iran closed the Strait of Hormuz — triggering a global trade disruption crisis.
The Strait of Hormuz is the world's most important oil chokepoint — ~20% of global oil passes through it.
Iran threatened to keep it closed until US-Israel forces withdrew.
This significantly affected global oil prices and supply chains.

The conflict between Hezbollah and Israel escalated into the 2026 Lebanon War
2,000+ civilians and militants killed in Lebanon
Hezbollah fighters killed: 1,000–1,900

6. INDIA & THE IRAN-ISRAEL WAR

India has significant interests in the Iran-Israel conflict:
  • Chabahar Port (India-developed port in Iran) — strategic India-Iran link
  • Large Indian diaspora in Gulf countries (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Oman)
  • India imports oil from the Gulf; Strait of Hormuz closure directly impacted India
  • India called for dialogue and diplomacy; did not take sides
  • India evacuated citizens from conflict zones if required
