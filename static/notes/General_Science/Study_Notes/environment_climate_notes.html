4. COP29 (Baku 2024) & COP30 (Belém 2025)

🌍 COP29 — Baku, Azerbaijan

Dates: November 2024
Host: Azerbaijan (Baku)

Key Outcomes:
  • Baku Climate Unity Pact: Target — at least $1.3 trillion/year from all actors
  • Developed countries to mobilize at least $300 billion/year by 2035
  • Baku–Belém Roadmap to 1.3T launched to scale up finance in 2025
  • India criticized the $300B goal as "abysmally poor"
  • Mitigation work programme — digital platform for exchanging info to be designed 2025

🌳 COP30 — Belém, Brazil

Dates: November 2025
Host: Brazil (Belém, Amazon region)

Key Outcomes:
  • Belém Package approved
  • Over 122 Parties submitted updated NDCs (Nationally Determined Contributions)
  • Call to triple adaptation finance by 2035
  • Global Climate Finance Accountability Framework launched
  • Belém Mission to 1.5 — action-oriented platform for enhanced climate ambition
COP Sequence Quick Recall:
COP26 = Glasgow 2021 → COP27 = Sharm el-Sheikh (Egypt) 2022 → COP28 = Dubai (UAE) 2023 → COP29 = Baku (Azerbaijan) Nov 2024 → COP30 = Belém (Brazil) Nov 2025 → COP31 = Australia 2026

7. Pollution & Environmental Hazards

Nurdles — Marine Plastic Pollution (May 2025)

Nurdles = tiny plastic pellets, 1–5 mm in size, raw material for plastic manufacturing
Made of: LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene) and HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene)
Incident: Found along Thiruvananthapuram coast (May 27, 2025) after Liberian cargo ship MSC ELSA 3 sank (May 25, 2025)
Harm: Marine animals ingest them → health problems; disrupt ecosystems

Tamulidoba Beel (Pobitora WLS)

Major wetland in Pobitora WLS, Assam — drying up; threat from water hyacinth to waterfowl; 72% of sanctuary area = wet savannah grasses

8. International Species & Geological News

Poas Volcano: Active volcano in Costa Rica (inside Poás Volcano National Park); 2,708 m altitude; composite stratovolcano; 1.5 km-wide crater (one of world's largest active craters); 400 sq km base
Saola ("Asian Unicorn"): Critically Endangered; Annamite Mountains (Vietnam–Laos); genome mapped from hunter-collected remains; 50–300 individuals; discovered 1992 (Vietnam Forestry + WWF joint survey)
Blyde Rondavel Flat Gecko: Rediscovered after 34 years in Blyde River Canyon, Mpumalanga, South Africa; first discovered 1991; IUCN = Data Deficient; Endangered Wildlife Trust (EWT) confirmed rediscovery — 5th lost species found by EWT
Jinchuanloong niedu: New genus of dinosaur (eusauropod); fossil found in Gansu Province, China; Middle Jurassic period (~165 million years ago); long-necked herbivore
