Cell Biology, Chlorophyll & Light కణ జీవశాస్త్రం · క్లోరోఫిల్ · కాంతి
English — What Plants Make That Animals Can't Plants and animals share many biochemicals (proteins, fats, sugars), but cellulose (Q1) — the long-chain β-1,4 polymer of glucose that builds plant cell walls — is uniquely synthesized by plants (also by some bacteria/fungi). Animals lack the enzyme cellulose synthase. Q1's answer is therefore cellulose. Inside plant cells, ribosomes (Q5) appear under the electron microscope as clusters or single small dots floating in cytoplasm — these are the protein-synthesis factories (~20 nm; subunits 60S+40S in eukaryotes). Other organelles like Golgi and peroxisomes have characteristic membrane shapes, not point-cluster appearance. Chlorophyll is a chelate compound — a porphyrin ring with a central magnesium ion (Q23 — like Fe in haemoglobin, Cu in haemocyanin). The chlorophyll molecule contains C, H, O, N and Mg — but NOT calcium (Q16).
తెలుగు — మొక్కలు మాత్రమే తయారు చేసేవి మొక్కలు, జంతువులు చాలా జీవరసాయనాలను పంచుకుంటాయి, కానీ సెల్యులోజ్ (Q1) — గ్లూకోజ్‌లతో తయారైన β-1,4 బంధ గొలుసు, మొక్కల కణ గోడలు — మొక్కల్లో మాత్రమే (కొన్ని బ్యాక్టీరియా/శిలీంధ్రాలలో). జంతువులకు cellulose synthase ఎంజైమ్ లేదు. రైబోజోములు (Q5) = ఎలక్ట్రాన్ మైక్రోస్కోప్ కింద చిన్న చుక్కల సముదాయంగా; ప్రోటీన్ సంశ్లేషణ కేంద్రాలు. క్లోరోఫిల్ = పోర్ఫిరిన్ రింగ్‌లో కేంద్ర మెగ్నీషియం అయాన్ (Q23). క్లోరోఫిల్‌లో C, H, O, N, Mg ఉంటాయి కానీ కాల్షియం లేదు (Q16).
English — Light & Photosynthesis (Set 17 angle) Q9 confirms green light is NOT absorbed by chlorophyll — the leaf reflects green wavelengths (~550 nm), which is why we see leaves as green. Q40 echoes this: green light is least effective in photosynthesis because most of it bounces off. Q29 reaffirms that blue (~430 nm) and red (~660 nm) are absorbed — the two peaks of chlorophyll-a's absorption spectrum. The Calvin cycle's first stable product (Q50) is 3-Phosphoglyceric Acid (PGA), a 3-carbon molecule formed when CO₂ is fixed by RuBisCO onto the 5-carbon RuBP. PGA is then phosphorylated and reduced to G3P → glucose → starch. Plants are unmatched in solar energy fixation (Q38) because chlorophyll captures photons and drives the rest of the food chain.
తెలుగు — కాంతి + కిరణజన్యక్రియ Q9: ఆకుపచ్చ కాంతి గ్రహించబడదు; ఆకు ఆ తరంగాన్ని ప్రతిబింబిస్తుంది (అందుకే ఆకుపచ్చగా కనిపిస్తుంది). Q40: ఆకుపచ్చ కాంతిలో కిరణజన్యక్రియ తక్కువ ప్రభావం. Q29: నీలం + ఎరుపు గ్రహించబడతాయి. Q50: మొదటి స్థిర ఉత్పత్తి = PGA (3-ఫాస్ఫోగ్లిసరిక్ ఆమ్లం; 3-కార్బన్; RuBisCO ద్వారా ఏర్పడుతుంది). Q38: ఆకుపచ్చ మొక్కలు సౌర శక్తిని అత్యధికంగా స్థిరీకరిస్తాయి.

⚠ PGA = first stable product, NOT glucose
A common trap is to assume photosynthesis directly produces glucose. It does not. The Calvin cycle proceeds: CO₂ + RuBP (5C) → 2 × PGA (3C, first stable product — Q50) → G3P → eventually glucose, starch, sucrose. PGA appears in milliseconds; glucose only after several cycle turns. So in the exam, "first stable product" = PGA / 3-Phosphoglyceric Acid. కిరణజన్యక్రియలో మొదటిది PGA (3 కార్బన్), గ్లూకోజ్ కాదు. RuBP+CO₂ → 2 PGA → G3P → గ్లూకోజ్

Plant Hormones, Movements & Reproduction హార్మోన్లు · చలనాలు · పునరుత్పత్తి
English — Five Hormones, Distinct Roles Plants regulate growth via five hormone classes. Auxin (IAA) drives cell elongation and the bending of shoots toward light — phototropism (Q37). When light hits one side of a stem, auxin migrates to the shaded side, causing those cells to elongate more, bending the shoot toward the light. Gibberellins = stem elongation, seed germination. Cytokinins (also called "kinins") = cell division (Q21) — they trigger mitosis and delay senescence; widely used in tissue culture. Ethylene (gas) = fruit ripening; that's why one ripe banana ripens nearby green fruits. Abscisic acid (ABA) = leaf abscission, stomatal closure under drought, seed dormancy. The "anti-growth" hormone. So the answers: phototropism = Auxin (Q37); cell division = Kinins/Cytokinins (Q21); leaf shedding = ABA (cf. Set 16 Q14).
తెలుగు — 5 హార్మోన్లు, ప్రత్యేక పాత్రలు ఆక్సిన్ (IAA) = కణ పొడిగింపు, ఫోటోట్రోపిజం (Q37). కాంతి ఒక వైపు నుండి వచ్చినప్పుడు, ఆక్సిన్ నీడ వైపుకు వెళ్ళి ఆ వైపు కణాలను ఎక్కువ పొడిగించి, కాండం కాంతి వైపు వంగుతుంది. జిబరెల్లిన్ = కాండం పొడిగింపు. సైటోకైనిన్/కైనిన్ = కణ విభజన (Q21). ఇథిలీన్ = పండు పక్వత. ABA = ఆకు రాలడం, స్టోమాటా మూసివేత.
English — Pollination & Reproduction Pollination has multiple modes. Autogamy = pollen on same flower's stigma. Allogamy (Q15) = cross-pollination — pollen from one flower lands on another flower's stigma (often on a different plant). Cleistogamy = pollination inside an unopened flower (guaranteed self-pollination, e.g., Commelina). Chasmogamy = open flower with insect/wind cross-pollen. After successful pollination + fertilisation, an ovary normally becomes a fruit. But sometimes a fruit can form WITHOUT fertilisation — this is parthenocarpy (Q14), producing seedless fruits like banana, pineapple, seedless watermelon. Parthenocarpy is induced commercially with hormones (auxin, gibberellin) for seedless oranges, grapes, watermelons (Q22 — but mango parthenocarpy is impractical because mango pulp is firmly attached to the seed; source key marks mango as having "no advantage" from seedlessness). Q19 says vegetative methods (grafting, cuttings, budding) are best for maintaining fruit quality — they are clones, so they preserve the elite parent's traits. Q7 reaffirms: fruit trees are propagated by grafting.

⚠ Q22 — Why "no advantage" in seedless mango
Seedless varieties are profitable for grapes (Q22 distractor), watermelon, citrus — fruits where the seed is a chewing nuisance. Mango pulp clings to the seed (stone), so making it seedless is biologically tough AND commercially pointless: people scoop pulp around the seed easily anyway. Hence the source key picks mango (Q22) as having no advantage in seedless production. Note: technically you could engineer it, but no major commercial demand exists. Plant Classification & Ecology వర్గీకరణ & ఆవరణ శాస్త్రం
English — Plant Groups Recap Plants are split first into Cryptogams (no flowers, "hidden reproduction") and Phanerogams (Q13) (visible reproduction — they produce flowers and seeds). Cryptogams include algae, bryophytes, pteridophytes; phanerogams include gymnosperms (naked-seeded conifers) and angiosperms (flowering plants). Pteridophytes (Q30) are the first vascular plants — they have xylem and phloem but reproduce by spores, not seeds. Examples: ferns, horsetails (Equisetum), lycophytes (Selaginella). They were dominant during the Carboniferous (~350 mya), forming today's coal deposits. Among phyla of animals, Porifera (sponges) is the OLDEST living metazoan group (Q31) — fossils ~580 million years old. Sponges have no true tissues; they filter feed via choanocytes. The blood-sucking leech (Q2) belongs to phylum Annelida (segmented worms — same as earthworm); leeches secrete hirudin, a powerful anticoagulant. Plants by water habitat: Halophytes = salt soils; Xerophytes (Q41) = drought (cactus, acacia); Heliophytes (Q36) = sun-loving (sunflower, most crops); Sciophytes = shade-loving (ferns, mosses). The insectivorous plant Drosera (Q34) ("sundew") traps insects on sticky tentacles to obtain nitrogen — common in N-poor bog soils. Other carnivores: Nepenthes (pitcher), Venus flytrap, Utricularia (bladderwort).

⚠ Q31 — Why sponges win "oldest metazoan"
"Metazoan" = multicellular animals. Phylum Porifera (sponges) are the basal-most metazoan branch — their fossils predate Cambrian (~580 mya, before complex bilaterians). They have NO tissues, no organs, no symmetry; just choanocytes that pump water through pores. Sycon (option C) is one sponge GENUS, but the broader answer is Sponges (Q31). Don't pick anemones (Cnidaria, came later) or hydra (also Cnidaria). పోరిఫెరా (స్పంజికలు) = ~58 కోట్ల సంవత్సరాల పురాతన మెటాజోవాన్; కణజాలాలు, అవయవాలు లేవు; కేవలం పోర్‌లు + కోనోసైట్‌లు. సైకాన్ ఒక genus మాత్రమే; సాధారణ జవాబు = స్పంజికలు

English — Plants for Medicine, Food & Industry Quinine — the classic anti-malarial — comes from the bark of Cinchona (Q17) tree, native to South American Andes; British colonial expansion was made possible by quinine controlling malaria. Don't confuse with Rauwolfia serpentina (sarpagandha) which gives reserpine for hypertension. Saffron (Q42), the world's most expensive spice (~$10,000/kg), is the dried stigma of the autumn-flowering crocus Crocus sativus; each flower yields just 3 stigmas, requiring ~150,000 flowers for 1 kg. India's saffron region: Pampore, Kashmir. Cinnamon (Q43) = inner bark of Cinnamomum verum (Sri Lanka) or C. cassia (China); strips are dried into "quills". Soybean (Q10) is emphasised because of its 40% high-quality protein with all 9 essential amino acids — rare in plants; vital for vegetarian diets and replacing meat protein. Eucalyptus (Q11) is controversial in India because it depletes groundwater (very high transpiration; deep tap roots) — banned/discouraged in some states despite quick timber yield. Azolla (Q39) is a small water fern that hosts nitrogen-fixing cyanobacterium Anabaena in its leaf cavities; flooded into paddy fields, Azolla doubles in 2-3 days and adds N → boosts rice yield. Nerium (Q25) (oleander) is the only listed tree that does NOT absorb SO₂ from air; the others (Ilex, Jhar, Neem) actively scavenge sulfur dioxide. English — Carbohydrate Storage & Sugars Carbohydrates exist in three forms relevant to exams. Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆) = single-ring monosaccharide; main blood sugar; "grape sugar" or dextrose; ripe grapes (Q46) are particularly rich in glucose (also fructose). Sucrose (Q35) = glucose + fructose disaccharide = the white "table sugar" we cook with; from sugar cane / beet. Glycogen (Q45) = highly branched glucose polymer; the animal storage form (in liver and muscle of humans), structurally similar to plant starch but more branched. Plants store carbohydrate as starch; animals/humans store it as glycogen. Other sugars: fructose (fruit sugar, sweetest); galactose (milk sugar component); lactose (milk = glu+gal); maltose (malt = 2 glucose). The iodine test (Q47) is specific to starch (turns blue-black) — different from Fehling's/Benedict's (which detect reducing sugars).
తెలుగు — కార్బోహైడ్రేట్ నిల్వ + చక్కెరలు గ్లూకోజ్ = ఒక మోనోశాకరైడ్; "ద్రాక్ష చక్కెర"; ద్రాక్షలో సమృద్ధం (Q46). సుక్రోజ్ (Q35) = గ్లూకోజ్ + ఫ్రక్టోజ్ = పంచదార. గ్లైకోజెన్ (Q45) = జంతు నిల్వ రూపం (కాలేయం, కండరాలలో); మొక్కల్లో పిండిపదార్థంగా (starch). ఇతరాలు: ఫ్రక్టోజ్ (పండు), గెలాక్టోజ్ (పాల), లాక్టోజ్ (పాలు), మాల్టోజ్ (మాల్ట్). అయోడిన్ పరీక్ష (Q47) = స్టార్చ్‌కు ప్రత్యేకం (నీలి-నలుపు).
🧠 Memory — Carbohydrate Storage Forms
Plants store carbohydrates as STARCH (long unbranched + branched amylose/amylopectin chains in plastids).
Animals/Humans store carbohydrates as GLYCOGEN (Q45) (highly branched; in liver + muscle).
Both are α-glucose polymers; cellulose (Q1) is β-glucose, indigestible by humans (we have no β-glucosidase enzyme — that's why we can't digest fibre, but cellulose-eating cattle have gut microbes that can). మొక్కలు = స్టార్చ్; జంతువులు = గ్లైకోజెన్ (Q45). మూడూ గ్లూకోజ్ పాలిమర్‌లే; సెల్యులోజ్‌ను మానవులు జీర్ణం చేయలేరు (β-గ్లూకోజ్ బంధం)

Seeds, Germination & Plant Records విత్తనాలు · మొలకెత్తడం · రికార్డులు
English — How a Seed Wakes Up A dormant seed has the lowest metabolism of any plant tissue and the highest resistance to harsh atmospheric conditions (Q6) — frost, drought, heat. Some seeds (lotus) have germinated after 1300 years dormant. To wake up, a seed needs three essentials: water (imbibition swells cells, activates enzymes), oxygen (for aerobic respiration during germination), and suitable temperature (typically 25-30°C). It does NOT need light (Q4, Q28) — most seeds germinate underground in darkness; only a few "photoblastic" seeds (lettuce, mistletoe) need light. So both Q4 and Q28 mark light/sunlight as not required for germination. The dormant seed protects against atmospheric extremes via a hard, often waxy seed coat (testa) and reduced water content (10-15%).
తెలుగు — విత్తనం ఎలా మొలకెత్తుతుంది నిద్రాణ విత్తనం = అతి తక్కువ జీవక్రియ + గరిష్ట ప్రతిఘటన (Q6). కొన్ని విత్తనాలు (తామర) 1300 సంవత్సరాలుగా నిద్రాణం పైగా ఉండి తర్వాత మొలకెత్తాయి. మొలకెత్తడానికి 3 అవసరాలు: నీరు, ఆక్సిజన్, తగిన ఉష్ణోగ్రత. కాంతి అవసరం లేదు (Q4, Q28) — చాలా విత్తనాలు చీకటిలో మొలకెత్తుతాయి. కేవలం కొన్ని ఫోటోబ్లాస్టిక్ విత్తనాలకు (లెట్యూస్) కాంతి అవసరం.

Crop Rotation Logic (Q18): The 4-stage rotation Pea → Barley → Wheat → Potato is designed for soil-fertility upkeep. Pea (legume) hosts Rhizobium nodules that fix atmospheric N₂ → enriches soil. Barley (cereal) is light-feeding; uses some of the fixed N. Wheat (cereal, heavy feeder) consumes more nutrients. Potato (heavy feeder + tuber crop) takes K and P; its harvest disturbs/aerates soil. After potato, repeat with legume to replenish N. The general principle: ALWAYS START with a legume after exhausting cereals; the cycle keeps soil productive without heavy synthetic fertiliser. పంట మార్పిడి (Q18): లెగ్యూమ్ (బఠానీ) → సైరియల్ (బార్లీ, గోధుమ) → దుంప (బంగాళదుంప). లెగ్యూమ్ నైట్రోజన్ స్థిరీకరణ; సైరియల్/దుంపలు దానిని వాడుకుంటాయి. ప్రతి చక్రం తర్వాత లెగ్యూమ్‌తో పునరావృతం
