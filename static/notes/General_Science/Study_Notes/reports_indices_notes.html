📊 Reports & Indices 2024–2026 — AP High Court Section Officer Study Notes

1. PRESS FREEDOM & MEDIA INDICES

World Press Freedom Index 2025

Published by: RSF (Reporters Sans Frontières / Reporters Without Borders)
India's Rank: 151 out of 180 countries
Top Countries: 1. Finland  |  2. Estonia  |  3. Netherlands
USA Rank: 57  |  Bottom: North Korea (180)
India slipped in the 2025 index due to concerns over media concentration, digital restrictions, and journalist safety. RSF is headquartered in Paris, France.

Freedom of the Press Index (Freedom House)

Published by: Freedom House (US-based NGO)
Separate from RSF index; categorises countries as Free, Partly Free, Not Free.

2. FOOD SECURITY & HUNGER REPORTS

Global Report on Food Crises (GRFC) 2025

Published by: GNAFC (Global Network Against Food Crises) + FSIN (Food Security Information Network)
Acute Hungry: 295 million people in 53 countries/territories
Increase: +13.7 million vs 2023 (281.6M in 53 countries)
Key causes: Conflict, climate extremes, economic shocks
Top affected: Sudan, Gaza, South Sudan, Haiti, Mali
The GNAFC was established in 2016. FSIN is co-chaired by FAO, WFP, and IFPRI.

Global Hunger Index (GHI)

Published by: Concern Worldwide (Ireland) + Welthungerhilfe (Germany)
India 2024: Rank 105 out of 127 countries — "Serious" category
Measures: Undernourishment, child wasting, child stunting, child mortality

State of Food Security and Nutrition in the World (SOFI)

Published by: FAO, IFAD, UNICEF, WFP, WHO (joint report)
Annual report tracking progress toward Zero Hunger (SDG 2)

3. CLIMATE & ENERGY INDICES

Climate Change Performance Index (CCPI) 2025

Published by: Germanwatch + NewClimate Institute + Climate Action Network (CAN) International
India's Rank: 10th (among the best)
Top Rank (4th): Denmark (Ranks 1–3 intentionally left empty — "no country performs sufficiently in all categories")
India's scores: GHG = High | Energy use = High | Climate policy = Medium | Renewables = Low
Total countries assessed: 63 + EU
India consistently ranks high on CCPI due to its low per-capita emissions despite having a large population. The three top positions are always left vacant because no country does "enough."

World Energy Investment Report 2025

Published by: IEA (International Energy Agency)
Global investment: $3.3 trillion
Clean energy: $2.2 trillion = double fossil fuels ($1.1T)
China leads global clean energy investment
First time clean energy investment = 2× fossil fuel investment globally

State and Trends of Carbon Pricing 2025

Published by: World Bank
Coverage: 28% of global GHG emissions
Revenue: Over $100 billion generated
Instruments: 80 active carbon pricing instruments (up from just 5 in 2005)
Includes: Emissions Trading Systems (ETS) and carbon taxes

Renewable Energy Statistics 2025 (IRENA)

Published by: IRENA (International Renewable Energy Agency, HQ: Abu Dhabi)
Global renewable capacity continues to grow — solar and wind dominate new additions.

4. SOCIAL & HUMAN DEVELOPMENT REPORTS

Social Security Coverage — India 2nd Globally (ILO)

Source: ILO ILOSTAT database
India's Rank: 2nd globally for absolute number covered
People covered: ~94 crore (940 million)
Coverage jump: 19% (2015) → 64.3% (2025) — fastest expansion in the world
Key schemes: PM-JAY, ESIC, EPFO, PM Kisan, MGNREGS

UNFPA – State of World Population (SOWP) 2025

Published by: UNFPA (United Nations Population Fund)
Theme 2025: "The Real Fertility Crisis"
Key finding: Both very high and very low fertility rates pose challenges; one-sided narrative on "population bomb" is misleading.
India overtook China as world's most populous country in 2023.

World Population Prospects (UN DESA)

Published by: UN DESA (Department of Economic and Social Affairs)
World population: ~8.2 billion (2025)

UNHCR – Global Trends: Forced Displacement 2024

Published by: UNHCR (UN Refugee Agency)
Total displaced: 123.2 million people (record high)
Children: 40% of all forcibly displaced
Internally Displaced Persons (IDPs): 73.5 million
Top source countries: Sudan, Syria, Afghanistan, Ukraine, Democratic Republic of Congo

Global Education Monitoring (GEM) Report

Published by: UNESCO
Key stat: 272 million children out of school worldwide
Focus: Inclusive and equitable quality education (SDG 4)

5. ECONOMIC & FINANCIAL REPORTS

UN World Economic Situation and Prospects (WESP) 2025

Published by: UN DESA (Department of Economic and Social Affairs)
Global GDP growth: 2.4%
India GDP growth: 6.3% (fastest growing major economy)
China GDP growth: ~4.5%
USA GDP growth: ~1.9%

World of Debt Report (UNCTAD)

Published by: UNCTAD (UN Conference on Trade and Development)
Global debt: $102 trillion (record high)
Developing countries face increasing debt burden; calls for debt restructuring mechanisms.

World Economic Outlook (IMF)

Published by: IMF (International Monetary Fund)
Released twice a year (April + October)
India consistently projected as fastest-growing major economy.

Global Financial Stability Report (IMF)

Published by: IMF — assesses stability risks in global financial system.

Ease of Doing Business (World Bank)

Published by: World Bank (discontinued 2021 due to data irregularities)
Replaced by: Business Ready (B-READY) Report — first edition released 2024.

6. FISHERIES & MARINE RESOURCES

Review of the State of World Marine Fishery Resources 2025

Published by: FAO (Food and Agriculture Organization)
Sustainable stocks: 64.5%
Overfished stocks: 35.5%
Deep-sea stocks sustainable: 29%
The report calls for stronger management to reverse overfishing trends.

The State of World Fisheries and Aquaculture (SOFIA)

Published by: FAO (released every 2 years)
Global fish production: ~185 million tonnes; aquaculture now exceeds wild capture.

7. MILITARY & NUCLEAR REPORTS

SIPRI Yearbook 2025 (Military Expenditure & Nuclear Forces)

Published by: SIPRI (Stockholm International Peace Research Institute)
Russia nuclear warheads: 5,459
USA nuclear warheads: 5,177
Global military spending: Record high ~$2.4 trillion (2024)
Largest spenders: USA, China, Russia, India, UK
India's military budget: ~$83.6 billion (4th globally)
SIPRI is headquartered in Solna (Stockholm), Sweden. India is among the top military spenders.

Global Peace Index (GPI)

Published by: IEP (Institute for Economics and Peace), Sydney, Australia
Measures: Internal peace, external peace, militarization
Iceland is consistently ranked most peaceful country.

8. SDG & GOVERNANCE INDICES

India SDG Index 2025

Published by: NITI Aayog
India's Global Rank: 99th — first time in top 100
SDG Score improved: 57 (2018) → 71 (2025)
Top performing states: Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Himachal Pradesh

Performance Grading Index (PGI) 2.0 – School Education

Published by: Ministry of Education, Government of India
Top performer: Chandigarh (UT)
Bottom performer: Meghalaya
Parameters: 1000 points across 73 indicators in 6 domains
Domains: Learning Outcomes, Access, Infrastructure, Equity, Governance, Digital Initiatives

Democracy Index (Economist Intelligence Unit)

Published by: EIU (Economist Intelligence Unit)
Categorises countries as: Full Democracy, Flawed Democracy, Hybrid Regime, Authoritarian
India: "Flawed Democracy" — rank ~41

Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI)

Published by: Transparency International (Berlin, Germany)
India's Rank 2024: 93 out of 180
Top (least corrupt): Denmark, Finland, Singapore
Scale: 0 (highly corrupt) → 100 (very clean)

Global Innovation Index (GII)

Published by: WIPO (World Intellectual Property Organization)
India's Rank 2024: 39th
India has improved consistently from 81st (2015) to 39th (2024)
Top: Switzerland, Sweden, USA, UK, Singapore

9. HUMAN DEVELOPMENT & GENDER INDICES

Human Development Index (HDI) 2025

Published by: UNDP (United Nations Development Programme)
India's Rank: 134 out of 193 countries — "Medium Human Development"
Top: Switzerland, Norway, Iceland
Measures: Life expectancy + Education + GNI per capita

Gender Inequality Index (GII)

Published by: UNDP
India's Rank: ~108 — reflects gender disparities in health, empowerment, labour market

Gender Gap Report

Published by: World Economic Forum (WEF), Davos, Switzerland
India's Rank 2024: 129 out of 146 countries
Measures 4 pillars: Economic participation, Education attainment, Health, Political empowerment
Iceland tops consistently.

World Happiness Report

Published by: UN Sustainable Development Solutions Network (UNSDSN)
India 2025: ~118
Top: Finland (for 8th consecutive year in 2025)
Based on: Gallup World Poll data (life evaluation)

Global Competitiveness Index (GCI)

11. HEALTH & WELL-BEING REPORTS

World Health Statistics (WHO)

Published by: WHO (World Health Organization, Geneva, Switzerland)
Annual publication tracking global health indicators and SDG 3 progress.

Global TB Report (WHO)

Published by: WHO
India: Highest TB burden country globally (~26% of global TB cases)
India's target: Eliminate TB by 2025 (5 years ahead of global SDG target of 2030)

National Family Health Survey (NFHS-5)

Published by: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India
Data collected: 2019–21 | Key findings:
India's TFR (Total Fertility Rate): 2.0 (below replacement level of 2.1 — first time)
IMR (Infant Mortality Rate): 35.2 per 1,000 live births
Full immunisation: 76.4%
BMI > 25 (overweight): 24% women, 23% men

14. EXAM TIPS & MEMORY AIDS

🧠 Memory Trick — "GNAFC Feed 295 Million"
GNAFC = Global Network Against Food Crises | GRFC = the report | 295M in 53 countries (2025)
🧠 RSF vs Freedom House
RSF = Press Freedom Index (Paris, France)
Freedom House = Freedom of the Press Report (Washington DC, USA)
🧠 CCPI Top 3 are EMPTY
Positions 1–3 are always intentionally vacant. Denmark = 4th. India = 10th (high performer!)
🧠 IEA = Energy (Paris) | IRENA = Renewables (Abu Dhabi)
🧠 India Rankings to Remember:
Press Freedom: 151 | CCPI: 10 | GII: 39 | HDI: 134 | Gender Gap: 129 | CPI: 93 | Social Security: 2nd | SDG Index: 99
⚠️ Common Confusions:
• GHI (Global Hunger Index) ≠ GRFC (Global Report on Food Crises) — different publishers!
• SIPRI = military/nuclear data ≠ UNHCR = displacement data
• UNCTAD = trade/debt ≠ UNDP = human development
• PGI = school education (Chandigarh tops, not Delhi!)
