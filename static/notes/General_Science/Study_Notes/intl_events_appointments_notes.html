🌍 International Events, Appointments & Developments 2024–2026
AP High Court Section Officer Study Notes

1. GLOBAL ELECTIONS & LEADERSHIP CHANGES 2024–2025

United States Presidential Election 2024

Result: Donald Trump won — elected 47th President of USA (Nov 5, 2024)
Defeated: Kamala Harris (Democrat)
Inauguration: January 20, 2025
VP: JD Vance
Key policies: "Liberation Day" reciprocal tariffs (April 2, 2025); expressed desire to acquire Greenland and Panama Canal

United Kingdom General Elections 2024

Result: Labour Party won landslide (July 4, 2024)
New PM: Keir Starmer (succeeded Rishi Sunak)
India-UK FTA: Free Trade Agreement signed May 6, 2025 — major milestone after 3 years of negotiations

Japan – First Female Prime Minister

New PM: Sanae Takaichi — elected October 21, 2025
First female PM of Japan; 104th Prime Minister
Party: Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) + Japan Innovation Party (Ishin) coalition
Succeeded Shigeru Ishiba

Canada – New Prime Minister 2025

New PM: Mark Carney (Liberal Party, March 14, 2025)
Succeeded Justin Trudeau (who resigned January 2025)
Former Governor of Bank of Canada and Bank of England

Germany – New Chancellor 2025

New Chancellor: Friedrich Merz (CDU/CSU)
Won federal elections February 23, 2025
Succeeded Olaf Scholz (SPD coalition collapsed)

South Korea – Presidential Impeachment

President Yoon Suk-yeol declared martial law on December 3, 2024
Impeached by National Assembly on December 14, 2024
Constitutional Court upheld impeachment April 4, 2025
New President: Lee Jae-myung (Democratic Party) won June 3, 2025 snap election

Nepal – First Woman Interim PM

Sushila Karki sworn as interim PM of Nepal (September 2025)
Nepal's first woman Chief Justice (2016–17); known for anti-corruption stance
Succeeded KP Sharma Oli who resigned after mass protests

Jamaica – PM Re-elected

Andrew Holness won 3rd term as PM of Jamaica (September 2025)
Jamaica Labour Party (JLP) won 34 of 63 seats
Crime reduction: 43% drop in killings in 2025

Bangladesh – Sheikh Hasina Resignation

Sheikh Hasina resigned and fled to India on August 5, 2024 after student-led mass protests
Muhammad Yunus — Nobel laureate economist — heads Bangladesh's interim government
Yunus invited by Bangladesh's Army Chief and student leaders

Vietnam – New Party General Secretary

To Lam became Vietnam's Communist Party General Secretary (2024)
Succeeded Nguyen Phu Trong who died July 2024

2. KEY INTERNATIONAL EVENTS 2024–2025

Pope Francis Passes Away

Pope Francis died on April 21, 2025 at age 88
First Latin American pope (Jorge Mario Bergoglio; Argentinian)
Served from 2013 to 2025

New Pope Elected – Pope Leo XIV

Pope Leo XIV elected on May 8, 2025
Birth name: Robert Francis Prevost
First American pope in history (born Chicago, USA)
First Augustinian to become pope; previously served as Bishop in Peru

Syria – Fall of Assad Regime

Bashar al-Assad fled Syria on December 8, 2024
Rebel coalition led by HTS (Hayat Tahrir al-Sham) captured Damascus
HTS leader: Ahmed al-Sharaa (Abu Mohammad al-Jolani) leads interim government
Assad fled to Russia; ending 54 years of Assad family rule

Gaza Ceasefire 2025

Phase 1 ceasefire agreed on January 15, 2025 (began January 19, 2025)
Mediated by: Qatar, Egypt, and the United States
Between Israel and Hamas; involved hostage–prisoner exchange
Conflict began after Hamas attack on Israel (October 7, 2023)

India – Operation Sindoor

India launched Operation Sindoor on May 7, 2025
Precision strikes on 9 terror infrastructure sites in Pakistan and PoK
Response to Pahalgam terrorist attack (April 22, 2025) in which 26 tourists were killed
India-Pakistan ceasefire announced May 10, 2025

BRICS Expansion – New Members January 2024

New BRICS members (officially joined January 1, 2024):
Saudi Arabia, UAE, Egypt, Iran, Ethiopia
BRICS Summit 2024: Kazan, Russia (October 2024)
BRICS now = Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa + 5 new = 10 members

Russia Formally Recognises Taliban

Russia = first country to formally recognise Taliban government (July 3, 2025)
Removed Taliban from list of outlawed organisations
Afghanistan Taliban FM: Amir Khan Muttaqi visited Delhi (India's engagement)

3. INDIA'S INTERNATIONAL ENGAGEMENT 2024–2025

Operation Sagar Bandhu

India launched Operation Sagar Bandhu — HADR mission to Sri Lanka
After Cyclone Ditwah (landfall Nov 27, 2025)
IAF delivered ~12 tonnes of relief (tents, blankets, food)

BHISHM Cubes to Maldives

PM of India gifted 2 BHISHM Cubes to Maldivian President Mohamed Muizzu
On Maldives' 60th Independence Day
BHISHM = Bharat Health Initiative for Sahyog, Hita & Maitri
Each cube handles 200 emergency cases; includes AI systems, surgical tools

India–China Relations 2025

India–China resumed Kailash Mansarovar Yatra in 2025 (after 5 years)
Also: restored visas, direct flights, water-data sharing on Brahmaputra
Part of post-Galwan rapprochement begun late 2024

India–UK Free Trade Agreement

India-UK FTA signed May 6, 2025
Covers goods, services, digital trade, investment
3-year negotiations concluded (2022–2025)

India & Deep Sea Minerals

India signed 15-year contract with ISA (International Seabed Authority) for polymetallic sulphides in Indian Ocean
India = first country to hold two ISA contracts for polymetallic sulphides
Contains copper, zinc, gold, silver (for clean energy applications)

India Interpol Representation

CBI (Central Bureau of Investigation) = India's national central bureau for Interpol
India elected to Interpol Asian Committee at 25th Asian Regional Conference in Singapore

India at ICAO

India re-elected to Part II of ICAO Council (states contributing most to civil air navigation)
India = founding member of ICAO since 1944 — 81 years of uninterrupted Council membership

India Hosted IEC General Meeting

India hosted 89th General Meeting of IEC (International Electrotechnical Commission)
Venue: Bharat Mandapam, New Delhi (September 15–19, 2025)
4th time India hosted (previous: 1960, 1997, 2013)

India–Brazil Agritech (Maitri 2.0)

ICAR launched Maitri 2.0 (Brazil–India Cross-Incubation Programme in Agritech)
Builds on 77-year agricultural partnership; aligns with BRICS and G20

LeadIT – India–Sweden Initiative

LeadIT (Leadership Group for Industry Transition) launched 2019 by India and Sweden
Supported by WEF; targets net-zero emissions from heavy industries by 2050
Secretariat: Stockholm Environment Institute; 18 countries + 27 companies
LeadIT 2.0 launched at COP28; discussed at COP30 in Belém, Brazil

4. INTERNATIONAL ORGANISATIONS IN NEWS

WHO Developments

WHO declared polio outbreak in Papua New Guinea (2025)
WHO certified Suriname malaria-free (June 30, 2025) — first Amazon country
WHO World Health Assembly (WHA) passed resolution on Skin Diseases as Global Public Health Priority (1.9 billion people affected)

IAEA

IAEA HQ: Vienna, Austria | 178 member countries
Statute approved October 23, 1956; came into force July 29, 1957
India: calls for Pakistan nuclear weapons to be monitored by IAEA

ICAO

ICAO HQ: Montreal, Canada | UN specialized agency
Established under Chicago Convention of 1944
India performing above global average in ICAO safety audit

UNESCO – New Director General

Khaled el-Anani (former Egyptian Minister of Tourism and Antiquities) nominated as next UNESCO Director-General
UNESCO HQ: Paris, France | 194 members + 12 associates

UNODC

UNODC HQ: Vienna, Austria
Established 1997 (merged UN Drug Control Programme + Centre for International Crime Prevention)
Joint WHO-UNODC report: contaminated medicines killed 1,300+ people in 90 years

New Development Bank (NDB)

NDB = established by BRICS (Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa)
HQ: Shanghai, China
Also known as BRICS Development Bank; funds infrastructure in developing nations

MERCOSUR

MERCOSUR = Southern Common Market | HQ: Montevideo, Uruguay
Founded 1991 by Treaty of Asunción
Original members: Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay, Uruguay
India discussing expansion of Preferential Trade Agreement with MERCOSUR

SICA

SICA = Central American Integration System
Founded 1991 via Tegucigalpa Protocol
India–SICA Foreign Ministers' Meeting held in New York
Areas: trade, digital payments, agriculture, renewables, IT

International Seabed Authority (ISA)

ISA governs deep-sea mineral exploration in international waters
India holds two ISA contracts for polymetallic sulphides (Indian Ocean)

IEC

IEC (International Electrotechnical Commission) | HQ: Geneva, Switzerland
Established 1906; publishes standards for electrical/electronic technologies
India hosted 89th GM at Bharat Mandapam (September 2025)

WTO Director General

Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala (Nigeria) = WTO Director General, reappointed for 2nd term through 2027
First African and first woman to lead WTO

IMF Managing Director

Kristalina Georgieva (Bulgaria) = IMF Managing Director (renewed through 2027)

5. COUNTRIES IN NEWS

China – Space Missions

Tianwen-2 launched on Long March 3-B rocket to collect samples from asteroid 2016HO3
Mission also to explore main-belt comet 311P; continues till end of 2027
Tianwen-3: Mars sample return ~2028 | Tianwen-4: Jupiter ~2030

Iran – Nuclear & Gas

Bushehr = Iran's only operational nuclear plant (built by Russia, 2011)
South Pars gas field = world's largest gas field; jointly owned by Iran and Qatar
Iran struck by Israel during Iran-Israel tensions 2025
Iran = world's 3rd largest gas producer (after USA and Russia; ~275 billion m³/yr)

Israel – Samson Option

Samson Option = Israel's alleged doctrine of massive nuclear retaliation if facing existential threat
Amimut policy = deliberate ambiguity (Israel neither confirms nor denies nuclear weapons)
Named after biblical Samson

Taiwan – Natural Disasters

Typhoon Danas made rare direct landfall in Taiwan (Chiayi County — first recorded typhoon there)
Gusts up to 222 km/h

Indonesia – Volcano

Mount Lewotobi Laki-laki erupted in Indonesia (Flores island, East Nusa Tenggara)
Active stratovolcano; part of twin-peaked Lewotobi complex
Located in Pacific Ring of Fire; alert raised to highest level

Papua New Guinea – Polio

WHO declared polio outbreak in Papua New Guinea
Country was polio-free since 2000; returned due to low vaccination rates
Poliovirus found in healthy children in Lae city

Japan – AI Law 2025

Japan passed Act on Promotion of Research, Development and Utilisation of AI-Related Technologies (May 2025)
Flexible approach vs EU AI Act (2024) which has strict risk-level regulation

Hawaii – Green Fee

Hawaii = first US state to introduce Green Fee (climate impact fee)
Applies to hotel stays, short-term rentals, and cruise ship passengers
Funds climate resilience, conservation, invasive species control

Saudi Arabia – Tourism

Saudi Arabia launched TOURISE — global platform to redefine tourism for next 50 years
Inaugural TOURISE Summit: Riyadh, November 2025
Includes digital collaboration, TOURISE Awards for sustainability

Jakarta – Most Populous City 2025

Jakarta = world's most populous city (42 million) — UN World Urbanisation Prospects 2025
Dhaka = 2nd (37M) | Tokyo = 3rd (33M)
Global megacities: 33 (most in Asia); urban population = ~50% of world

6. KEY AGREEMENTS & INITIATIVES

EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM)

CBAM = EU policy to price carbon in imports (steel, aluminium, cement etc.)
Part of EU's "Fit for 55" strategy (55% GHG reduction by 2030)
Transitional phase: 2023–2025; Full enforcement: 2026
India has expressed deep reservations

Blue NDC Challenge – UNOC3

France and Brazil launched Blue NDC Challenge at 3rd UN Ocean Conference (UNOC3)
Urges countries to put oceans at centre of climate strategies ahead of COP30 (Belém, Brazil)

NATO BALTOPS 25

NATO annual exercise BALTOPS 25 (Baltic Operations 2025) held in Latvia
Focuses on rapid-response, interoperability across Baltic Sea region

UK-France One-In-One-Out Migration Deal

Migration agreement: UK returns illegal Channel crossers to France
For each returned migrant, UK grants asylum to one eligible migrant from France with family ties
An Indian migrant was the first person deported under this scheme

Russia – Closed Fuel Cycle Nuclear by 2030

Putin announced Russia will launch world's first nuclear power system with closed fuel cycle by 2030
Location: Tomsk Region; reuses up to 95% of spent fuel, minimising waste
Announced at World Atomic Week (Moscow); IAEA chief Rafael Grossi was present

SoLAR Phase II

SoLAR Phase II = Solar Irrigation for Agricultural Resilience in South Asia
Launched by IWMI (International Water Management Institute) + SDC (Swiss Agency for Development and Cooperation)
Countries: India, Bangladesh, Kenya, Ethiopia
Builds on Phase I (2019–2024)

Codex Alimentarius Commission

Codex Alimentarius Commission (CAC) = global body for international food standards
Established jointly by FAO and WHO
India's role appreciated at 88th Executive Committee session (CCEXEC 88) in Rome, July 2025
India leading work on Millets Group Standards
