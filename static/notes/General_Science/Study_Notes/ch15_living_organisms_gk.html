Microbiology — Pioneers, Bacteria & Viruses సూక్ష్మజీవశాస్త్రం · పయొనీర్లు
English — A Brief History of Microbes In 1674, Dutch draper-turned-scientist Anton van Leeuwenhoek (Q1) examined a drop of pond water under his self-made microscope (270× magnification) and described Spirogyra alga — naming the moving microbes "animalcules" ("little animals"). He is the father of microbiology. Later milestones: Robert Hooke coined the word "cell" (1665, cork); Louis Pasteur germ theory (1860s); Robert Koch discovered TB and cholera bacilli; Alexander Fleming discovered penicillin (1928). Bacteria are classified by Gram stain — Gram-positive bacteria like Staphylococcus aureus (Q4) have a thick peptidoglycan + teichoic acid wall that holds the violet dye. Hormones are absent in bacteria (Q5) — they regulate by quorum sensing, not endocrine signalling. HIV (Q11) is considered "alive" because it can auto-reproduce inside human host cells. The characteristic feature of all viruses (Q16) is they multiply only on hosts — they have no metabolism of their own. AIDS (Q37) attacks the immune system: HIV destroys CD4⁺ T-helper cells, leaving the body open to opportunistic infections (TB, pneumonia). Chlorella (Q22), a unicellular green alga, is used in space programs to regulate oxygen and recycle CO₂.
తెలుగు — సూక్ష్మజీవి చరిత్ర 1674లో డచ్ శాస్త్రవేత్త ఆంటోనీ వాన్ లీవెన్‌హుక్ (Q1) చెరువు నీటిని తన మైక్రోస్కోప్‌తో పరీశీలించి, Spirogyra ను వర్ణించి, కదిలే సూక్ష్మజీవులను "animalcules" అని పిలిచారు — సూక్ష్మజీవశాస్త్ర పితామహుడు. Staphylococcus aureus (Q4) = గ్రామ్-పాజిటివ్; మందమైన కణ గోడ. బ్యాక్టీరియాలో హార్మోన్లు లేవు (Q5). HIV = స్వీయ-పునరుత్పత్తి (Q11). వైరస్ లక్షణం = అతిధేయిపై మాత్రమే వృద్ధి (Q16). AIDS = రోగనిరోధక వ్యవస్థను దెబ్బతీస్తుంది (Q37). క్లోరెల్లా = అంతరిక్షంలో O₂ నియంత్రణ (Q22).

Birds, Bird Biology & Special Mammals పక్షులు · ప్రత్యేక క్షీరదాలు
English — Birds The largest living bird is the ostrich (Q2) — Africa's flightless ratite, weighing up to 150 kg. Birds achieve flight by flapping wings (Q3) — flapping creates both lift (upward force) and thrust (forward push). Wing aerofoil shape uses Bernoulli effect for additional lift. The cerebellum (Q14) is the brain region for balance and coordination — destroying a pigeon's cerebellum stops it flying (cannot coordinate wing muscles). Webbed feet (Q21) belong to the duck; the skin between toes acts as paddles for swimming — also seen in geese, pelicans, frogs. A common trap: Plymouth Rock (Q29) is NOT a dog breed — it is an American chicken (poultry) breed; the other options (Rottweiler, Bull Mastiff, Springer Spaniel) ARE all dog breeds.
తెలుగు — పక్షులు ఆస్ట్రిచ్ (Q2) = అతిపెద్ద పక్షి (ఆఫ్రికా; ఎగరదు; 150 kg). రెక్కల కొట్టడం (Q3) వల్ల థ్రస్ట్ + లిఫ్ట్. సెరిబెల్లం (Q14) = సమతుల్యత / సమన్వయం. ఈత పాదాలు = బాతు (Q21). ప్లైమౌత్ రాక్ (Q29) = కోడి జాతి (కుక్క జాతి కాదు).
English — Special Mammal Facts Most mammals give birth to live young, but monotremes are the egg-laying exception. The two living species are the spiny ant-eater (Echidna) (Q10) and the duck-billed platypus (Q50) — both Australian; both lay leathery eggs and feed pups with milk through skin pores (no nipples). The mule (Q15) is a sterile hybrid between a female horse + male donkey (mare 64 chr + jack 62 chr → 63-chromosome offspring; meiosis fails). A horse-stallion + female donkey produces a hinny (rarer). Cow gestation (Q8) = 280 days, similar to humans (~280 d). Trypsin (Q23) is the protein-digesting pancreatic enzyme that helps predators digest blood/meat. Guinea pig (Q25) is a short-tailed rodent widely used in scientific labs (and famously cannot make Vit C — see ch14 Q46). The deer (Q27) is NOT a rabid animal — rabies (lyssavirus) infects mammals like dogs, cats, foxes, bats, raccoons; deer rarely transmit it.
తెలుగు — ప్రత్యేక క్షీరదాలు మోనోట్రీమ్‌లు = గుడ్లు పెట్టే క్షీరదాలు. రెండే జాతులు: ఎకిడ్నా (Q10) + డక్-బిల్డ్ ప్లాటిపస్ (Q50). మ్యూల్ (Q15) = ఆడ గుర్రం + మగ గాడిద (వంధ్య హైబ్రిడ్; 63 క్రోమోజోములు). ఆవు గర్భధారణ = 280 రోజులు (Q8). ట్రిప్సిన్ (Q23) = క్లోమ ఎంజైమ్; ప్రోటీన్ జీర్ణం. గినియా పంది (Q25) = ప్రయోగశాలలో. జింక (Q27) = రేబిస్ లేదు.

🧠 Memory — Mule chromosome math
Horse = 64 chr · Donkey = 62 chr · Average = (64 + 62) / 2 = 63 chr = mule. Odd chromosome count → meiosis pairs fail → sterile (cannot reproduce). Mules are valued for their stamina + sure-footedness. Hinny = stallion + female donkey (rarer, smaller). గుర్రం 64 + గాడిద 62 → మ్యూల్ 63 (బేసి సంఖ్య = వంధ్యత్వం). మ్యూల్ = బలం + స్థిరమైన పాదం

Worms, Insects, Symbiosis & Antibodies పురుగులు · కీటకాలు · సహజీవనం
English — Worms & Useful/Harmful Insects Leeches (Q6) are blood-sucking annelids that secrete hirudin, a potent anticoagulant — used historically (and still occasionally) in medicine to drain congested blood. Tapeworm (Taenia solium) (Q9) is a flatworm parasite living in the human intestine; its scolex hooks into intestinal wall, and proglottid segments shed eggs in faeces. Pigs are intermediate hosts. Silk in the silkworm life cycle comes from the cocoon stage (Q7) — the larva spins one continuous thread (~1 km!) of fibroin protein around itself before pupation. Earthworm is a hermaphrodite (Q31) — both male and female reproductive organs in one body; cross-fertilises with another worm. The insect NOT useful to humans (Q26) is the rice weevil — a stored-grain pest. The greatest value of bees to mankind (Q32) is pollination of crop plants (~75% of food crops depend on bee pollination); honey, wax and royal jelly are valuable but secondary.
తెలుగు — పురుగులు + కీటకాలు జలగలు (Q6) = రక్తం పీల్చే అనెలిడ్‌లు; హిరుడిన్ (anticoagulant). తేతువు (Taenia solium) (Q9) = మనిషి ప్రేగులో పరాన్నజీవి. పట్టు = కోకూన్ దశ నుండి (Q7); ~1 km పొడవు ఒకే తంతువు. వానపాము = ద్విలింగ జీవి (Q31). హానికర కీటకం (Q26) = రైస్ వీవిల్. తేనెటీగల ప్రయోజనం = పంటల పరాగసంపర్కం (Q32) — 75% ఆహార పంటలు తేనెటీగలపై ఆధారపడతాయి.
English — Symbiosis & Antibodies Mycorrhiza (Q24) is the symbiotic association between fungi and roots of higher plants. The fungus extends hyphae deep into soil to absorb water and minerals (especially phosphorus); the plant supplies sugars from photosynthesis. ~90% of all land plants form mycorrhizal associations — without them, terrestrial life as we know it would collapse. Colostrum (Q12) is the yellowish thick "first milk" produced by mammals (cow/human) in the 2-3 days after birth. It is rich in antibodies (IgA, IgG), lactoferrin and white blood cells — providing passive immunity to the newborn before its own immune system matures. WHO recommends colostrum-feeding within the first hour of birth. Calves that miss colostrum suffer high mortality from infections.
తెలుగు — సహజీవనం + ప్రతిరక్షకాలు మైకోరైజా (Q24) = శిలీంధ్రం + ఉన్నత మొక్కల వేర్లు. ~90% భూమిపై మొక్కలు మైకోరైజాతో ఉంటాయి (P శోషణ). పాలముదురు (కొలొస్ట్రమ్) (Q12) = ప్రసవం తర్వాత 2-3 రోజులు; ప్రతిరక్షకాలు (IgA, IgG) సమృద్ధిగా; శిశువుకు నిష్క్రియ రోగనిరోధకత. WHO = మొదటి గంటలోపే ఇవ్వాలి.

Earthworm hermaphrodite explained (Q31): Each earthworm has both testes (male) and ovaries (female) in different segments. Two worms align belly-to-belly in opposite directions, exchange sperm via the clitellum (the swollen "saddle"), then separate. Each later forms a cocoon with eggs + stored sperm and lays it in soil. This ensures pairing happens easily despite slow movement. Aquatic Layers, Lower Animals & Records జల జీవులు · నిమ్న జీవులు
English — Aquatic Zonation & Curiosities Aquatic life occupies four distinct zones. Plankton drift in the water column (Set 14 Q4); Nekton = active swimmers (fish, whales); Benthos (Q18) = bottom dwellers (crabs, sponges, sea cucumbers); Neuston = surface-film life (water striders). The peripatus (Q13), known as a "living fossil", is the connecting link between Annelida and Arthropoda — it has stayed structurally similar for ~500 million years. Other living fossils: horseshoe crab (Limulus), coelacanth (Latimeria), tuatara, and ginkgo. Hydra (Q17) is a small Cnidarian that has NO blood but still respires directly through its body wall (gas exchange by diffusion across the thin two-layer body). Bats (Q20) have the highest upper limit of audible range — up to ~200 kHz (humans only 20 kHz). The sponge (Q30) is an animal, not a plant — phylum Porifera, sessile filter feeder. The incorrectly matched pair (Q19) is "Insecta–Prawn" — prawn is actually a crustacean, not an insect.

GK — History, Geography & Physics సాధారణ జ్ఞానం
English — History GK Three British/Bengal-renaissance facts appear in Set 15. Lord Cornwallis (Q34, Governor-General 1786-93) introduced the Police Administration in India through reforms of 1791-93 — establishing district-level police, separating revenue and judicial functions, codifying civil services. He is also called the "Father of Indian Civil Service" and architect of the Permanent Settlement (1793) for Bengal land revenue. The Brahmo Samaj (Q35) was founded by Raja Ram Mohan Roy in 1828 at Calcutta — a monotheistic reform movement opposing sati, child marriage, idolatry; led to the abolition of sati in 1829 by Bentinck. "Nil Darpan" (Q39) is a Bengali drama by Dinabandhu Mitra (1858-59) that exposed the cruelty of British indigo planters and fuelled the Indigo Revolt; James Long was tried for translating it.

English — Geography GK India observes Indian Standard Time (IST) based on the meridian 82.5°E passing through Mirzapur (UP). ⚠ Solar fusion vs fission
Q36: Sun's energy = Nuclear FUSION, NOT fission. In the proton-proton chain, four ¹H nuclei combine to form one ⁴He nucleus, releasing 26.7 MeV of energy. Mass defect = 0.7% of original mass, converted via E = mc² (Einstein, Q49). Fission (heavy nucleus splits) powers nuclear reactors and atomic bombs — but NOT the Sun. Sun's core temperature ~15 million K; fusion will continue for ~5 billion more years. సూర్య శక్తి = సంలీన చర్య (4¹H → ⁴He), విచ్ఛిత్తి కాదు. E = mc² ద్వారా 0.7% ద్రవ్యరాశి శక్తిగా మారుతుంది. సూర్య కేంద్ర ఉష్ణోగ్రత ~1.5 కోట్లు K
