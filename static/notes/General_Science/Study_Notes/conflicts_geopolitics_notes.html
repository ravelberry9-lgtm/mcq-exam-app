Section 1: Russia-Ukraine War 2025

Background

  • Russia invaded Ukraine on February 24, 2022 — called "Special Military Operation" by Russia
  • Triggered the largest land war in Europe since WWII
  • Ukraine backed by NATO/Western weapons, financial aid; Russia under heavy sanctions

Key 2024-2025 Developments

Peace Talks (2025): US President Donald Trump (re-elected Nov 2024) pushed for ceasefire; held separate calls with Putin & Zelensky in Feb 2025. Black Sea shipping deal discussed. 2025లో ట్రంప్ రష్యా-ఉక్రెయిన్ యుద్ధంలో శాంతి చర్చలు నిర్వహించారు.
Kursk Incursion (Aug 2024): Ukraine launched surprise cross-border offensive into Russia's Kursk Oblast — first time since WWII foreign troops entered Russian soil. ఉక్రెయిన్ రష్యా భూభాగమైన కుర్స్క్‌లోకి దూసుకుపోయింది — ద్వితీయ ప్రపంచ యుద్ధం తర్వాత మొదటిసారి.
  • Ukraine struck Russian territory using Western-supplied long-range missiles (ATACMS, Storm Shadow)
  • North Korea sent ~10,000 troops & weapons to support Russia (confirmed 2024)
  • Russia used Iranian-made Shahed drones against Ukrainian cities
  • Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Plant (Europe's largest) under Russian control — safety concerns

Section 2: Israel-Gaza War 2025

Hamas Attack & War Start

  • Hamas launched surprise attack on Israel on October 7, 2023 (Kibbutzim, Nova music festival)
  • ~1,200 Israelis killed; ~250 taken hostage — deadliest day for Jews since Holocaust
  • Israel declared war; launched massive military campaign in Gaza

Key 2025 Developments

Ceasefire Phase 1 (Jan 19, 2025): Qatar & Egypt brokered 6-week ceasefire between Israel & Hamas. 33 hostages released in exchange for ~1,900 Palestinian prisoners. జనవరి 2025లో కతర్ & ఈజిప్ట్ మధ్యవర్తిత్వంతో యుద్ధవిరమణ కుదిరింది.
War Resumed (March 2025): Israel resumed military operations in Gaza after ceasefire collapsed over Phase 2 negotiations. Gaza humanitarian crisis deepened.
ICJ Ruling (2024-25): International Court of Justice ordered Israel to prevent genocide, allow humanitarian aid. South Africa filed the genocide case against Israel. అంతర్జాతీయ న్యాయస్థానం ఇజ్రాయెల్‌కు మానవతా సహాయాన్ని అనుమతించమని ఆదేశించింది.

Israel-Lebanon (Hezbollah)

Ceasefire Nov 27, 2024: US-brokered 60-day ceasefire between Israel & Hezbollah in Lebanon after months of cross-border strikes. Israel killed Hezbollah chief Hassan Nasrallah (Sept 2024). నవంబర్ 2024లో ఇజ్రాయెల్-హెజ్బొల్లా యుద్ధవిరమణ కుదిరింది.

Section 3: Iran — Nuclear & Regional Tensions 2025

Iran-Israel Direct Strikes (2024)

April 13-14, 2024: Iran launched first-ever direct attack on Israel — 300+ drones & missiles (mostly intercepted). Israel struck back on April 19, 2024 (strikes inside Iran). ఏప్రిల్ 2024లో ఇరాన్ మొదటిసారి నేరుగా ఇజ్రాయెల్‌పై దాడి చేసింది.
Oct 1, 2024: Iran fired ~200 ballistic missiles at Israel (in retaliation for killing of Nasrallah & Haniyeh). Israel threatened major retaliation.

Iran Nuclear Programme

  • Iran enriching uranium to 60% purity (weapons-grade = 90%) — JCPOA abandoned by US (2018, Trump)
  • IAEA reports Iran has enough enriched uranium for multiple nuclear bombs if weaponised
  • 2025: Trump administration imposed maximum pressure sanctions; new nuclear deal talks stalled
  • Iranian President: Masoud Pezeshkian (elected July 2024 after Raisi died in helicopter crash May 2024)

Section 4: Syria — Fall of Assad HOT 2025

Assad Regime Fell — December 8, 2024: Rebel group HTS (Hayat Tahrir al-Sham) led by Ahmad al-Sharaa (Abu Mohammad al-Jolani) swept through Syria and captured Damascus. President Bashar al-Assad fled to Russia. Ended 54-year Assad family rule (Hafez + Bashar). డిసెంబర్ 2024లో సిరియా అధ్యక్షుడు అసద్ రష్యాకు పారిపోయాడు. 54 సంవత్సరాల అసద్ పాలన ముగిసింది.

Section 5: Operation Sindoor — India vs Pakistan VERY HOT 2026

Pahalgam Terror Attack — April 22, 2025: 26 tourists (mostly Hindu) killed by Pakistan-linked terrorists in Baisaran meadow, Pahalgam, Jammu & Kashmir. Pakistan-based Lashkar-e-Taiba linked group "The Resistance Front" claimed responsibility. ఏప్రిల్ 22, 2025న జమ్మూ కాశ్మీర్‌లోని పహల్‌గామ్‌లో 26 మంది పర్యాటకులు చంపబడ్డారు.
Operation Sindoor — May 7, 2025: India launched precision missile strikes on 9 terrorist camps across Pakistan and Pakistan-Occupied Kashmir (PoK) — targeting Lashkar-e-Taiba, Jaish-e-Mohammed, & Hizbul Mujahideen infrastructure. Named "Operation Sindoor" — symbolic reference to the sindoor (vermilion) of widows of Pahalgam victims. India-Pakistan ceasefire agreed May 10, 2025 (DGMO hotline). 4-day conflict. మే 7, 2025న భారత్ పాకిస్తాన్‌లోని 9 టెర్రరిస్ట్ స్థావరాలపై "ఆపరేషన్ సింధూర్" ప్రారంభించింది. మే 10న కాల్పుల విరమణ.

Weapons Used in Operation Sindoor

  • SCALP Missile (Storm Shadow) — Air-launched cruise missile, range 250+ km, deep precision strikes
  • HAMMER Bombs (Highly Agile Modular Munition Extended Range) — Range 50–70 km; hits bunkers & hardened targets
  • Loitering Munitions (Kamikaze Drones) — Hover, spot, and strike; surveillance + attack capability

Section 6: South China Sea & Taiwan Strait

South China Sea

  • China claims ~90% of South China Sea via "Nine-Dash Line" — rejected by PCA (Permanent Court of Arbitration) 2016
  • Dispute with Philippines, Vietnam, Malaysia, Brunei, Taiwan
  • Second Thomas Shoal (Ayungin Shoal): Philippines resupply missions to BRP Sierra Madre (grounded warship) repeatedly blocked by Chinese Coast Guard water cannons (2023–2025)
  • US-Philippines Mutual Defense Treaty invoked as deterrence
  • AUKUS (Australia-UK-US) submarines — partly targeting China's South China Sea ambitions
Philippine-China Standoffs (2024-25): Multiple incidents at Scarborough Shoal & Second Thomas Shoal — China Coast Guard seized Philippine supplies, fired water cannons at Filipino sailors.

Taiwan

  • Taiwan President: Lai Ching-te (elected Jan 2024) — seen as pro-independence
  • China conducted large military drills ("Joint Sword") around Taiwan after Lai's inauguration May 2024
  • US supplies weapons to Taiwan under Taiwan Relations Act (1979)
  • Taiwan Strait is ~180 km wide — strategic shipping lane

  • North Korea (DPRK) conducted multiple ICBM tests 2023–2025; claimed new "Hwasong-18" solid-fuel ICBM
  • Russia-DPRK Treaty (June 2024): Putin visited Pyongyang — signed "Comprehensive Strategic Partnership" mutual defence treaty; DPRK sent artillery & troops to Russia (Ukraine war)
  • Kim Jong-un declared South Korea "principal enemy" in Jan 2024 — scrapped reunification policy
  • North Korea sent ~10,000 troops to Russia (confirmed by US/NATO/Ukraine, Oct 2024)
  • South Korea President: Yoon Suk-yeol — declared martial law Dec 3, 2024 (22:27); National Assembly lifted within 6 hours; impeached Dec 14; arrested Jan 15, 2025; sentenced to life imprisonment Feb 19, 2026 (insurrection)

Section 8: Yemen & Houthi Red Sea Crisis

Houthi attacks on Red Sea shipping (Nov 2023 onwards): Iran-backed Houthis launched missiles & drones at commercial ships in the Red Sea, claiming solidarity with Gaza. Disrupted global trade — ships rerouted around Africa (Cape of Good Hope), raising shipping costs. హూతీలు ఎర్ర సముద్రంలో నౌకలపై దాడులు చేయడంతో ప్రపంచ వ్యాపారం దెబ్బతింది.
  • US-UK launched "Operation Prosperity Guardian" and later "Operation Poseidon Archer" — strikes on Houthi sites in Yemen
  • Houthis control capital Sana'a & much of northwest Yemen; internationally-recognised govt in Aden
  • Yemen civil war: Houthis (backed by Iran) vs Saudi-led coalition (since 2015)
  • UN-brokered Saudi-Yemen ceasefire discussions ongoing 2025
  • Gaza ceasefire Jan 2025 → Houthis announced pause in Red Sea attacks

Section 9: Africa — Conflicts & Coups

Sudan Civil War

Sudan War (April 2023–present): Sudan Armed Forces (SAF) vs Rapid Support Forces (RSF) — one of the world's worst humanitarian crises. 10+ million displaced. Famine declared in parts of Sudan (2024). సూడాన్‌లో SAF వర్సెస్ RSF యుద్ధం — లక్షలాది మంది నిరాశ్రయులయ్యారు.
  • SAF led by Gen. Abdel Fattah al-Burhan; RSF led by Gen. Mohamed Hamdan Dagalo (Hemedti)
  • Darfur region most affected — RSF accused of genocide

Sahel Coups — Alliance of Sahel States

  • Mali: coup 2021; expelled French troops; joined Russia's Wagner Group
  • Burkina Faso: coup Sept 2022; expelled French troops; Wagner Group arrived
  • Niger: coup July 2023; expelled French & US troops; banned uranium exports to France
  • Mali + Burkina Faso + Niger formed Alliance of Sahel States (AES) — withdrew from ECOWAS (Jan 2025)
  • All three countries have Russian Wagner Group (now "Africa Corps") presence

Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger withdrew from ECOWAS in Jan 2025 and formed Alliance of Sahel States (AES). ECOWAS threatened sanctions but didn't use military force. Wagner Group rebranded as "Africa Corps" after Prigozhin's death (Aug 2023).

Other Africa Events

  • Ethiopia: Tigray conflict ended with Pretoria Agreement (Nov 2022); Tigray People's Liberation Front (TPLF) peace deal
  • DRC (Congo): M23 rebel group (backed by Rwanda) captured Goma (Jan 2025) — eastern DRC conflict escalating
  • Mozambique: Post-election violence (Oct 2024–2025) after disputed results

Section 10: NATO, AUKUS & Geopolitical Alliances

NATO Expansion

Finland (31st member): Joined NATO April 4, 2023 — added 1,340 km border with Russia. Sweden (32nd member): Joined NATO March 7, 2024 — ending 200+ years of neutrality. ఫిన్‌లాండ్ (2023), స్వీడన్ (2024) NATO లో చేరాయి.
  • NATO now has 32 members (after Sweden joined 2024)
  • NATO Vilnius Summit 2023: Ukraine invited (no timeline); GUAM (non-article 5 partner for Ukraine)
  • NATO Washington Summit July 2024: 75th anniversary; pledged continued Ukraine support
  • NATO The Hague Summit June 2025: agreed to increase defence spending to 5% of GDP (from 2% target)
  • NATO Secretary-General: Mark Rutte (Netherlands) — took over from Stoltenberg in Oct 2024
